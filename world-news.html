Sunday, July 12, 2026 · Asia

Israel

Asia · population ~9.8M

Politics & Government

Israel is barreling toward early elections after the Knesset is expected to dissolve on July 17, setting up a national vote on October 27 — the latest date permitted by law. Prime Minister Netanyahu, who shored up his coalition with the ultra-Orthodox parties, is racing to pass legislation before dissolution, including a politically appointed probe into the failures surrounding the October 7 massacre, which cleared its first Knesset reading 59-0 after an opposition boycott. New electoral alignments are also taking shape, including a fresh Arab-Jewish party and an alliance between Hili Tropper and Yoaz Hendel's Reservists Party.

Economy & Business

The IMF has cut its 2026 growth forecast for Israel to 3.5%, down from a pre-war projection of 4.8%, citing elevated defense spending, regional tensions, and a labor supply squeezed by military mobilization. Despite the drag, the country's tech sector keeps humming: Israel has held onto its #3 global ranking in startup ecosystem value, now worth $335.1 billion with 26 active unicorns, led by cybersecurity, biotech, and AI infrastructure. On the ground, shoppers are seeing a mixed picture, with steep discounts at some retailers offset by price hikes of up to 14% in parallel categories.

Military & Security

Nine months into a ceasefire, Israel now controls nearly 70% of Gaza, with troops continuing to push into new "orange zone" territory. Satellite analysis has identified roughly 40 Israeli military outposts inside Gaza, eight built entirely from scratch since the truce took hold. Aid groups have suspended operations in parts of northern Gaza, and Gaza's health ministry reports nearly 930 Palestinians killed and over 2,800 injured in the seven months since the ceasefire began, underscoring how fragile the truce remains.

Society & Culture

Israel marked 1,000 days since the October 7 massacre with large memorial gatherings in Tel Aviv's Hostages Square, where thousands demanded a state commission of inquiry; related marches blocked the Ayalon Highway and led to clashes and arrests outside the Knesset. On a lighter note, the Maccabiah Games kicked off in Jerusalem, drawing a record 43 countries of Jewish athletes to what's billed as the "Jewish Olympics," with tributes to hostages and fallen soldiers woven into the opening ceremony.

Science & Technology

Israel's government has approved a sweeping national AI program aimed at making the country a leading AI power, with planned investment in computing infrastructure, quantum research, and public services. In healthcare, the Health Ministry and Innovation Authority launched a regulatory sandbox for AI medical tools, with pilots including an at-home ultrasound platform for autonomous pregnancy-scan analysis. The moves come as Israel's startup ecosystem holds its #3 global ranking for a sixth straight year, anchored by strength in cybersecurity and deep tech.

🔥 Trending
Temper City — "Self Aware"
Music · Israeli trio (L.A.-based)

A scrappy rock trio of Israeli writer-producers — Eytan Peled, Chen Kordova, and Aviv Barenholtz — has become an unlikely breakout story: their song "Self Aware" made Temper City the first Israeli band to crack the Billboard Hot 100. The fraught-relationship anthem took off almost entirely through organic TikTok spread rather than major-label push, snowballing from niche listens into a genuine chart hit by April. Its momentum has turned the group into a talking point back home as a homegrown act finding global traction through grassroots internet virality rather than industry machinery.

Saturday, July 11, 2026 · Europe

Finland

Europe · population ~5.6M

Politics & Government

Prime Minister Petteri Orpo's four-party coalition continued a busy week of diplomacy, with Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi visiting Helsinki on July 5 to meet Finnish counterpart Elina Valtonen, followed by Finnish officials traveling to the NATO Summit Defence Industry Forum in Ankara on July 7–8. Domestically, the government is under pressure over an economy sending mixed signals — strong first-quarter GDP growth alongside an 11-year-high unemployment rate. The cabinet has also extended the indefinite closure of all land borders with Russia, first imposed amid migration-instrumentalization concerns, with no timeline yet for reopening.

Economy & Business

Finland's economy is at what the Bank of Finland calls a "turning point," with forecasts of 0.7% to as much as 1.5% growth this year after two years of stagnation, even as elevated Middle East-driven energy prices keep inflation near 2.4%. The recovery is being undercut by unemployment that hit an 11-year high of 11.6% in April, with youth unemployment at 28%, while a VAT increase to 25.5% has squeezed household budgets and public debt is climbing toward 91% of GDP. On the brighter side, investment is set to rise on the back of F-35 fighter jet deliveries and a data-centre construction boom, and Helsinki's startup ecosystem raised €1.2 billion in 2024 and remains a top-tier European hub.

Military & Security

Finland used the Ankara NATO summit to deepen its defence posture, with Defence Minister Antti Häkkänen signing 14 contracts worth hundreds of millions of euros covering space, Arctic and maritime defence technology, while President Alexander Stubb announced France will join NATO's Forward Land Forces battlegroup in Finland. Helsinki also joined new NATO air-refuelling, surveillance and drone-training programmes designed to reduce European reliance on the United States. Finnish defence- and security-related spending is set to reach 2.4% of GDP in 2026, well above NATO's 1.5% target, and on July 6 Finland joined the UK, Netherlands and Poland in a joint statement on a new multilateral defence-financing mechanism.

Society & Culture

A restructuring of Finland's arts funding took effect July 1, as the country's regional arts councils were discontinued in favor of a new peer-review structure under the Council of Arts and Culture, even as the National Council for the Visual Arts handed out €950,000 in public-display grants to 95 artists. The northern city of Oulu is serving as European Capital of Culture in 2026, staging Sámi-inspired exhibitions and electronic-music festivals throughout the year, while Turku's new Music Hall Fuuga is set to open this autumn and the Kvarken Archipelago marks 20 years of UNESCO World Heritage status.

Science & Technology

Finnish quantum computing hit a milestone as IQM Quantum Computers began trading on the Nasdaq Global Select Market on July 2 under ticker IQMX, becoming the first European quantum firm to list on a major U.S. exchange after selling 23 quantum computers worldwide. Finland has also joined VINES, a €100 million international initiative coordinated by the U.S. National Science Foundation to advance next-generation network technologies, building on a startup scene already strong in healthtech, gaming, deeptech and industrial software centered on Helsinki and Espoo.

🔥 Trending
Hopeasiivet
Music / Indiepop · Helsinki

Helsinki-based band Hopeasiivet has emerged as a leading voice in Finland's new wave of indiepop, built out of Helsinki's cross-disciplinary scene of young musicians, artists and designers. Their single "Olet kaunis ja minä vain" spread organically through fan shares rather than any major-label push, and the band rode that momentum into a January 2026 debut album, Vaikka se loppuni ois, which marks a shift from bedroom-pop roots toward a more cinematic, ensemble-driven sound with sharp Finnish lyricism. The grassroots buzz has carried them into the lineup at Helsinki Music Week alongside fellow emerging acts, cementing the city's reputation as an incubator for homegrown indie talent.

Friday, July 10, 2026 · South America

Brazil

South America · population ~213M

Politics & Government

Brazil's October election is shaping up as a coin flip between President Lula da Silva, seeking an unprecedented fourth term at 80, and Senator Flávio Bolsonaro, who was endorsed by his father Jair to carry the family's far-right movement into the first round on October 4. Flávio's polling advantage has slipped since a scandal over a $12 million payment tied to a film financing former banker, even as parties prepare to formally register candidates between July 20 and August 15. Domestically, Lula's proposal to end the six-day workweek remains stalled in the Senate, where roughly seven in ten Brazilians back the shorter week despite a leadership standoff blocking committee action. Meanwhile, a Brazil–US trade hearing got underway this week, adding fresh uncertainty to the electoral backdrop.

Economy & Business

Brazil's central bank has trimmed the benchmark Selic rate to 14.25%, a third straight quarter-point cut as inflation gauges continue to cool, though annual inflation near 4.7% still runs above the 3% target. Markets wobbled this week — the Ibovespa slid as Vale fell on softer iron ore prices while Petrobras gained on an oil-price spike tied to tensions near the Strait of Hormuz, and foreign capital outflows added pressure amid the new US trade hearing and election jitters. Beneath the volatility, Brazil's startup ecosystem keeps expanding, with fintech, agritech and AI ventures such as Nubank, CloudWalk and Olist drawing continued investor interest.

Military & Security

Brazil's Foreign Ministry has warned Congress of a real risk of US military action after Washington designated the criminal groups PCC and Comando Vermelho as foreign terrorist organizations, a move Brasília fears could be used to justify extraterritorial action against its financial and immigration institutions. Officials note that neighboring Argentina and Paraguay have already aligned with Washington's framing, while Brazil insists on treating the gangs as a domestic policing matter. Separately, the federal government has extended its National Public Security Force deployment in Rio de Janeiro through late September to help contain organized-crime violence in the state.

Society & Culture

Brazil's cultural world is mourning the death of telenovela writer Benedito Ruy Barbosa at 95, the storyteller behind national classics like Pantanal, O Rei do Gado and Terra Nostra. On a more disruptive note, widespread flight delays and cancellations hit São Paulo and Rio airports this week, with roughly 280 delays and 60 cancellations affecting LATAM, GOL, Azul and international carriers alike, disrupting travel for thousands.

Science & Technology

The World Bank has thrown its weight behind Brazil's clean-transport push, backing a $500 million electromobility program with Caixa Econômica Federal that will fund roughly 540 electric buses, depot upgrades and charging infrastructure as cities modernize aging diesel fleets. On the research side, CNPq has opened its 2026 Chamada Universal grant call, offering R$300 million in funding for university research through August 3, a welcome infusion after years of unstable funding cycles for the country's scientists. Brazil's tech sector also continues to lean into fintech and agritech innovation, per the same startup landscape reshaping the broader economy.

🔥 Trending
Franco, The Sir!
Music (Trap) · Icoaraci, Belém, Pará

An independent 18-year-old trap artist from Icoaraci, a working-class district of Belém in Brazil's northern Pará state, Franco, The Sir! broke out after an intimate acoustic TikTok performance of his track "Fujifilm" went viral, racking up over 700,000 views. The song shot to number one on Spotify Brazil's viral chart, and despite accusations of being an "industry plant" after praise from established rap figures, he has kept producing, mixing and composing everything himself. He's carried that momentum into 2026, releasing a full album in March and a string of new singles, cementing his status as one of the country's most-watched grassroots breakout acts and putting the North region on trap's national map.

Thursday, July 9, 2026 · Asia

India

Asia · population ~1440M

Politics & Government

India's parliament is inching toward major constitutional change, with the BJP-led National Democratic Alliance edging closer to the two-thirds majority in both houses it needs to pass the Delimitation Bill, 2026, which would expand the Lok Sabha from 543 to 816 seats, alongside the long-pending women's reservation and one-nation-one-election bills. The push has come through a mix of electoral wins, resignations and shifting party loyalties rather than a single decisive vote. Meanwhile, infrastructure quality made headlines after the newly opened Delhi-Dehradun Expressway developed deep potholes near Shamli just weeks after inauguration, and fresh political tensions have flared in West Bengal alongside an ongoing debate over E20 petrol, per Outlook's latest issue.

Economy & Business

India remains the world's fastest-growing major economy, posting 7.8% first-quarter growth even as the Reserve Bank holds a cautious 6.6% full-year forecast, with FY2026 growth revised up to 7.6%. Trade momentum is building on two fronts: customs rules bringing the India-UK free-trade pact into force from July 15, and negotiators in Washington describing an interim India-US deal as "very, very close" ahead of a July 24 tariff deadline, according to a weekly business review. A record $170 billion energy-investment year and the Tata-ASML semiconductor tie-up anchoring an $11 billion chip fab are underpinning the growth story, while the Sensex has held above 77,500 on the best single day for IT stocks in 14 months.

Military & Security

India's Defence Acquisition Council has cleared roughly ₹52,000 crore in procurement proposals spanning air defence, anti-tank, electronic warfare and naval systems, including the indigenous Akash Tarang anti-drone system and a new medium-range surface-to-air missile network, with a particular focus on next-gen drone warfare. The Navy is set to commission its sixth Project 17A stealth frigate, INS Mahendragiri, at Visakhapatnam on July 11, barely a month after INS Dunagiri entered service. New Army Chief General Dhiraj Seth, who took over on June 30, has outlined a modernisation roadmap dubbed "VIJAY" aimed at building a digitised, multi-domain force, as India's annual defence production hit a record ₹1.78 lakh crore for FY2025-26.

Society & Culture

A Gen Z protest movement dubbed the "Cockroach Party" has taken root in Delhi, born from the chief justice's remark equating youth with cockroaches and now channeling anger over repeated exam-paper leaks, a chronic jobs shortage and a sense that politicians aren't listening. The movement's Instagram following has already surpassed 22 million, more than double that of Modi's own party, drawing comparisons to Gen Z protest waves elsewhere in Asia this year. Youth unemployment remains stuck near 15% for the 15-29 age group, roughly triple the national rate, fueling the discontent.

Science & Technology

India's push into deep tech advanced with confirmation that IBM will commission one of the country's first physical quantum computers — a 156-qubit Quantum System Two — in Amaravati by September 2026, part of a tie-up between the Andhra Pradesh government, IBM and Tata Consultancy Services to build a regional "Quantum Valley." Separately, researchers at the Physical Research Laboratory in Ahmedabad reported strong new evidence of subsurface water ice in permanently shadowed craters near the Moon's south pole, using radar data from the Chandrayaan-2 orbiter — a finding with direct implications for future lunar missions. Union Minister Jitendra Singh also flagged that artificial intelligence could add nearly ₹70,000 crore to India's agricultural economy, underscoring the government's growing focus on AI-driven productivity gains.

🔥 Trending
Gini
Music / Indie-Pop · Dehradun, Uttarakhand

Gini, a self-taught singer-songwriter from the hill town of Dehradun, has become one of the most talked-about names in Indian indie-pop without any major-label campaign behind her — Spotify tapped her as a RADAR artist at just 19. Her cross-cultural collaboration with Goa-based rapper Tsumyoki, "Don't Even Text", blends Hindi and English into a deeply vulnerable, lyric-driven anthem that has become one of the year's most-played Gen Z tracks. The song's organic spread through playlists and social shares, rather than any industry push, is credited with landing her a spot among the young musicians redefining Indian pop in 2026 — a rare rise for an artist who built her following from a small regional scene rather than Mumbai's industry machine.

Wednesday, July 8, 2026 · North America

Mexico

North America · population ~130M

Politics & Government

President Claudia Sheinbaum continues to govern with the Morena-led "Sigamos Haciendo Historia" coalition holding commanding majorities in both chambers of Congress, a position analysts warn is enabling contentious constitutional reforms, including a planned electoral overhaul that critics say would weaken citizen-organized elections. Lawmakers in the lower house have already passed a 10.2 trillion-peso 2026 budget on party lines. Separately, a Los Angeles Times report cited by AS/COA's approval tracker notes the U.S. Department of Justice is investigating Sonora Governor Alfonso Durazo and Tamaulipas Governor Américo Villarreal over alleged drug trafficking and fuel contraband links, while Mexico's electoral institute has approved two new political parties, Construyendo Sociedades de Paz and Somos México.

Economy & Business

Growth forecasts have been revised upward, with the Bank of Mexico lifting its 2026 GDP outlook to 1.6% from 1.1% and the OECD projecting 1.4% growth, even as the IIF's earlier estimate of 0.9% would have marked a second straight sub-1% year. The formal six-year USMCA review began July 1, with the Trump administration signaling it wants a genuine renegotiation covering tighter rules of origin on Chinese content, higher auto-content thresholds, and energy-market access guarantees. Banxico has held its policy rate at 6.5% after a May cut, and the country's startup ecosystem remains a bright spot, with over 1,400 startups and nearly $9.9 billion raised, led by fintech.

Military & Security

Security forces remain on heightened alert following the February killing of Jalisco New Generation Cartel leader Nemesio "El Mencho" Oseguera Cervantes, an operation that triggered a wave of retaliatory blockades, arson, and clashes across roughly 20 states, according to the 2026 Jalisco operation record. Analysts at the GIS Reports security outlook describe cartels like CJNG and Sinaloa as increasingly adopting insurgent-style tactics, including drone strikes and IEDs, prompting large troop surges and, per CNN reporting, an escalation of covert CIA operations against cartel targets inside Mexico.

Society & Culture

Social tensions have collided with World Cup festivities in Mexico City, where the CNTE teachers' union staged large Zócalo protests demanding reinstated pensions and higher wages, putting the official FIFA fan fest's location at risk of relocation, with authorities bracing for roughly 19 separate social movements to demonstrate near Estadio Azteca and the Zócalo during the tournament's opening week, per PBS News. Anti-gentrification protests have also continued in neighborhoods like La Condesa and Roma over rising rents and Airbnb-driven housing pressure, contributing to Fodor's placing Mexico City on its 2026 "No List" for travelers.

Science & Technology

The State of Mexico announced a steep 32.3% cut to its science and innovation budget for 2026, a roughly MX$376 million reduction that ends two years of growth and pushes funding below 2023 levels, even as advocates push to close a persistent gender gap in STEM fields. On the private side, Mexico's tech workforce keeps expanding, having produced over 211,000 ICT graduates since 2020, while the startup sector continues drawing strong investment in AI, healthtech, and logistics applications.

🔥 Trending
"Esto es México" by Coray
Music · León, Guanajuato

A homegrown World Cup anthem has overtaken the tournament's official soundtrack in popularity: "Esto es México," released just before the 2026 FIFA World Cup by León-born DJ and artist Coray (Emmanuel Armando Centeno), stitched together popular Mexican slang and colloquial phrases over a catchy beat, and spread organically across social media rather than through any official campaign. Its momentum snowballed after President Claudia Sheinbaum called it her favorite song of the tournament, and it's now played at every Mexico match, per Billboard's roundup of unofficial World Cup hits. FIFA president Gianni Infantino and Mexican Football Federation president Mikel Arriola later invited Centeno to Estadio Ciudad de México to formally recognize the track's grassroots success.

Tuesday, July 7, 2026 · Europe

Spain

Europe · population ~48M

Politics & Government

Spain's government is navigating a fresh controversy over a citizenship law dispute that grants nationality to descendants of Spaniards exiled during the Civil War and Franco dictatorship, with critics warning that voting rights for the large diaspora in the Americas could sway national elections while supporters call it overdue historical redress. Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez has paired the debate with a new Integration and Citizenship Plan on migration, alongside measures protecting unaccompanied foreign minors and addressing the country's housing affordability crunch. Commentary from Al Jazeera argues the ruling socialists' governing formula is running out of runway as coalition tensions persist.

Economy & Business

Madrid raised its official 2026 growth forecast to 2.6% from 2.2%, arguing the economy remains well-positioned to absorb energy-price shocks tied to the Middle East conflict, with GDP growth now running three times faster than the wider euro area. The labour market keeps outperforming, with unemployment at its lowest level since 2008 even as inflation held at 3.2% in June for a third straight month. Beneath the headline figures, Spain's startup ecosystem keeps expanding, now counting more than 12,000 startups and 18 unicorns as software, travel tech and fintech attract the bulk of new funding.

Military & Security

Spain currently has more than 3,000 troops deployed across 17 international missions on four continents under UN, NATO and EU mandates, including command of the eastern sector of the UN's Lebanon mission and a NATO battlegroup rotation in the Baltics. The country is also rolling out an 11-year, €35 billion military modernisation programme covering tanks, artillery, naval systems and digital communications, alongside delivery of its first HALCÓN I Eurofighters to replace ageing F/A-18 Hornets. Separately, Foreign Minister José Manuel Albares' proposal for the EU to build its own standing armed forces has so far drawn little concrete support from other member states.

Society & Culture

More than 50 civil-society groups are mobilising behind a July 26 demonstration aiming to "collapse" Palma de Mallorca, the latest escalation in Spain's wave of anti-tourism protests driven by soaring housing costs and overcrowding in the Balearics, Barcelona and San Sebastián. Researchers have also flagged the rapid growth of Núcleo Nacional, a neo-Nazi network claiming roughly 2,000 members that registered a political party, Noviembre Nacional, earlier this year — a sign of hardening far-right activism online.

Science & Technology

Spain approved a €719 million investment in an AI "gigafactory" as it courts EU co-financing, a push underscored by OpenAI's decision to open a Madrid office to deepen local ties. On the green-energy side, the country's renewables buildout is already showing up in lower electricity bills, with marine protected areas expanded to 22.5% of Spanish waters en route to a 30% target. Spain is also coordinating the €400 million PIXEurope initiative to scale up European photonics manufacturing, part of a broader bid to carve out a niche in semiconductors and quantum computing.

🔥 Trending

Music · Catalonia (Barcelona region)

A Catalan indie band's homage to their own sound engineer has turned into one of Spain's unlikeliest summer hits: "Xavier, el tècnic de so" is a sincere, humorous tribute to the sound technician who mixes their live shows, and by extension to every unsung technician who makes live music possible. Despite running past five minutes — far from radio-friendly — it has spread organically through fans imitating Xavier's on-stage banter on social media, with its loose, playful rhythm fitting neatly into Spain's summer town-festival season. La Ludwig Band, part of the current Catalan indie scene blending pop, folk-rock and singer-songwriter influences, is now being tipped as a genuine contender for Spain's "song of the summer" — grassroots momentum built without a major-label push.
