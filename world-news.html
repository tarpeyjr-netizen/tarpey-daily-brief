Cuba

North America · population ~12M

Politics & Government

Washington intensified pressure on Havana this month, with the State Department designating ten additional entities on July 13 as part of the Trump administration's push to cut off funding to Cuba's security apparatus, alongside a new report alleging the regime fosters anti-American activism in the US. Cuba's foreign minister said he has observed "no progress" in US negotiations, even as House Democrats returning from a Cuba visit called on the White House to lift sanctions they blame for worsening blackouts. Under the strain, Cuba's Communist Party has approved an unprecedented package of free-market reforms opening new room for private enterprise and foreign investment, including from Cubans abroad.

Economy & Business

Cuba's economy continues to contract sharply, with forecasters now projecting output to fall as much as 7.2 percent in 2026 amid an energy collapse years in the making, driven by the loss of subsidized Venezuelan oil after the US-led capture of Nicolás Maduro. Blackouts of 20 to 25 hours a day are now common across much of the island. In response, the Communist Party's unprecedented economic opening would let state enterprises convert into private, share-holding businesses — one of the most significant departures from central planning since the revolution.

Military & Security

Tensions with Washington have sharpened, with President Trump and Secretary of State Marco Rubio raising the prospect of military action and Pentagon planners reportedly weighing options including an air-assault operation by the 101st Airborne Division. Cuba's 49,000-strong armed forces remain on heightened alert following January's US raid in Venezuela, even as senior American and Cuban officers held a rare, "positive" meeting near Guantánamo Bay to keep communication channels open.

Society & Culture

Cuba is experiencing its largest wave of street protests in years, with a record 107 demonstrations recorded in June alone, concentrated in Havana and Santiago de Cuba and fueled by punishing blackouts and food shortages. Fresh unrest broke out again in July as residents banged pots and built barricades over renewed outages, and rights monitors say the government carried out nearly 2,000 repressive actions against citizens in the first half of the year, with more than 1,300 political prisoners currently held.

Tourism

Cuba's tourism sector is being squeezed from multiple directions: the US Treasury added the Ministry of Tourism itself to its sanctions list on July 13, Spain's Meliá has begun terminating contracts on fifteen hotels amid the fuel crisis, and the government has been forced to shut hotels and relocate visitors as shortages disrupt transport. In response, Havana has unveiled a sweeping package of market-oriented tourism reforms and is courting Mexico on a joint "Maya World" circuit linking Cancún, Chichén Itzá and Havana to diversify its visitor base.

Site to visit: Old Havana and its colonial fortification system have been a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1982, prized for Baroque and Neoclassical architecture along squares like the Plaza Vieja and Plaza de la Catedral. Visitors can wander the pedestrian Calle Mercaderes, tour the star-shaped Castillo de la Real Fuerza, and finish with a daiquiri at El Floridita — best done early morning or late afternoon to avoid both the heat and the crowds.

🔥 Trending
Chezca Zana & "Hyper-Reparto"
Music · Havana

On Cuban social media, an underground offshoot of reparto called "hyper-reparto" is having a moment, led by the artist Chezca Zana, who fuses reparto's jagged street percussion with punk-rock distortion and pitch-bent vocals. Reparto itself — nicknamed "the reggaeton of the poor" — has become the dominant sound of Cuba's youth since its mid-2000s roots, but Zana's rockier, more abrasive spin has split listeners into fierce camps online, with a viral debate over whether "el repa is the new punk" spreading nearly as fast as the songs themselves on TikTok and YouTube.

Uzbekistan

Asia · population ~36M

Politics & Government

President Shavkat Mirziyoyev has kept an unusually dense diplomatic schedule this month, holding the first-ever Uzbek-Albanian summit and making a July 14 visit to Minsk billed as a step toward a strategic partnership with Belarus. The same week, Uzbek and Turkmen foreign ministries held political consultations in Ashgabat, and a delegation joined the EU-Central Asia High-Level Dialogue on Politics and Security in Brussels. Domestically, Tashkent named a new head of its State Security Service and signed a presidential decree advancing the country's Digital Government program.

Economy & Business

Uzbekistan's economy grew 8.5 percent in the first half of 2026, powered by a 16.9 percent surge in services, 8 percent industrial growth and construction up nearly 14 percent, prompting the government to raise its full-year growth forecast to 8.1 percent. Total investment reached $28 billion and foreign trade turnover hit $14.4 billion in H1. The World Bank's newly published Country Private Sector Diagnostic cautions, however, that the state's continued dominance of the economy remains the central obstacle to further private-sector growth.

Military & Security

Tashkent has been deepening defense ties on multiple fronts: Russia and Uzbekistan adopted a military-strategic partnership program running through 2030, while US and Uzbek officers mapped out a 2026–2027 cooperation plan and explored expanded participation in Washington's IMET training program. The moves follow a February live-fire air-defense exercise showcasing Chinese and Russian missile systems, part of a push that has seen Uzbekistan ranked as Central Asia's strongest military in the 2026 Global Firepower index.

Society & Culture

Uzbekistan hosted the first International Forum of Islamic Civilization on July 7, where President Mirziyoyev called for greater investment in education and religious tolerance amid rising global extremism. Days later, a Days of Culture of Uzbekistan event with Belarus opened discussion on joint virtual-reality art cooperation, and Mirziyoyev reviewed an ambitious international cultural program for 2026–27. The cultural calendar came against the backdrop of an unprecedented mid-July heatwave, with temperatures hitting 46–49°C in southern desert regions.

Tourism

Uzbekistan's tourism boom shows no sign of slowing: the country welcomed roughly 6.5 million international visitors in the first half of 2026, up nearly 25 percent year-on-year, putting it on pace toward a stated goal of 12 million visitors this year. Growth is being driven by regional arrivals from Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan and Kazakhstan alongside a sharp rise in Chinese tourists, while Karakalpakstan is being developed as a new cultural and ecological hub alongside Silk Road staples Samarkand, Bukhara and Khiva. The country has also simplified visas and expanded its lodging base to nearly 6,900 accommodations offering more than 185,000 beds.

Site to visit: The Registan in Samarkand — three monumental 15th-to-17th-century madrasahs framing a single stone courtyard — anchors Samarkand's UNESCO-listed old city and is widely considered Central Asia's most iconic public square. Entry runs around 100,000 som (roughly $8) for foreign visitors, and the site is open daily from 8 a.m. to 11 p.m., with early morning or late-afternoon visits recommended to avoid both midday heat and tour-group crowds.

🔥 Trending
NavoTim — "Chaki Chaki Boron"
Music · nationwide (TikTok-driven)

An ethno-pop track called "Chaki Chaki Boron" ("Drip Drip Rain") by the group NavoTim has become one of the biggest homegrown viral moments on Uzbek TikTok this year, blending traditional Uzbek melodic phrasing with dance-pop production in a way that's proven endlessly loopable for short-form video. The song has spread organically through Uzbek-music discovery tags rather than any formal marketing push, part of a broader wave of Uzbek-language tracks — from folk-inflected pop to novelty songs — breaking out purely on algorithmic word-of-mouth rather than celebrity or state promotion.

Tuesday, July 21, 2026 · Asia & Europe

Saudi Arabia

Asia · population ~37M

Politics & Government

King Salman signed off on a cabinet reshuffle this month as regional tensions dominated the diplomatic calendar. Riyadh's foreign minister joined Arab counterparts in condemning recent Iranian strikes, while the kingdom firmly rejected Houthi claims of a maritime blockade after its defense ministry said it intercepted ballistic missiles aimed at Saudi territory. Washington reaffirmed the relationship this week when Secretary of State Marco Rubio met with the kingdom's ambassador, underscoring continued US-Saudi coordination amid the wider regional security picture tracked by Al Jazeera.

Economy & Business

Saudi Arabia's non-oil sectors are driving a stronger growth outlook, with the IMF raising its 2027 growth forecast to 5.5% even as 2026 estimates were trimmed on regional trade disruptions. A Saudi-Canada Investment Forum in Jeddah produced 15 new agreements spanning finance, mining and AI, alongside a headline $2 billion PIF infrastructure deal with I Squared Capital. Riyadh's startup scene continues to expand too, with the kingdom now home to more than 2,500 startups and four unicorns.

Military & Security

Saudi Arabia remains the world's fifth-largest defense spender, and its armed forces have been actively engaged this month intercepting Houthi ballistic missiles targeting the kingdom. Under Vision 2030, Riyadh is pushing to localize half of its military procurement by 2030, and has reportedly turned to Ukraine for counter-drone technology to blunt Iranian-linked attacks. New industrial partnerships, including a helicopter production MoU with Turkish firm TUSAŞ, point to a deepening domestic defense-manufacturing push.

Society & Culture

Saudi daily life continues to shift under Vision 2030, with expanded employment options for women, a wider range of accepted careers and the mainstreaming of public entertainment reshaping how the kingdom operates day to day, even as private and family life remain anchored in tradition. Cultural institutions built around the Ministry of Culture continue to balance heritage preservation with a modern entertainment scene, visible in events ranging from the traditional Jinādiriyyah Heritage Festival to the modern MDLBeast music festival in Riyadh.

Tourism

Saudi Arabia is on pace for a record tourism year, having logged nearly 37.2 million visitors and SAR 82.7 billion in spending in Q1 2026 alone. The Saudi Tourism Authority just launched a unified digital booking platform for its "Saudi Summer 2026" campaign, while the southern Asir region opened 44 new seasonal investment sites aimed at drawing over three million heat-escaping domestic travelers this summer. The kingdom is targeting 150 million annual visitors by 2030 as part of its broader economic diversification push.

Site to visit: Hegra, Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site in AlUla, is a 2,000-year-old Nabataean city of monumental rock-cut tombs rivaling Jordan's Petra. Entry is by timed, guided tour only — tours start around SAR 95 per person and run roughly two hours, with cooler months (October to March) the more comfortable time to explore the desert site.

🔥 Trending
Creator-driven F&B launches
Food & Consumer Trend · Riyadh

A wave of Saudi food and beverage brands are being built directly out of social-media fandom, with local content creators using their TikTok followings to launch products that sell out on demand. The Namq Matcha Tala launch is the current flashpoint example, turning a creator's personal taste and loyal online community into a physical product line almost overnight. It reflects a broader shift in Saudi youth culture, where TikTok fame is increasingly a direct pipeline to homegrown retail success rather than just entertainment.

Russia

Europe · population ~144M (transcontinental; European portion listed here)

Politics & Government

Russia is gearing up for nationwide elections in September, when voters will pick a new State Duma and regional officials in a vote the ruling United Russia party is expected to dominate, with regions reportedly instructed to secure high turnout. A government meeting in mid-July addressed writing off regional budget-loan debt and shoring up housing and utilities funding, while Prime Minister Mikhail Mishustin joined leaders from Armenia, Belarus, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan at the INNOPROM industrial exhibition. Russia is also among the founding members of the new World AI Cooperation Organisation.

Economy & Business

Russia's economy is showing signs of strain, with a fuel crisis stoking stagflation fears as refining capacity losses push up business costs. Manufacturing activity contracted for a second straight month in July, with the S&P Global PMI slipping to 47.0, and the state rail giant is pressing businesses to absorb more war-economy costs. The first-quarter budget deficit already exceeded the government's full-year target as oil and gas revenues decline under sanctions pressure.

Military & Security

The war in Ukraine continues to escalate on multiple fronts: Kyiv is ramping up drone production and released footage of strikes on 20 Russian vessels in the Black Sea, including 17 oil tankers, and Ukrainian drones reportedly struck an FSB coordination center in occupied Kherson Oblast. Ukraine is increasingly targeting Russia's logistics network to disrupt military supply lines despite Western sanctions, and ten countries met in Paris to advance an Integrated Anti-Ballistic Missile Coalition aimed at bolstering allied air defense.

Society & Culture

Domestic pressure is building as rolling mobile internet shutdowns in Moscow and St. Petersburg have touched a nerve with ordinary citizens, with police detaining at least 14 people at a peaceful protest against the restrictions. Authorities have further escalated a crackdown on civil society, expanding censorship and surveillance of critics both at home and in exile as the war enters its fourth year. Culture remains a flashpoint too: an anti-war documentary's Oscar win sparked heated debate both inside Russia and among the émigré community.

Tourism

Foreign arrivals are climbing sharply, with tourist flow up 23% year-on-year from January to May 2026, reaching roughly 2 million visitors, and the government projecting 6.5 million foreign tourists for the full year. Visa-free travel has been extended to visitors from China, Oman, Saudi Arabia, Jordan and Myanmar, while the e-visa program has expanded from 53 to 64 eligible countries, with Moscow and St. Petersburg remaining the top draws for arrivals.

Site to visit: Lake Baikal in Siberia, the world's oldest and deepest freshwater lake, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site prized for its clarity and unique wildlife. Summer (June–August) is peak season for hiking and boat excursions, while winter brings ice thick enough to walk on; the usual gateway is a flight to Irkutsk, followed by a short bus or taxi ride to the lakeside town of Listvyanka.

🔥 Trending

Music · Bashkortostan

Bashkir folk-pop trio Ay Yola has become a genuine grassroots phenomenon, with their track "Homay" topping Russian streaming charts on the strength of organic TikTok virality rather than major-label promotion. The song's appeal comes from fusing ancient Bashkir mythic storytelling with modern pop production, resonating with listeners looking for regional and ethnic identity in domestic music at a moment when much of the industry is inward-looking. Its sustained chart run into mid-2026 reflects a wider trend of Russia's regional folk-fusion acts breaking out nationally through short-form video rather than traditional radio.

Monday, July 20, 2026 · Europe / Asia

Ukraine

Europe · population ~44M (pre-war estimate)

Politics & Government

Kyiv underwent its biggest political shake-up in months after parliament accepted the resignation of Prime Minister Yulia Svyrydenko, whom President Volodymyr Zelensky said he was moving into a role focused on relations with a "key partner," fueling speculation she could become ambassador to Washington. Ukrainian media point to Naftogaz chief Sergii Koretskyi as her likely successor as the country braces for renewed Russian strikes on the power grid this winter. The reshuffle also swept up the defense ministry, where Zelensky dismissed Mykhailo Fedorov after just six months in office, with Interior Minister Ihor Klymenko reportedly offered the post. Zelensky framed the changes as a "renewal" needed to secure more air defenses from allies and bolster Ukraine's EU membership bid.

Economy & Business

Ukraine's wartime economy remains under strain, with GDP growth projected to slow to around 1% in 2026 after a winter of heavy attacks on civilian infrastructure. Electricity exports still climbed more than 50% in June even as Russian strikes left six regions without power in mid-July. On the recovery side, Kyiv signed 160 agreements worth over €10 billion at the Ukraine Recovery Conference in Gdańsk, including a €3.2 billion first EU budget-support tranche, while the domestic startup scene — now numbering roughly 874 active companies — keeps posting gains in cybersecurity, drones and cross-border B2B software despite a $9.5 billion funding gap for 2026 recovery needs.

Military & Security

Russia opened July with one of its largest bombardments of the war, firing more than 70 missiles and nearly 500 drones at Ukraine in a strike that killed 17 people in Kyiv and damaged 30 sites across the capital. Moscow cast the attack as retaliation for Ukraine's deepening drone campaign against Russian oil refineries, a campaign the Institute for the Study of War says has outpaced Russia's spring-summer offensive on the ground. The abrupt dismissal of Defense Minister Mykhailo Fedorov, credited with driving that drone-warfare surge, followed a reported rift with General Staff chief Oleksandr Syrskyi over blocked reform efforts.

Society & Culture

Fedorov's ouster triggered a rare wartime protest movement, with crowds exceeding five thousand gathering in Kyiv over three consecutive nights and solidarity rallies spreading to Lviv, Kharkiv, Odesa, Dnipro and cities abroad including London and Prague. Demonstrators carried signs reading "Bring Misha back" and demanded the removal of Commander-in-Chief Oleksandr Syrskyi, arguing the war's tech-led approach is under threat from entrenched command culture. The protests prompted notable resignations, including Air Force deputy commander Pavlo Yelizarov and volunteer drone advocate Serhii Sternenko.

Tourism

International tourism remains largely collapsed with Ukrainian airspace closed, forcing visitors to enter overland via neighboring European airports, though officials report a modest uptick in foreign and domestic wartime travel this spring and summer. Visitors are permitted to move freely but must observe curfew and air-raid shelter rules. Hospitality investment continues in safer western cities: TULIP Hotels & Residences, part of Louvre Hotels Group, opened its first Ukrainian aparthotel in Lviv.

Site to visit: Lviv's UNESCO-listed Old Town, centered on the Renaissance-era Rynok Square and its 15th-century Town Hall, is the best-preserved historic core in the country and a rare cultural anchor still open to travelers. Climbing the Town Hall's 65 steps to its observation deck gives panoramic views over the city's red-tiled roofs; spring and autumn bring the mildest weather and fewer crowds, though visitors should check current travel advisories before planning a trip given the active conflict.

🔥 Trending
Recycle
Tech / Sustainability · Kyiv

Recycle is a Kyiv-founded app that lets households, businesses and building associations sell sorted recyclables — certified recyclers bid on listed waste and pay out directly, flipping the usual "pay to have trash removed" model on its head. Co-founder Anton Ustimenko, a former game-industry entrepreneur, built it after his own Kyiv office struggled to sort waste efficiently, and the platform has since signed on retailers like homeware chain Jysk. It has grown organically at roughly 20% month-over-month with near-zero churn since launch, and a charity arm now funds recycling stations in schools and children's homes as the team eyes expansion into Poland and Bulgaria.

Turkey

Asia · population ~86M

Politics & Government

Turkey used its role hosting the July 7–8 NATO summit in Ankara as a diplomatic showcase for President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan, even as domestic political tensions simmered. Ousted opposition CHP leader Özgür Özel urged visiting NATO allies not to treat Erdoğan's government as Turkey's only political voice, citing a "legitimacy crisis" from the state's use of courts against opponents. The justice ministry confirmed investigations against 58,000 people and trials of 24,000 tied to the Gülen movement are still active, while a cost-of-living squeeze — annual inflation at 32.11% and a net minimum wage equivalent to about $600 — continues to strain households.

Economy & Business

Turkey's foreign trade deficit widened to $10.4 billion in June, pushing the 12-month gap to $95.8 billion, and the IMF trimmed its 2026 growth forecast to 2.9% in its second downgrade of the year. New business formation kept climbing regardless, up 9.3% in the first half of 2026 to over 57,000 companies, and home sales rose nearly 16% year-on-year in June on a mortgage-driven surge. On trade diplomacy, Ankara hosted the UK's trade minister to discuss doubling bilateral trade to $40 billion and signed a memorandum for a new gas pipeline to northern Cyprus.

Military & Security

The NATO summit doubled as a showcase for Turkey's defense sector, with the country unveiling its new Ay Yıldız Joint Headquarters unifying its military command structure, and roughly 3,500 companies attending an accompanying Defence Industry Forum after exports grew 48% in 2025. More than 40,000 security personnel, missile-defense systems and an alerted F-16 fleet were deployed to secure Ankara during the summit. Separately, Turkey and Egypt deepened military cooperation with a joint Golden Eagle 2026 exercise, and Ankara is reportedly weighing a sale of its Russian S-400 system to a Gulf buyer to help revive its bid to rejoin the U.S. F-35 program.

Society & Culture

Istanbul marked the anniversary of the 2016 coup attempt with citywide commemorations for its victims, while cultural diplomacy featured prominently around the NATO summit, with Ankara replacing the traditional red carpet with turquoise and staging performances by the Ottoman Mehter band. Trabzon's 34th Akçaabat festival drew folk dance troupes from five countries, and Istanbul's Kadıköy district hosted free concerts as part of the pan-European "Sound of Europe Festival" on July 18–19.

Tourism

Turkey held its position as the world's fourth most-visited country, having welcomed 60.6 million international visitors and $56.3 billion in tourism revenue over the past year. Early 2026 arrivals were up 4.2% year-on-year, led by strong UK and German demand, though Turkey's relatively high summer pricing has pushed some travelers toward the cheaper September–November shoulder season. Istanbul Airport has become the third-busiest airport in the world by passenger traffic, trailing only Atlanta and Dubai.

Site to visit: Cappadocia's sunrise hot-air balloon flights over its volcanic "fairy chimney" valleys remain one of Turkey's signature travel experiences, with standard flights running roughly €130–150 per person for a one-hour ride including breakfast and a champagne toast. April through October offers the most reliable flying conditions, with spring's mild temperatures and wildflowers considered peak season; travelers who'd rather skip the cost can watch the balloons for free from Sunset Point or Love Valley just before sunrise.

Sunday, July 19, 2026 · Africa

Nigeria

Africa · population ~225M

Politics & Government

With the 2027 elections looming, Nigeria's opposition is in disarray: the Labour Party has unveiled Hajia Bintu Konto as its vice-presidential candidate alongside Dr. Chibuzo Okereke, while an Abuja appeals court ruling barring INEC from recognising David Mark-led ADC state congresses has deepened rifts inside that party. Vice-President Kashim Shettima touted procurement reforms that saved more than ₦1.1 trillion in 2025, even as governors pushed for fiscal, judicial and constitutional reforms to underpin state policing. In Oyo State, Governor Seyi Makinde confirmed that all 39 pupils abducted in Oriire LGA have regained their freedom, though the rescue operation cost the lives of some security personnel.

Economy & Business

The IMF now projects Nigeria's economy will grow 4.1% in 2026 and 4.3% in 2027, with first-quarter growth already accelerating to 3.89%. Foreign reserves have surged to nearly $52 billion, a multi-year high, while inflation has eased to 15.9%, and Finance Minister Taiwo Oyedele unveiled a roadmap targeting a $1 trillion economy. Still, the IMF cautions that the sectors driving growth — services, ICT, finance, oil and gas — aren't the ones employing most Nigerians, and oil output running below budget target left the government's windfall far short of projections. The federal government also announced plans to overhaul struggling textile assets in Kaduna as part of a wider industrial revival push.

Military & Security

Security forces remain stretched across the north: escalating attacks by bandit groups, Boko Haram and ISWAP continue to put civilians at risk, even after a joint May offensive killed at least 175 militants. The United States withdrew most of its deployed forces in early July while continuing intelligence support, and Nigerian forces separately rescued 46 children and several teachers abducted by suspected Ansaru militants in Oyo State. The UN World Food Programme has warned that the conflict and shrinking humanitarian funding are pushing northern Nigeria toward its worst hunger crisis in nearly a decade.

Society & Culture

More than 17 million people across northern Nigeria are facing a worsening hunger crisis tied to the ongoing conflict. A broad coalition of civil society groups, unions, youth movements and faith organisations called nationwide protests demanding the release of citizens held captive across Oyo, Borno, Katsina and several other states, alongside relief from economic hardship and insecurity. Organisers say schools remain under threat and major highways are still considered unsafe despite repeated government assurances.

Tourism

United Nigeria Airlines is expanding its Boeing 737 fleet and joining the African Airlines Association, a move seen as strengthening regional and future intercontinental connectivity. Despite tourism's projected ₦11.2 trillion GDP contribution, industry leaders at NATOP's annual meeting in Lagos say the sector's potential remains largely untapped; the Nigerian Tourism Development Authority has since unveiled a roadmap including a "Naija Flavour Marketplace" and a planned international Tourism City. Several governments, including Australia's, continue to advise caution over terrorism, kidnapping and civil unrest risks for travellers.

Science & Technology

Communications Minister Bosun Tijani was inaugurated as a founding member of the AI for Good Global Commission in mid-July, underscoring Nigeria's push for a bigger voice in global AI governance. The National Information Technology Development Agency has begun validating a national IT standardisation framework, and the country's 5G spectrum auction was also reported this month. The federal government has committed roughly ₦838.7 billion to science, technology and innovation in the 2026 budget as part of a broader innovation-led growth strategy.

South Africa

Africa · population ~61M

Politics & Government

Tensions inside South Africa's government of national unity are showing: the Democratic Alliance has vowed to enforce proper consultation from its coalition partner, accusing the ANC of freezing it out of decisions on tax, land and education policy since the ANC lost its parliamentary majority in 2024. July is also Mandela Month, with the country marking Nelson Mandela's legacy on July 18. Meanwhile Johannesburg is grappling with an unfunded budget gap of roughly R2.1 billion for the new fiscal year, part of a forecast R7 billion overall deficit, and the ANC is eyeing a comeback in Cape Town and other DA-run municipalities ahead of the 2026 local elections.

Economy & Business

Growth is gradually firming: the economy expanded 1.1% in 2025 and posted a sixth straight quarter of positive growth in early 2026, though the RMB/BER business confidence index slipped to 39 in the second quarter, below its long-term average. July brought welcome fuel price cuts that are easing pressure on the construction sector and industrial supply chains, following an inflation spike to 4% in April driven largely by fuel costs. The startup ecosystem remains a bright spot, with roughly 1,237 tracked companies and more than $3.1 billion raised since 2015, led by fintech, even as electricity generation continues to lag pre-pandemic levels.

Military & Security

President Cyril Ramaphosa has authorised a large domestic deployment of the South African National Defence Force, with more than 3,000 soldiers sent nationwide from late June to bolster police during anti-migrant unrest. The military is also acting as a "force multiplier" for SAPS under Operation Prosper, targeting gang violence, extortion syndicates and illegal mining, though security analysts warn soldiers aren't trained for policing duties and risk escalating confrontations.

Society & Culture

More than 900 people were arrested during nationwide anti-migrant protests in early July, with 120 marches held across the country — most peaceful, though shops were looted and one person was shot dead. Organisers behind the March and March movement have vowed weekly Thursday demonstrations for six months, and in Alexandra protesters have entered homes searching for undocumented residents before handing them to police. Doctors Without Borders has warned of growing humanitarian strain and healthcare disruption as tens of thousands are displaced, with high unemployment fuelling much of the anti-foreigner sentiment.

Tourism

International arrivals rose 12.8% between January and May, with Brazilian visitor numbers up more than 40% and 4.2 million total visitors so far this year. LATAM launched direct Cape Town–São Paulo flights on July 2 and Emirates added a daily Cape Town–Dubai service, while new mandatory digital travel declarations for anyone entering or leaving the country took effect July 1. The boom hasn't been without friction: the same anti-immigration protests roiling politics have also disrupted airport transfers and business events in several cities.

Science & Technology

South Africa launched its first National Science Month on July 4, replacing the old National Science Week with a monthlong programme running through July 31 at venues including the Vaal University of Technology. The launch drew over 100 exhibitors and 132 stands covering AI, quantum computing, green energy and biotechnology, organised around 14 themes from climate change to science diplomacy. The push comes as the country still spends only about 0.61% of GDP on R&D, well short of its 1.5% target, despite ranking second in Africa for scientific publication output.

🔥 Trending
Timbuk2
Tech · Cape Town / townships nationwide

Timbuk2 is a small Cape Town-based AI startup paying ordinary township residents to map the informal businesses — spaza shops, hair salons, car washes — that keep local economies running but are invisible on Google Maps. "Data agents" roam their own neighbourhoods with just a phone, photographing and cataloguing shops for cash, with leaderboards and challenges keeping engagement high. In a recent mapping run in Tlhabane, Rustenburg, 79% of spaza shops turned out to have no digital footprint at all; the six-person company now hopes to expand the model across the continent.

Saturday, July 18, 2026 · Asia

Vietnam

Asia · population ~98M

Politics & Government

Vietnam's leadership settled into its new 2026–2031 term following March elections, with the National Assembly confirming Trần Thanh Mẫn as chairman, Tô Lâm as president, and Lê Minh Hưng as prime minister, with Tô Lâm's earlier elevation to Communist Party general secretary marking the first time the top party and state posts have been merged in the post-reunification era. The Communist Party continues to hold tight control over political life, and Human Rights Watch's latest country assessment notes continued suppression of dissent even as the government pushes an ambitious economic and technology agenda. Regular cabinet sessions in Hanoi have focused on implementing the new leadership's growth and administrative reform priorities.

Economy & Business

Vietnam's economy is on a tear, with GDP growth reaching 8.18% in the first half of 2026, outpacing the same period last year and pushing second-quarter growth to 8.39% year-on-year. Trade turnover hit $549.69 billion in the half, up 27.1%, though a widening trade deficit of $16.65 billion emerged as imports from China outpaced exports to the United States, still Vietnam's largest export market. Inflation ticked up to 4.38%, and the Asian Development Bank now projects full-year growth easing to around 7.2% in 2026 and 7.0% in 2027.

Military & Security

Tensions with China in the South China Sea are intensifying even as diplomatic ties warm: ship-tracking data show Chinese vessel incursions near Vietnamese-held features nearly doubling over three years. Hanoi continues building a layered deterrence posture, including coastal anti-ship missiles and the $700 million BrahMos supersonic cruise missile deal with India, while deepening defense cooperation with the United States on maritime security and training. In June, Vietnam and the Philippines jointly declared freedom of navigation in the South China Sea "non-negotiable," renewing a bilateral defense pact as both nations face Chinese pressure.

Society & Culture

Hanoi is currently hosting the Vietnam Summer Camp 2026, a two-week program bringing more than 100 young overseas Vietnamese from 32 countries on a nearly 1,500-kilometre journey through Hanoi, Hue, Da Nang and other historic sites to reconnect with their heritage. Da Nang, meanwhile, is gearing up for its Enjoyment Festival later this month after welcoming 9.77 million visitors in the first half of the year, a 22% jump driven by tourism revenue. 🔥 Trending
Cà Phê Trứng (Egg Coffee)
Food · Hanoi

A decades-old Hanoi drink is having a viral moment: cà phê trứng, invented in the 1940s by Nguyễn Văn Giang at Hanoi's Sofitel Legend Metropole during a milk shortage, is spreading across TikTok as creators whip egg yolks and condensed milk into a custard-like foam to spoon over strong Vietnamese coffee. Giang's son still runs the original Cà Phê Giảng shop, where tourists queue for the source recipe, even as a wave of home cooks worldwide post their own attempts — and mixed results. The trend's organic spread has also drawn scrutiny: doctors have flagged salmonella risk from raw egg yolks, adding a debate that's kept the drink in the conversation well beyond its usual Hanoi tourist trail.

France

Europe · population ~68M

Politics & Government

Far-right leader Marine Le Pen confirmed she will run for president again in 2027 after a Paris appeals court upheld her conviction for embezzling EU parliamentary funds but reduced her sentence, leaving the door open to a fourth presidential bid. Her National Rally party was found to have misused funds meant for European Parliament staff between 2004 and 2016, and left-wing MPs branded the party one of "white collar crime," while President Macron declined to comment on the court's ruling, calling it healthier for democracy for the presidency to stay out of judicial matters. Analysts see the ruling, and Le Pen's steadily rising support, as a pivotal marker heading into the 2027 race.

Economy & Business

The French government trimmed its 2026 growth forecast to just 0.7%, with Finance Minister Roland Lescure citing a delayed national budget and the Middle East conflict's drag on output, and Paris unveiled an extra €3 billion in spending cuts to keep its deficit-reduction targets within reach after a divided parliament forced a special law to roll over spending and taxation. Still, the Bank of France raised its short-term growth estimate, pointing to improving activity across all sectors in June and more encouraging inflation and consumption figures.

Military & Security

France's parliament approved a new military programming law committing roughly $497 billion in defense spending through 2030, lifting the 2026 budget to €66.7 billion, with the Minister of the Armed Forces warning that "high-intensity warfare has returned to Europe" amid multiplying cyber, space and hybrid threats. The Bastille Day parade showcased the army's SCORPION transformation, featuring next-generation armored vehicles and counter-drone systems reflecting lessons drawn from the war in Ukraine, where French forces remain deployed to NATO's eastern flank.

Society & Culture

France's justice ministry completed its emergency review of outstanding child sexual abuse cases, ordered by Justice Minister Gérald Darmanin after the rape and murder of 11-year-old Lyhanna exposed repeated judicial failures to act on prior allegations against her killer; the review turned up 85,047 complaints nationwide, launched 1,350 new investigations, and led to 675 arrests, with Darmanin calling it a "terrible failure from the state." Separately, Macron pledged to replant the historic Fontainebleau forest after wildfires scorched over 10% of the 20,000-hectare royal woodland and forced roughly 1,000 residents to flee, launching a fundraising campaign with the Fontainebleau municipality and the National Forest Office.

Science & Technology

Researchers at the CNRS and Université de Toulouse III published new findings on "micropeptides", atypical short proteins encoded in the genome, showing they help regulate other cellular proteins in studies using fruit flies — a discovery that could open new avenues in molecular biology. Elsewhere in French research, physicist Valentina Emiliani was named the 2026 Irène Joliot-Curie Female Scientist of the Year for her work in neurophotonics, underscoring France's continued strength in fundamental life-sciences research through CNRS's network of over 1,100 laboratories.
