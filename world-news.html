Total disbursements between April and May are set to reach US$150 million to clear a backlog owed to construction firms.
  • Mercosur-EU Trade Deal Enters Force, Benefiting Paraguay — The association agreement between Mercosur and the European Union provisionally entered into force on May 1, after 25+ years of negotiations. The deal covers 95% of Mercosur products for preferential tariffs and is expected to boost Paraguayan agricultural exports significantly.

Peru

  • Peru Confirms June 7 Presidential Runoff Between Fujimori and Sánchez — Peru's electoral board formally confirmed the June 7 runoff after the first-round results placed Keiko Fujimori (17.2% of the vote) and Roberto Sánchez as the two leading candidates out of 35. The winner will be sworn in July 28 to succeed interim President José María Balcázar.
  • Peru's Election: A Battle for the Presidency Amid Political Chaos and Crime — NPR examines Peru's turbulent political climate heading into the runoff, where both candidates have pledged to tackle surging crime rates — the electorate's top concern. The country has cycled through multiple presidents in recent years due to corruption scandals and congressional clashes.

Suriname

  • Venezuela and Suriname Officials Discuss Caribbean Energy Cooperation — Venezuela's interim government under Delcy Rodríguez received Suriname's Foreign Minister Melvin Bouva at Miraflores Palace in mid-May to discuss direct dialogue and Caribbean regional cooperation, particularly around energy. The meeting signals Suriname's effort to maintain pragmatic ties as Venezuela navigates post-Maduro instability.
  • India and Suriname Deepen Bilateral Ties at 9th Joint Commission Meeting — India and Suriname held their 9th Joint Commission meeting in early May, reviewing cooperation in defence, trade, digital infrastructure, and AI. The talks reflect Suriname's growing international profile as it prepares for a major oil boom from Block 58 offshore development.

Uruguay

  • Uruguay Signs MOU with UK on Aerospace, Infrastructure and Digital Innovation — Uruguay's government signed a memorandum of understanding with the United Kingdom on May 26 to advance bilateral cooperation in aerospace, infrastructure, and digital innovation for at least four years. The agreement reflects the Orsi administration's strategy of deepening ties with European partners alongside the Mercosur-EU deal.
  • Uruguay's Orsi Hits Lowest Approval Since Taking Office — President Yamandú Orsi's approval rating fell to 29% in May 2026 (disapproval at 46%), reflecting public frustration with slow growth projected at 1.8% for the year. The administration faces competing pressures from left-wing Frente Amplio factions demanding higher wealth taxes and the need for fiscal consolidation.

Venezuela

  • Venezuela's Acting President Rodríguez Announces Government Reorganization — Acting President Delcy Rodríguez, who assumed power after the US military capture of Nicolás Maduro in January 2026, announced a major government reorganization in May, appointing Hector Rodríguez to lead restructuring efforts. The move comes as Venezuela's interim authorities attempt to stabilize governance amid ongoing US pressure.
  • Chevron Consolidates Venezuela Heavy Oil Position in Asset Swap — Chevron announced an asset swap to consolidate its heavy oil position in Venezuela, signaling continued US energy sector engagement despite the political upheaval following Maduro's removal. The deal underscores Venezuela's strategic importance to global oil markets even as the country navigates a leadership transition.
