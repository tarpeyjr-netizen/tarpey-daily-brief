Friday, July 17, 2026 · Europe

France

Europe · population ~68M

Politics & Government

Far-right leader Marine Le Pen confirmed she will run for president again in 2027 after a Paris appeals court upheld her conviction for embezzling EU parliamentary funds but reduced her sentence, leaving the door open to a fourth presidential bid. Her National Rally party was found to have misused funds meant for European Parliament staff between 2004 and 2016, and left-wing MPs branded the party one of "white collar crime," while President Macron declined to comment on the court's ruling, calling it healthier for democracy for the presidency to stay out of judicial matters. Analysts see the ruling, and Le Pen's steadily rising support, as a pivotal marker heading into the 2027 race.

Economy & Business

The French government trimmed its 2026 growth forecast to just 0.7%, with Finance Minister Roland Lescure citing a delayed national budget and the Middle East conflict's drag on output, and Paris unveiled an extra €3 billion in spending cuts to keep its deficit-reduction targets within reach after a divided parliament forced a special law to roll over spending and taxation. Still, the Bank of France raised its short-term growth estimate, pointing to improving activity across all sectors in June and more encouraging inflation and consumption figures.

Military & Security

France's parliament approved a new military programming law committing roughly $497 billion in defense spending through 2030, lifting the 2026 budget to €66.7 billion, with the Minister of the Armed Forces warning that "high-intensity warfare has returned to Europe" amid multiplying cyber, space and hybrid threats. The Bastille Day parade showcased the army's SCORPION transformation, featuring next-generation armored vehicles and counter-drone systems reflecting lessons drawn from the war in Ukraine, where French forces remain deployed to NATO's eastern flank.

Society & Culture

France's justice ministry completed its emergency review of outstanding child sexual abuse cases, ordered by Justice Minister Gérald Darmanin after the rape and murder of 11-year-old Lyhanna exposed repeated judicial failures to act on prior allegations against her killer; the review turned up 85,047 complaints nationwide, launched 1,350 new investigations, and led to 675 arrests, with Darmanin calling it a "terrible failure from the state." Separately, Macron pledged to replant the historic Fontainebleau forest after wildfires scorched over 10% of the 20,000-hectare royal woodland and forced roughly 1,000 residents to flee, launching a fundraising campaign with the Fontainebleau municipality and the National Forest Office.

Science & Technology

Researchers at the CNRS and Université de Toulouse III published new findings on "micropeptides", atypical short proteins encoded in the genome, showing they help regulate other cellular proteins in studies using fruit flies — a discovery that could open new avenues in molecular biology. Elsewhere in French research, physicist Valentina Emiliani was named the 2026 Irène Joliot-Curie Female Scientist of the Year for her work in neurophotonics, underscoring France's continued strength in fundamental life-sciences research through CNRS's network of over 1,100 laboratories.

🔥 Trending
Mauvais Djo
Music · Paris nightclub scene

Mauvais Djo (Joel Raul Marcolino), a rapper who cut his teeth as a nightclub MC and co-founded the hip-hop trio Triangle des Bermudes, has become one of France's most unexpected breakout stars of 2026. His song "Pilé" took off organically after the YouTube channel Kano Choir uploaded an AI-generated gospel remake that went viral on its own; rather than fight the trend, Djo leaned in and released an official gospel version featuring Kano Choir, Taliixo and Dj Wiils, which climbed to No. 8 in France, No. 3 in the Netherlands and No. 2 in Switzerland and earned platinum certification at home. The follow-up single "Maladie" has kept the momentum going through the summer, with fans crediting the grassroots, fan-driven remix culture — not a label push — for turning a nightclub MC into an international chart act. Thursday, July 16, 2026 · Asia

Japan

Asia · population ~124M

Politics & Government

Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi is heading into the final stretch of a tense Diet session, with the administration extending the sitting by about a week to July 24–27 so it can push through remaining priorities, including a bill to establish a secondary capital. Opposition parties ended their committee boycott after Takaichi agreed to testify over the Bunshun smear-campaign allegations, easing one flashpoint even as friction with coalition partner Ishin and tension within Taro Aso's faction persist. Separately, two imperial succession reform bills cleared the Lower House and were headed for swift Upper House approval, and the government moved to launch a new intelligence council chaired by the prime minister to centralize coordination against foreign espionage.

Economy & Business

Japan's economy is recovering moderately even as the yen slides to its weakest level against the dollar since 1986, a trend Takaichi has arguably encouraged by signaling a preference for continued monetary easing, straining smaller exporters and importers alike. The Bank of Japan has nonetheless pushed its policy rate to a 31-year high of 1%, and the government unveiled a sweeping ¥370 trillion ($2.3 trillion) public-private investment plan spanning 14 years to bolster AI, semiconductors, biotech, defense, energy and shipbuilding, though economists have flagged fiscal risk in the state picking industrial winners. Workers have captured some of the upside: employees at 5,368 Rengo-affiliated companies secured average wage gains of 5.01%, a third straight year above 5%. Consumers face a countervailing squeeze, with price hikes hitting 2,566 food items in July as elevated crude and naphtha costs ripple through packaging and processed foods.

Military & Security

Takaichi is accelerating a historic shift in Japan's defense posture, reframing pacifist Japan's buildup around "new ways of defense" language drawn from lessons in Ukraine and Iran, while removing restrictions on arms transfers and expanding intelligence capabilities. The FY2026 defense budget rose to roughly ¥8.81 trillion ($56 billion) as part of a plan to nearly double defense spending to 2% of GDP by 2028, with funding tilted toward long-range strike, missile defense and unmanned systems meant to hold the first island chain against Chinese pressure. Tokyo is also courting outside partners, with Defense Minister Koizumi holding bilateral talks with the Netherlands and Sweden at the NATO Ankara Summit as Japan works to keep the alliance engaged in the Indo-Pacific.

Society & Culture

Japan is easing into summer festival season even as health authorities report that seasonal infection cases have already crossed the nationwide warning threshold for the first time in two years, prompting some fireworks shows and festivals to shift their traditional July-August dates. Culturally, nostalgia is having a curated moment: Showa-era cafes, Heisei pop aesthetics, disposable cameras and chunky typography have moved from ironic throwback to mainstream comfort culture. Alongside it, oshikatsu (idol fan culture) has shed its old stigma and is now openly discussed at work and on dating profiles, offering a sense of structure and community many say feels harder to find elsewhere.

Science & Technology

Tokyo is moving to build sovereign AI capacity, funding Noetra — a new consortium of SoftBank, Sony, NEC and Honda working with the National Institute of Advanced Industrial Science and Technology — to develop a national AI foundation model amid concern that Japan is falling behind the U.S. and China. In space, JAXA notched a milestone with its experimental reusable RV-X rocket, which lifted off, hovered and moved laterally before landing upright in a brief test at the Noshiro Testing Center — a step toward cheaper launches to compete with SpaceX. The government is also pushing academia and industry toward closer three-way cooperation on dual-use technology research at universities and national institutions.

🔥 Trending
Norinori Core
Music / AI · Online (Japan-wide)

Since debuting in early May, the anonymous YouTube channel Norinori Core has been remixing six decades of J-pop into hyperactive, AI-assisted "nori-nori" (very upbeat) dance edits — stuttering vocal chops, driving EDM beats and a deliberately gonzo, unpolished style built for Ultra Music Festival energy rather than radio polish. The project has struck a nerve precisely because it doesn't try to pass as a slick human artist the way most AI-music controversies do; instead it leans into chaos, and The Japan Times called it "Japan's first great AI music trend" for reframing what AI remix culture could look like beyond simply replacing human musicians. It has spread largely through word of mouth among online music communities rather than any label push, turning old J-pop catalogues into an unlikely meme-driven dance subculture.

Wednesday, July 15, 2026 · North America

Panama

North America · population ~4M

Politics & Government

President José Raúl Mulino marked two years in office with a report to the nation in early July, coinciding with the National Assembly seating a new Board of Directors to lead the legislature. Panama's political arena remains dominated by rapid geopolitical realignment, with Mulino's government having exited China's Belt and Road Initiative, deepened security ties with Washington, and continued grappling with the Darién migration corridor. On July 7 the Security Cabinet approved four multimillion-dollar public-security contracts — police body cameras, a modernized national operations center, a foreign-resident ID system, and a new police barracks in Chitré — with the tender documents kept confidential, a move already drawing scrutiny.

Economy & Business

Panama's economy keeps outpacing its neighbors: Citi's country manager raised his 2026 growth forecast to 4.4%, above IMF and World Bank estimates, after GDP expanded 4.8% year-over-year in the first quarter. That strength masks a warning sign on investment: foreign direct investment fell 61.5% in the first quarter as reinvested profits dried up, complicating the government's plan to create 80,000 private-sector jobs and stoking concern over arbitration claims tied to the forced closure of the Cobre Panamá mine. The government is countering with an $11 billion infrastructure push covering health, education and roads, while the Canal and Colón Free Zone continue anchoring the logistics-driven economy.

Military & Security

Panama is hosting Panamax 2026, a multinational exercise running July 6 through August 14 that brings together security forces from more than a dozen allied nations and roughly 1,500 personnel to protect the Canal and other strategic infrastructure. The exercise dovetails with the Security Cabinet's newly approved body-camera and surveillance contracts for the National Police, part of a broader push to modernize domestic security capacity even as the tender details remain sealed from public view.

Society & Culture

Severe flooding tore through the Caribbean province of Bocas del Toro this month: Sinaproc reported more than 4,100 people affected and hundreds of homes damaged after days of rain triggered landslides and cut off several communities, including a slide that killed a child in the community of Bambú. The civil-protection agency extended its storm advisory through July 15. Separately, health officials are still battling a dengue wave: Panama logged 10 deaths and more than 3,100 cases in the first half of the year, with children aged 10 to 14 the hardest-hit group, even as hospitalizations have eased from 2025 levels.

Science & Technology

Panama is angling to become a regional AI and semiconductor hub, joining the US-led Pax Silica Initiative in late June to align with allied efforts on secure AI and chip supply chains, alongside a national AI strategy and a new residency pathway for foreign scientists. On the infrastructure side, the Ministry of Public Works has earmarked $1.2 billion for road modernization in 2026, a program expected to generate more than 30,000 jobs and further tie the country's tech ambitions to its logistics-driven economy.

🔥 Trending
Boquete Geisha Coffee
Food & Agriculture · Boquete, Chiriquí

Little has generated more organic buzz about Panama abroad than the tiny highland town of Boquete, where family-run farms keep shattering world coffee-price records. At the most recent Best of Panama auction, a washed Geisha lot from Hacienda La Esmeralda sold for $30,204 per kilogram — more than triple the previous record — pushing the sale of just 20 kilograms past $604,000. The variety, originally from Ethiopia, found its true fame in Boquete's volcanic highlands, and buyers from Dubai to Seoul now chase it with the fervor once reserved for rare wine, turning a once-obscure smallholder crop into a global pilgrimage destination for coffee tourists. This year's edition of the auction, expected later in 2026, is already drawing anticipation over whether the record will fall again.

Tuesday, July 14, 2026 · Asia

Iran

Asia · population ~90M

Politics & Government

Four months after Ali Khamenei's death, the regime staged a heavily militarized state funeral to project unity, formally installing Mojtaba Khamenei as the new supreme leader, with judiciary chief Gholamhossein Mohseni Ejei quickly pledging loyalty. The moment the state-mandated mourning period ended, nationwide protests and underground resistance activity resumed, with PMOI/MEK-linked units carrying out dozens of anti-regime actions between July 4 and 11 in direct defiance of the funeral proceedings. A Norway-based rights group has separately raised alarm over a surge in executions of political prisoners since the ceasefire with the US and Israel, with at least 101 Iranians executed in June alone. Adding to the external pressure, the UK proscribed Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps as a terrorist organisation on July 13, the first time Britain has designated a foreign state agency as such, citing a wave of alleged IRGC-linked plots on British soil.

Economy & Business

The IMF now projects Iran's economy will contract by 6.1 percent in 2026, compounding a 2.7 percent contraction the prior year, as nearly 40 days of bombardment, a record-length internet shutdown, and a US naval blockade of southern ports have battered output. Inflation has climbed to levels unseen since World War II, up 88.6 percent year-on-year, with bread prices in Tehran province roughly doubling and at least eight Iranian banks suffering service outages this week. Amid the strain, Iran has opened talks on resuming oil exports to Japan for the first time since 2019, though buyers are seeking a longer US sanctions waiver and guarantees around safe transit through the Strait of Hormuz before committing.

Military & Security

The ceasefire reached between the US and Iran in June has continued to unravel: fresh strikes rocked the region on July 9, with Iran firing missiles at Jordan after reported US strikes near the Bushehr nuclear plant and the IRGC hitting American bases in Kuwait and Bahrain in response. Washington's reimposed oil sanctions carry a July 17 wind-down deadline for Iranian crude, and the Treasury has designated a related financial network. Iran's acting defense minister has called the country's missile and drone programs a non-negotiable red line, even as satellite imagery suggests Tehran may be attempting to rebuild damaged nuclear and missile facilities.

Society & Culture

With the funeral mourning period over, retirees, defrauded depositors, and workers staged protests across several cities on July 12 over poverty, inflation, and systemic financial losses, following student rallies at Azad University campuses in Tehran, Karaj, and Ahvaz in late June. Rights monitors report at least 5,000 women were arrested during security operations and protests between January and June, though severe internet restrictions have made it possible to publicly identify only 368 of them. The broader executions crisis has also drawn renewed calls for accountability, with activists urging an international tribunal for senior regime officials.

Science & Technology

Despite sanctions, Iran's science ministry says the country ranks first among Islamic nations in cognitive sciences and ninth globally in advanced materials research, a point Minister Hossein Simaei-Sarraf made at the Turkey Educational Technologies Summit this month. The ministry has also outlined plans to launch 15 new AI laboratories across Iranian universities to build and test foundational AI models, part of a broader push on research commercialization and science diplomacy amid continued international isolation.

🔥 Trending
Evan Band — "Eyvallah Behet"
Music · Tehran

A Persian rock-pop track by the Tehran group Evan Band, fronted by singer Hossein Sharifi, has been steadily climbing Iran's informal music charts this year, turning up repeatedly across trending Persian playlists and short-form video clips. Its heartfelt, defiantly upbeat lyrics have resonated with young Iranians navigating a year of war, funerals, and economic strain, giving the song the feel of a communal release valve rather than a chart-chasing single. The band operates largely outside Iran's state-sanctioned pop machine, relying on streaming platforms and word-of-mouth sharing rather than official promotion — a pattern common to Iran's rock and alt-pop scene, which has long had to build audiences informally given restrictions on Western-style music. The track's live version and companion clips continue to circulate widely on Spotify and TikTok, cementing Evan Band as one of the more organically popular acts among Iran's diaspora and domestic listeners alike this summer.

Monday, July 13, 2026 · Africa

Kenya

Africa · population ~56M

Politics & Government

President William Ruto used a July 5 church service in Embu County to warn against tribal politics, urging Kenyans to reject leaders who thrive on ethnic rhetoric ahead of the 2027 vote. The opposition ODM party has said it will not field its own presidential candidate in 2027, instead backing Ruto's re-election, part of a broader look at the year's key political developments. Ahead of the July 16 Ol Kalou parliamentary by-election, rights groups have raised alarm over reported election offences, while police say 14 suspects have been arrested over clashes at opposition rallies in Kisumu and Nyahururu that left one person dead. A recent profile rounds up Kenya's top political leaders' plans for the rest of the year.

Economy & Business

The World Bank has trimmed its 2026 growth forecast for Kenya to 4.3%, down from an earlier 4.9% estimate, a downgrade confirmed by CNBC Africa and blamed on Middle East conflict spillover, pricier energy and softer remittances. The revision came despite a stronger-than-expected first quarter, when GDP grew 5.3% year-on-year as manufacturing, mining, construction and agriculture all posted gains. Higher global energy prices are expected to squeeze household purchasing power and slow private investment through the rest of the year. Nairobi's startup scene remains active regardless, with fintech, agtech and clean energy drawing continued investor interest per a July startup roundup.

Military & Security

Kenya remains locked in a long-running conflict with al-Shabaab along its Somali border, and analysts warn 2026 could bring a resurgence of surprise attacks alongside deepening inter-communal banditry tied to cattle rustling, according to a security outlook for the year. Domestically, low-level Gen Z-led unrest resurfaced in late June and early July as a remembrance of demonstrators killed in the 2024 and 2025 protests. Detectives from the Directorate of Criminal Investigations also raided a suspected gang hideout in Eldoret, recovering military-style gear linked to organized crime. The country continues to host more than 835,000 refugees, mostly from Somalia and South Sudan, adding pressure to an already strained security apparatus.

Society & Culture

Kenya marked Saba Saba on July 7, the anniversary of the 1990 pro-democracy protests, with heavy police deployments shutting down planned marches in Nairobi and beyond. Organizers had notified police of a march of up to 3,000 people to demand accountability for alleged extrajudicial killings and enforced disappearances, but at least ten people were arrested on obstruction charges, per coverage of the day's events. The protests unfolded against a backdrop of unresolved cases from 2024 and 2025, when dozens were killed in demonstrations over economic hardship and police brutality; a Human Rights Watch report notes 16 people abducted during last year's unrest are still missing. Authorities have yet to prosecute security forces in most excessive-force cases from the past three years.

Science & Technology

Kenya is piloting drone technology to speed up reforestation, dispersing tree seedballs across degraded, hard-to-reach land as part of a government target to plant 15 billion trees by 2032. The University of Nairobi's Nairobi Innovation Week, themed "Youth-Driven Solutions for a Sustainable Future," opens July 15 and will showcase student and startup projects publicly. More broadly, generative AI adoption is climbing across Kenyan businesses, concentrated in content creation, customer service and data analysis, while renewables such as geothermal, wind and solar now supply over 60% of the country's electricity.

🔥 Trending
UrbanTok
Tech / Social Video · Nairobi

UrbanTok, a Nairobi-built social video platform, launched at the Connected Africa Summit 2026 with a pitch that's resonating with young Kenyan creators: faster, fairer payouts for African content makers than global platforms like TikTok and Instagram have delivered. The app drew more than 20 African ICT ministers to its launch, and local creators are championing it as proof that Kenyan-built platforms can compete on the continent's own terms rather than importing Silicon Valley's monetization model. Momentum is building organically within Nairobi's creator community, who see it as a homegrown alternative finally designed around local creators' economics.

Sunday, July 12, 2026 · Asia

Israel

Asia · population ~9.8M

Politics & Government

Israel is barreling toward early elections after the Knesset is expected to dissolve on July 17, setting up a national vote on October 27 — the latest date permitted by law. Prime Minister Netanyahu, who shored up his coalition with the ultra-Orthodox parties, is racing to pass legislation before dissolution, including a politically appointed probe into the failures surrounding the October 7 massacre, which cleared its first Knesset reading 59-0 after an opposition boycott. New electoral alignments are also taking shape, including a fresh Arab-Jewish party and an alliance between Hili Tropper and Yoaz Hendel's Reservists Party.

Economy & Business

The IMF has cut its 2026 growth forecast for Israel to 3.5%, down from a pre-war projection of 4.8%, citing elevated defense spending, regional tensions, and a labor supply squeezed by military mobilization. Despite the drag, the country's tech sector keeps humming: Israel has held onto its #3 global ranking in startup ecosystem value, now worth $335.1 billion with 26 active unicorns, led by cybersecurity, biotech, and AI infrastructure. On the ground, shoppers are seeing a mixed picture, with steep discounts at some retailers offset by price hikes of up to 14% in parallel categories.

Military & Security

Nine months into a ceasefire, Israel now controls nearly 70% of Gaza, with troops continuing to push into new "orange zone" territory. Satellite analysis has identified roughly 40 Israeli military outposts inside Gaza, eight built entirely from scratch since the truce took hold. Aid groups have suspended operations in parts of northern Gaza, and Gaza's health ministry reports nearly 930 Palestinians killed and over 2,800 injured in the seven months since the ceasefire began, underscoring how fragile the truce remains.

Society & Culture

Israel marked 1,000 days since the October 7 massacre with large memorial gatherings in Tel Aviv's Hostages Square, where thousands demanded a state commission of inquiry; related marches blocked the Ayalon Highway and led to clashes and arrests outside the Knesset. On a lighter note, the Maccabiah Games kicked off in Jerusalem, drawing a record 43 countries of Jewish athletes to what's billed as the "Jewish Olympics," with tributes to hostages and fallen soldiers woven into the opening ceremony.

Science & Technology

Israel's government has approved a sweeping national AI program aimed at making the country a leading AI power, with planned investment in computing infrastructure, quantum research, and public services. In healthcare, the Health Ministry and Innovation Authority launched a regulatory sandbox for AI medical tools, with pilots including an at-home ultrasound platform for autonomous pregnancy-scan analysis. The moves come as Israel's startup ecosystem holds its #3 global ranking for a sixth straight year, anchored by strength in cybersecurity and deep tech.

🔥 Trending
Temper City — "Self Aware"
Music · Israeli trio (L.A.-based)

A scrappy rock trio of Israeli writer-producers — Eytan Peled, Chen Kordova, and Aviv Barenholtz — has become an unlikely breakout story: their song "Self Aware" made Temper City the first Israeli band to crack the Billboard Hot 100. The fraught-relationship anthem took off almost entirely through organic TikTok spread rather than major-label push, snowballing from niche listens into a genuine chart hit by April. Its momentum has turned the group into a talking point back home as a homegrown act finding global traction through grassroots internet virality rather than industry machinery.
