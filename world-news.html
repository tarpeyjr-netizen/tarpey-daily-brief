Tuesday, July 7, 2026 · Europe

Spain

Europe · population ~48M

Politics & Government

Spain's government is navigating a fresh controversy over a citizenship law dispute that grants nationality to descendants of Spaniards exiled during the Civil War and Franco dictatorship, with critics warning that voting rights for the large diaspora in the Americas could sway national elections while supporters call it overdue historical redress. Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez has paired the debate with a new Integration and Citizenship Plan on migration, alongside measures protecting unaccompanied foreign minors and addressing the country's housing affordability crunch. Commentary from Al Jazeera argues the ruling socialists' governing formula is running out of runway as coalition tensions persist.

Economy & Business

Madrid raised its official 2026 growth forecast to 2.6% from 2.2%, arguing the economy remains well-positioned to absorb energy-price shocks tied to the Middle East conflict, with GDP growth now running three times faster than the wider euro area. The labour market keeps outperforming, with unemployment at its lowest level since 2008 even as inflation held at 3.2% in June for a third straight month. Beneath the headline figures, Spain's startup ecosystem keeps expanding, now counting more than 12,000 startups and 18 unicorns as software, travel tech and fintech attract the bulk of new funding.

Military & Security

Spain currently has more than 3,000 troops deployed across 17 international missions on four continents under UN, NATO and EU mandates, including command of the eastern sector of the UN's Lebanon mission and a NATO battlegroup rotation in the Baltics. The country is also rolling out an 11-year, €35 billion military modernisation programme covering tanks, artillery, naval systems and digital communications, alongside delivery of its first HALCÓN I Eurofighters to replace ageing F/A-18 Hornets. Separately, Foreign Minister José Manuel Albares' proposal for the EU to build its own standing armed forces has so far drawn little concrete support from other member states.

Society & Culture

More than 50 civil-society groups are mobilising behind a July 26 demonstration aiming to "collapse" Palma de Mallorca, the latest escalation in Spain's wave of anti-tourism protests driven by soaring housing costs and overcrowding in the Balearics, Barcelona and San Sebastián. Researchers have also flagged the rapid growth of Núcleo Nacional, a neo-Nazi network claiming roughly 2,000 members that registered a political party, Noviembre Nacional, earlier this year — a sign of hardening far-right activism online.

Science & Technology

Spain approved a €719 million investment in an AI "gigafactory" as it courts EU co-financing, a push underscored by OpenAI's decision to open a Madrid office to deepen local ties. On the green-energy side, the country's renewables buildout is already showing up in lower electricity bills, with marine protected areas expanded to 22.5% of Spanish waters en route to a 30% target. Spain is also coordinating the €400 million PIXEurope initiative to scale up European photonics manufacturing, part of a broader bid to carve out a niche in semiconductors and quantum computing.

🔥 Trending
La Ludwig Band — "Xavier, el tècnic de so"
Music · Catalonia (Barcelona region)

A Catalan indie band's homage to their own sound engineer has turned into one of Spain's unlikeliest summer hits: "Xavier, el tècnic de so" is a sincere, humorous tribute to the sound technician who mixes their live shows, and by extension to every unsung technician who makes live music possible. Despite running past five minutes — far from radio-friendly — it has spread organically through fans imitating Xavier's on-stage banter on social media, with its loose, playful rhythm fitting neatly into Spain's summer town-festival season. La Ludwig Band, part of the current Catalan indie scene blending pop, folk-rock and singer-songwriter influences, is now being tipped as a genuine contender for Spain's "song of the summer" — grassroots momentum built without a major-label push.

Monday, July 6, 2026 · South America

Venezuela

South America · population ~28M

Politics & Government

Acting President Delcy Rodríguez is facing rising political pressure as public anger builds over her government's handling of the aftermath of last month's twin earthquakes, with her disapproval rating climbing to 63.3% in June. An AtlasIntel survey published this week found nearly half of Venezuelans now say holding new elections is more urgent than rebuilding, with no concrete election date yet in sight despite continued backing from the Trump administration. Rodríguez, who took over in January after Nicolás Maduro's abduction by U.S. forces, recently made her first trip abroad since taking office, traveling to The Hague for a land dispute case with Guyana.

Economy & Business

International oil majors are rushing back into Venezuela, with BP signing a deal with PDVSA to develop the Cocuina-Manakin field and Eni inking an agreement for a joint heavy-crude development. Oilfield services giant SLB also signed a long-term framework agreement with PDVSA to help modernize the country's oil and gas sector. Venezuela's crude exports hit a seven-year high, with the U.S. remaining the top buyer, followed by India and Europe, after Treasury eased sanctions and dispute-resolution rules for firms signing contracts with PDVSA.

Military & Security

Nearly 2,000 U.S. military personnel remain deployed in Venezuela supporting earthquake disaster relief operations, with Navy ships including the USS Fort Lauderdale and USS Billings providing logistics and medical support from Puerto Rico and Curaçao. The Fort Lauderdale's arrival in La Guaira marked the first U.S. Navy port call to Venezuela in decades, executed at the direct request of the Venezuelan government as it continues recovering from the earlier U.S. operation that captured Maduro in January.

Society & Culture

Venezuela continues grappling with the aftermath of the June 24 twin earthquakes, with the confirmed death toll now above 3,300 and thousands still missing. A man was pulled alive from rubble eight days after the quakes in what rescuers called a "miraculous" survival, one of several dramatic rescues this week involving international teams from the U.S., Chile, Portugal, Costa Rica, El Salvador and Mexico. Communities in hard-hit areas like La Guaira have organized their own shelters and meal distribution amid strained hospitals and shortages of food and clean water.

Science & Technology

Venezuela Tech Week 2026 drew more than 700 participants from 50 countries, connecting local startups with over 120 international investors managing $1 billion-plus in assets. The government used the event to announce "Startup Venezuela," a new fund backed by the Ministry of Finance and the Science and Technology Fund to support entrepreneurship at public and private universities. Organizers noted the country's digital economy now accounts for 96% of commercial transactions, with 17.6 million internet users nationwide.

🔥 Trending
Andry Kiddos
Music · Venezuelan diaspora (Colombia/Spain/Mexico/U.S.)

Andry Kiddos, a Venezuelan producer-turned-singer who left the country and has since lived across Colombia, Spain, Mexico and the U.S., is drawing organic buzz for "asimilando," a gentle alt-pop track blending folk and flamenco textures. The song has become an unofficial anthem for displaced Venezuelans and the wider diaspora, resonating especially now as earthquake devastation has pushed even more Venezuelans abroad. Kiddos describes the track as being about acceptance rather than sadness — "reconciling with it in order to keep growing in peace" — a message that's spread largely through word of mouth and diaspora playlists rather than major label push, earning him a spot on Rolling Stone's list of Latin artists to watch in 2026.

Sunday, July 5, 2026 · Africa

Egypt

Africa · population ~114M

Politics & Government

Prime Minister Mostafa Madbouly ordered updated economic contingency scenarios to shield Egypt from the fallout of regional conflict, while also announcing a 20% electricity tariff hike for restaurants and cafés that keep operating beyond government-mandated closing hours. President Abdel Fattah El-Sisi reviewed gas supply plans for the electricity sector alongside Madbouly, Electricity Minister Mahmoud Esmat and Petroleum Minister Karim Badawi, as regional tensions continue to pressure energy markets. Separately, El-Sisi presided over the inauguration of the new State Strategic Command Headquarters, which First Lady Entissar el-Sisi described as reflecting a vision centered on national security, and renewed the leadership appointments at the National Training Academy for a second consecutive year.

Economy & Business

Egypt's economy is showing renewed strength, with real GDP growth hitting a three-year high of 5.3% in the first half of FY26, up from 3.9% a year earlier, driven by non-oil manufacturing, a rebound in Suez Canal traffic, and strong tourism and communications activity. The IMF projects growth will moderate to 4.2% in 2026 before rebounding to 4.8% in 2027, while inflation is expected to ease toward 11% by June, giving the central bank room for further rate cuts. The government is targeting EGP 3.7 trillion in investment for FY2026/27, with the private sector expected to supply 59% of that total, and the EGX30 is up 20.8% year-to-date as Egypt's startup ecosystem ranks first in North Africa.

Military & Security

Egypt is recalibrating its naval modernization strategy, shifting investment away from large surface combatants toward submarines and unmanned maritime systems as it works to secure sea lanes across the Mediterranean, Red Sea, and Suez Canal amid fiscal pressure. The shift comes as Israeli assessments continue to flag a substantial Egyptian military buildup in Sinai, with close to 40,000 troops now stationed in the peninsula. Meanwhile, renewed Gaza ceasefire talks hosted in Egypt in June left major issues unresolved, including Hamas disarmament and Israeli troop withdrawal.

Society & Culture

Egypt's national football team advanced in dramatic fashion, winning its World Cup knockout-round debut with a 4-2 penalty shootout victory over Australia on July 4, a result celebrated nationwide. Private-sector workers were also given a paid holiday on July 2 to mark the anniversary of the 30 June Revolution. On the tourism front, the Grand Egyptian Museum now displays 57,000 artifacts including the full Tutankhamun collection, helping drive a 21% jump in visitor numbers, in a country where a large Sunni Muslim majority coexists with the Middle East's largest Christian community, the Coptic Orthodox Church.

Science & Technology

Egypt's Ministry of Higher Education unveiled a national research and innovation strategy, noting the country now has over 140,000 researchers listed in Scopus and has climbed to 86th in the Global Innovation Index, with plans for a "national challenges bank," technology transfer units, and a "From University to Factory" initiative. On energy, Prime Minister Madbouly signed agreements between the New and Renewable Energy Authority, the SCZone Infrastructure Company, and Suez Canal Container Terminal to power terminal operations with clean energy, while Egypt's accession to Horizon Europe is opening new cooperation on AI, biotechnology, and renewable hydrogen with the EU.

🔥 Trending
Ahmed Nada
Food · New Cairo (Fifth Settlement)

Haty Ahmed Nada, a traditional Egyptian restaurant in New Cairo's Fifth Settlement, has become a viral sensation thanks to a homemade TikTok song built around its name: every time a table orders molokhia, staff play the "Ahmed Nada" track and perform a theatrical pouring show that draws the whole restaurant's attention. What started as an in-house gimmick around a classic home-style menu of mombar, fatta, and baked rice with pigeon spread organically across Egyptian social media, turning a neighborhood dinner spot into a must-visit destination purely on the strength of diners filming and sharing the ritual themselves.

Saturday, July 4, 2026 · Asia

China

Asia · population ~1,410M

Politics & Government

President Xi Jinping marked the Communist Party's 105th founding anniversary on July 1 with a 40-minute speech at the Great Hall of the People urging cadres to adapt to changing circumstances while "safeguarding advances," a nod to headwinds from slowing growth and demographic decline. The same week, the Ministry of Public Security began piloting a national digital identification system for online verification, drawing concern from rights defenders over expanded tracking capability. On the diplomatic front, Foreign Minister Wang Yi opened a tour of Nordic nations on July 2, part of Beijing's continued outreach to Europe.

Economy & Business

China's economy picked up pace in June, with manufacturing and retail sales improving on the back of rebounding U.S.-bound exports, which analysts say were up around 15% over the year's first four months. Beijing has set a 2026 GDP growth target of 4.5-5%, slightly below last year's goal, while the 10% Section 122 tariff on goods from major trading partners is set to expire July 24, prompting exporters to rush shipments in case rates surge again. Regulators have meanwhile elevated a slate of "future industries" — semiconductors, advanced displays, aerospace, and the low-altitude drone economy — as long-term growth engines, with new CAAC certification rules for drone manufacturing taking effect this month.

Military & Security

A new Pentagon assessment released last week described China's military expansion as "historic," projecting Beijing will field six additional aircraft carriers by 2035 — nine total, just behind the U.S. fleet of eleven — and that its nuclear arsenal will exceed 1,000 warheads by 2030. Separately, Reuters reported that covert Chinese military training of Russian forces was personally approved by Russia's defence minister, with generals from both countries directly involved — a revelation that has alarmed European officials. On the diplomatic side, U.S. President Trump has accepted an invitation to visit China and invited Xi for a reciprocal state visit, describing the relationship as "extremely strong."

Society & Culture

A controversial "Law on Ethnic Unity and Progress" took effect July 1, which human rights groups say enshrines forced assimilation of Tibetans, Uyghurs, and Mongolians; parliamentarians from Estonia, Latvia, and Lithuania have called for Europe-wide condemnation. Freedom House's 2026 country report notes that civic and labor protests have risen at the local level despite police repression, with economic grievances like unpaid wages and land seizures driving roughly 85% of unrest, even as overtly political dissent remains rare given the risk of severe penalties.

Science & Technology

China launched a new marine satellite on July 2 and dispatched research vessels on their 16th Arctic expedition on July 3, continuing a build-out of polar and ocean-monitoring capability noted by state science outlets. Longer-running efforts to close the country's gap with the U.S. in drug discovery and biotech continue to draw fresh funding, part of a five-year push to make biotech and alternative energy new pillars of the economy.

🔥 Trending
Sileme ("Are You Dead?")
Tech · Nationwide (urban China)

A bluntly named check-in app called Sileme, or "Are You Dead?", has held onto grassroots buzz across China's app-store charts after climbing to the top with zero ad spend. Users living alone tap a daily button to confirm they're okay; two missed check-ins trigger an alert to an emergency contact. Its creators are Gen-Z developers who say they built it out of the isolation of urban solo living rather than for the elderly market many assumed it targeted, and its organic spread has surfaced a wider conversation about loneliness and unmet eldercare needs. The app's continued attention has even prompted talk of a rebrand as its creators respond to the scrutiny.

Thursday, July 3, 2026 · Europe

United Kingdom

Europe · population ~68M

Politics & Government

Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced his resignation this week after a mounting Labour Party rebellion, triggered in part by Greater Manchester Mayor Andy Burnham's decisive parliamentary by-election victory in Makerfield. The UK is set to appoint its seventh prime minister in ten years, with a formal Labour leadership contest beginning July 9 and concluding before the summer recess. Meanwhile, the House of Lords wrapped its June 29–July 3 business schedule covering debates on burial plots, lobbying transparency, and nature's rights. Upcoming elections include a Norfolk Police and Crime Commissioner by-election on July 16 and a Greater Manchester mayoral by-election on July 30.

Economy & Business

The UK economy showed mixed signals heading into July: GDP grew 0.6% in Q1 2026 but fell 0.1% in April, while inflation remains above target at 2.8% and real household disposable income dropped 0.8% in the first quarter. In a significant trade development, the UK-India Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement is set to enter into force on July 15, and a tightened steel safeguard effective July 1 cut tariff-free import quotas by 60%. Business confidence weakened sharply in June, with the IoD sentiment index falling to minus 61, and 23% of hospitality businesses now operating at a loss—up from 15% three months ago.

Military & Security

In one of the biggest defence announcements in years, Prime Minister Starmer unveiled a nearly £300 billion defence investment plan to modernise Britain's armed forces, raising spending to 2.7% of GDP by 2029 and targeting 3.5% by 2035. The plan prioritises self-flying fighter jets, uncrewed submarines, and drones, with Special Forces receiving a 12% funding uplift. British forces boarded a sanctioned shadow-fleet oil tanker in the Channel last month, and the UK pledged 150,000 Ukrainian-produced drones and over 350 air defence missiles to Kyiv. Starmer is expected to present the plan at the NATO summit in Ankara on July 7–8.

Society & Culture

Anti-immigration protests flared in Belfast and Southampton in early June after a Sudanese man was arrested for a stabbing in Belfast, with UK leaders calling for calm amid demonstrations under the banner "Enough is Enough." On the healthcare front, over 100 medical workers and Amnesty International supporters gathered on Brighton Beach on June 26 to protest the NHS's contract with data firm Palantir, pressing paint-covered hands onto a giant Palantir logo to highlight the company's ties to Israel's military. A cross-party House of Commons committee had previously called for the contract to be terminated.

Science & Technology

The UK government is doubling down on AI with a £1.6 billion strategy covering 2026–2030, including two new research labs at Oxford (SOFAIR, focused on open-source AI) and UCL (BOLD, focused on rethinking machine learning) sharing £60 million in government funding. Google DeepMind will establish its first automated laboratory in the UK this year, dedicated to materials science research. UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) is channelling investment across six priority areas, from advancing AI development to building world-class data infrastructure, with healthcare and clean energy as headline applications.

🔥 Trending

Music · London

London alt-rock four-piece Keo have had a rapid and genuinely grassroots ascent through the UK's indie circuit, with their debut single I Lied, Amber surpassing 1 million Spotify streams shortly after release and the band graduating from a packed 200-capacity EBGB's set at Liverpool Sound City 2025—fans spilling into the street—to headlining the festival's main stage in May 2026. Thursday, July 2, 2026 · South America

Argentina

South America · population ~46M

Politics & Government

President Javier Milei's government underwent a significant cabinet shake-up as Diego Santilli replaced Manuel Adorni as Cabinet chief after Adorni resigned amid scandal over concealing $500,000 from his financial disclosures. Despite the turbulence, Milei's La Libertad Avanza party emerged from last year's midterms with more than double its previous congressional seats, giving the president increased legislative leverage — though still short of a majority. The Senate approved the 2026 budget 46 to 25, projecting 5% economic growth, annual inflation of 10.1%, and a peso exchange rate near AR$1,423 by December. Meanwhile, Human Rights Watch flagged that nearly 35% of federal judicial positions remain vacant, raising concerns about the independence of the judiciary under Milei's administration.

Economy & Business

Argentina's inflation stabilisation effort showed further progress as June monthly inflation came in at just 1.6%, a remarkable decline from the hyperinflationary crisis Milei inherited. Annual inflation still runs at roughly 33%, but the government points to a poverty rate falling to 28.2% — the lowest since 2018 — and a primary fiscal surplus of 1.4% of GDP. The economy is forecast to grow 3–4% in 2026, buoyed by a $20 billion currency swap deal with the Trump administration. A major tech investment also headlined the week: OpenAI and Argentine-U.S. firm Sur Energy announced Stargate Argentina, a $25 billion, 500MW AI data centre project in Patagonia — the first Stargate initiative in Latin America, with construction expected to begin this year and operations in early 2027.

Military & Security

Argentina deepened its security partnership with the United States this week on two fronts. The U.S. and Argentina co-hosted the 2026 Proliferation Security Initiative Western Hemisphere Engagement in Buenos Aires (June 23–25), drawing more than 80 officials from 20 nations to discuss regional proliferation challenges and interdiction best practices. Separately, Argentina's Navy signed a five-year cooperation agreement with the U.S. Southern Command to boost maritime surveillance in the South Atlantic, targeting illegal fishing and other threats under the "Protecting Global Commons Program." The deal drew criticism from opposition lawmakers who warned that the framing of Argentine waters as part of a "global commons" encroaches on national sovereignty.

Society & Culture

Argentina was rocked by nationwide protests after the body of 14-year-old Agostina Vega was found sexually assaulted, dismembered, and hanged in Córdoba — echoing the 2015 killing that sparked the landmark "Ni Una Menos" (Not One Woman Less) movement. Lawyers counted at least 63 registered femicides so far this year, with advocates warning the real toll exceeds 100. The case intensified public anger at President Milei, who has called the feminist movement "ridiculous and unnatural" and defunded programmes supporting gender violence victims. Argentina's social fabric also remains strained by austerity: the National Statistics Institute reports 31.6% of the population living in poverty as of June, with older people and those with disabilities hit hardest.

Science & Technology

The headline technology story is Stargate Argentina — the OpenAI and Sur Energy partnership for a $25 billion AI data centre in Patagonia — which positions Argentina as the first Latin American node in OpenAI's global infrastructure network and a regional hub for AI computing powered by renewable energy. The announcement stands in sharp contrast to deep cuts the Milei government has made to domestic research institutions: CONICET (the national scientific research council), INTA (agricultural technology), and ARSAT (satellites) have all seen budgets slashed and entire programmes eliminated. Scientists warn the hollowing out of public research capacity could undermine the very workforce needed to support Argentina's AI ambitions over the long term.

🔥 Trending
The Therian Movement
Culture / Identity · Buenos Aires

A striking grassroots phenomenon has taken hold among Argentine teenagers: the "therian" movement, in which young people identify mentally, spiritually, or psychologically with non-human animals. Buenos Aires plazas became gathering spaces for teens wearing animal masks, running on all fours, and inhabiting animal personas — beagles, cats, foxes, wolves — in scenes that went viral across TikTok, where #therian surpassed 2 million posts with Argentina leading all Latin American countries in engagement. The movement, which originated in niche online communities in the U.S. and Europe, found particularly fertile ground in Argentina's youth culture, blending identity exploration with community belonging during a period of intense economic anxiety. Critics have dismissed it as a fad, while youth advocates note that the trend reflects a broader search for connection and self-expression among a generation navigating austerity, social pressure, and a rapidly changing digital landscape.
