Cosmic Alphabet

ArtistEL Loko (Edoh Lucien Loko, 1950–2016)
StyleContemporary African Art / Mixed Media Installation
Year2016
MuseumZeitz Museum of Contemporary Art Africa, Cape Town, South Africa
Interesting Fact

EL Loko spent decades developing his "Cosmic Alphabet" — a completely invented universal script with no fixed meanings — as a deliberate protest against language as a tool of exclusion and colonial power. A student of Joseph Beuys in Düsseldorf, he described his invented letters as "a language that belongs to no one, and in which everyone can find themselves." The Cosmic Alphabet became his permanent legacy at Zeitz MOCAA, installed across nine large glass panels in Cape Town the very year he died in his birthplace village of Pédakondji, Togo.
