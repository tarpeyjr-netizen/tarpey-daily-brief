The Victory at Poltava

ArtistAlexander Evstafyevich Kotzebue (1815–1889)
StyleRussian Academic / Historical Battle Painting
Year1862
MuseumState Hermitage Museum — St. Petersburg, Russia
Interesting Fact

Kotzebue painted this monumental canvas over a century after the actual event, commemorating Peter the Great's 1709 rout of Charles XII's Swedish army — a battle that effectively ended Sweden's status as a European great power and cemented Russia's rise. The Hermitage actually holds two competing depictions of the same battle, this one and an earlier version by Louis Caravaque, allowing visitors to compare how successive generations of Russian artists mythologized the same pivotal victory. Kotzebue, born in Estonia to a German family, specialized almost exclusively in battle scenes and was the official battle painter to the Russian Imperial court.
