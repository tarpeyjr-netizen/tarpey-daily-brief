The Rongxi Studio

ArtistNi Zan (1301–1374)
StyleYuan Dynasty Literati Painting
Year1372
MuseumNational Palace Museum, Taipei, Taiwan
Interesting Fact

Ni Zan created this ink landscape at age 71 and gave it to a friend named Bo-xuan, who then passed it to his physician as a gift — the painting changed hands twice before the artist was even asked to inscribe it with a poem. The austere "one river, two banks" composition, with its deliberately spare brushwork and vast empty space representing water, was Ni Zan's signature way of expressing emotional detachment from worldly affairs. Remarkably, Ni Zan once said he painted only "the untrammeled spirit in my breast" — not the actual landscape — making the emptiness itself the subject.
