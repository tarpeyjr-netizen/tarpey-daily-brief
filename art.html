Portraits of Periodical Offering of Liang (梁職貢圖)

ArtistAttributed to Xiao Yi (Emperor Yuan of Liang, 508–555 CE); Song dynasty copy, 11th century
StyleCourt Documentary Scroll / Figure Painting
YearOriginal c. 526–539 CE; Song copy c. 11th century
MuseumNational Museum of China, Beijing, China
Interesting Fact

This scroll was painted not by a professional court artist but by Xiao Yi — a prince who would later seize the Liang throne as Emperor Yuan — during his years as governor of Jingzhou, where foreign envoys regularly passed through. It served as a living diplomatic archive, pairing each ambassador's portrait with a written description of their homeland. The original twenty-five figures have been largely lost; the Song dynasty copy now at the National Museum of China preserves twelve, making it the oldest surviving record of 6th-century China's extraordinary reach across Central Asia, Persia, Korea, and Japan.
