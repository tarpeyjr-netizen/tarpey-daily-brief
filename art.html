Kajikazawa in Kai Province

ArtistKatsushika Hokusai (1760–1849)
StyleUkiyo-e / Japanese Woodblock Print
Yearc. 1830–1832
MuseumBritish Museum, London, UK
Interesting Fact

This print is considered one of the hidden masterpieces of Hokusai's celebrated Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji series — less famous than The Great Wave, but arguably more compositionally daring. A lone fisherman balanced on a rocky outcrop mirrors the triangular silhouette of Mount Fuji above him, with his taut fishing lines forming a perfect echo of the mountain's peak. The earliest impressions were printed entirely in blue (aizuri-e), using newly imported Prussian blue pigment that had only recently arrived in Japan from Europe — a Western pigment that helped define the look of one of the most iconic series in Japanese art.
