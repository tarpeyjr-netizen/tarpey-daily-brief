Allegory of Wealth

ArtistSimon Vouet (1590–1649)
StyleFrench Baroque
Yearc. 1640
MuseumLouvre Museum, Paris, France
Interesting Fact

This painting's title has been debated for nearly 400 years. Catalogued in the French royal inventories as "Victory crowned with laurels holding an infant with bracelets and precious stones," it was renamed La Richesse (Wealth) in the 19th century — a title that went unchallenged until 2015, when scholar Nicolas Milovanovic argued it should be called Allegory of Contempt for Wealth, a complete reversal of meaning. The Louvre now hedges with the compromise title Allegory of Faith and of Contempt for Wealth. Vouet painted it likely for Louis XIII's château at Saint-Germain-en-Laye, making it a royal commission whose true subject remains contested to this day.
