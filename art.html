Golden Summer, Eaglemont

ArtistArthur Streeton (1867–1943)
StyleAustralian Impressionism / Heidelberg School
Year1889
MuseumNational Gallery of Australia, Canberra
Interesting Fact

Streeton painted this sweeping landscape en plein air at just 21 years old, during the height of a summer drought outside Melbourne — yet it radiates golden warmth rather than hardship. He deliberately set out to paint his grandest work yet, capturing what he called "nature's scheme of colour in Australia" through his trademark high-keyed blue and gold palette. The painting became a defining image of the Heidelberg School, a movement that sought to create a distinctly Australian art identity free from European conventions.
