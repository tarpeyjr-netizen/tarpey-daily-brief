ChatGPT

This Week in ChatGPT

ChatGPT is OpenAI's flagship AI assistant — the one that kicked off the modern AI boom back in late 2022 and now has hundreds of millions of users worldwide. This week was a big one, with a new model dropping, memory getting smarter, and a long-awaited coding tool landing for everyone.

The headline story is GPT-5.6, which OpenAI quietly launched on June 26 in a restricted preview. Here's the twist: access was initially limited to a group vetted and approved by the Trump administration, which raised plenty of eyebrows across the tech world. It's an unusual arrangement — typically OpenAI rolls out new models to paying subscribers first — but the company says broader availability across ChatGPT, the API, and Codex is coming soon. On the memory front, OpenAI rebuilt the whole system from scratch. The old approach basically hoarded every note about you indefinitely; the new one periodically re-synthesizes what it knows, so outdated information doesn't linger forever. Think of it like a friend who actually updates their mental notes about you rather than remembering forever that you "love sushi" from a conversation three years ago. Plus and Pro users also get double the memory capacity, and — for the first time — the full memory system is coming to free users too, made possible by a roughly 5x cut in compute costs. Meanwhile, Codex Remote went generally available on all plans, meaning you can now kick off coding tasks from your phone and have ChatGPT work on your actual Mac or Windows machine in the background, checking in with you when it needs approval.

Rounding things out, OpenAI rolled out a much-improved dictation model with better accuracy across languages and accents — particularly strong gains for Japanese, Korean, Vietnamese, and multilingual speakers who mix languages mid-sentence. And GPT-4.5 officially retired this week, with conversations seamlessly continuing on GPT-5.5. With GPT-5.6 on the horizon and free users finally getting memory, the platform is pushing hard to make the free tier feel genuinely powerful, not just a teaser for paid plans.

The shift from Llama to Muse Spark is one of the more significant strategic pivots in AI this year.
