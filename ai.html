ChatGPT

This Week in ChatGPT

It's been a quietly significant week for ChatGPT — not a flashy model launch, but the kind of steady improvements that make a tool genuinely more useful to live with. The headline is memory: OpenAI overhauled how ChatGPT remembers things about you, doubling its capacity and making updates automatic. Think of it like upgrading from a sticky note on your fridge to an assistant who actually pays attention. The new system also gives you a reviewable summary page, so you can see exactly what ChatGPT thinks it knows about you. Plus and Pro users in the US are getting it first.

The other big quality-of-life addition is email. If you've connected Gmail or Outlook, you can now ask ChatGPT to write and send a message without ever leaving the chat — no copy-pasting, no switching tabs. It's a small thing that removes a surprising amount of friction. ChatGPT also got interactive charts, a table of contents for long documents, and a proper full-screen writing mode, nudging it closer to a real productivity app rather than just a chat window.

On the model side, OpenAI simplified the picker into four tiers — Instant, Medium, High, and Pro-only — which is genuinely less confusing than the alphabet soup of model names it replaced. And if you're using GPT-4.5 anywhere, mark your calendar: it's being retired on June 27th. Not dramatic, just housekeeping — but worth knowing before something breaks.

Mistral AI

This Week in Mistral AI

Mistral had a big week — arguably one of the most action-packed stretches the French startup has had since it burst onto the scene. The splashiest move was renaming its AI assistant from "Le Chat" to "Vibe" and turning it into something far more ambitious. Vibe now has two modes: Work Mode, designed for long, multi-step tasks that take time to think through, and Code Mode, which can write code, open pull requests, and work across your entire project through a new VS Code extension. In one move, Mistral is going after ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot simultaneously.

Underneath Vibe sits a new model: Mistral Medium 3.5. It's multimodal — meaning it understands both images and text — and it's built for "agentic" tasks, where the AI doesn't just answer a question but takes a series of actions to complete a goal. There's even a dial for how much reasoning effort it applies, which is a clever way to balance speed against depth depending on what you're asking it to do.

Then there's Voxtral TTS, a new voice model that can clone any voice from just a short sample, speak multiple languages, and stream audio in real time. The zero-shot cloning part — meaning it needs no training examples, just a clip — is genuinely impressive, and a little eerie. Finally, Mistral partnered with Emmi AI to bring physics simulation into its enterprise platform, letting engineers in aerospace, manufacturing, and energy run AI-powered digital twins instead of expensive real-world tests. For a company that started as a research lab, Mistral is moving very fast into the real world.
