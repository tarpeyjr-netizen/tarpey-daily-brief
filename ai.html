ChatGPT

ChatGPT

ChatGPT is OpenAI's flagship AI chatbot — probably the most famous AI tool in the world right now. You can use it to write, research, code, brainstorm, and way more. Hundreds of millions of people use it every day.

  • Smarter memory, twice the capacity: ChatGPT overhauled how it remembers things about you. Think of it like upgrading your AI's notebook — now it holds twice as many notes, updates itself automatically, and actually uses what it knows to give you better answers. Plus and Pro users get this first, rolling out in the US. (OpenAI, June 2026)
  • Send emails straight from the chat: If you've connected Gmail or Outlook, you can now ask ChatGPT to write AND send an email — all without leaving the conversation. Like having an assistant who can actually hit "send" for you. (OpenAI, June 2026)
  • Cleaner model picker: Choosing which version of ChatGPT to use just got less confusing. OpenAI simplified the options into tiers: Instant (fast), Medium (balanced), High (deep thinking), and Pro-only — kind of like picking a coffee size that actually makes sense. (OpenAI, June 2026)
  • Interactive charts and writing tools: ChatGPT can now show you interactive charts you can explore, a table of contents for long documents, and full-screen writing mode. It's getting closer to being a real writing and research app, not just a chat window. (OpenAI, June 2026)
  • GPT-4.5 retiring June 27: The older GPT-4.5 model is being phased out at the end of the month. OpenAI is clearing the bench to focus on newer, more powerful models. If you use GPT-4.5 via the API, plan your switch now. (OpenAI, June 2026)
Mistral AI

Mistral AI

Mistral AI is a French startup that makes some of the best open-source AI models in the world — meaning anyone can download and run them. They also have a ChatGPT-style assistant called Vibe (recently renamed from Le Chat). They punch way above their weight compared to much bigger companies.

  • Le Chat becomes Vibe: Mistral renamed its AI assistant from "Le Chat" to "Vibe" and gave it a massive upgrade. It now has a "Work Mode" for tackling long complicated tasks and a "Code Mode" for writing and editing code — including a VS Code extension that works across your whole project. Basically, it's now competing head-to-head with ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot at once. (VentureBeat, June 2026)
  • Mistral Medium 3.5 released: A new model dropped — Mistral Medium 3.5 — which is multimodal (it understands images AND text) and is built for "agentic" tasks, meaning it can take a sequence of actions to complete a goal, not just answer a single question. You can even dial up or down how much it "thinks" before answering. (Mistral, June 2026)
  • Voxtral TTS — voice cloning for everyone: Mistral launched a text-to-speech model called Voxtral TTS that can clone any voice with zero examples — just give it a voice sample and it learns. It speaks multiple languages and streams audio in real time. This is the kind of tech that sounds like sci-fi but is now just... a product you can use. (Mistral, June 2026)
  • Industrial AI via Emmi: Mistral partnered with Emmi AI to bring physics-based AI simulations to industries like aerospace, manufacturing, and energy. Think: instead of running expensive physical tests, engineers can simulate them in AI. It's like a flight simulator but for designing real machinery. (Mistral, June 2026)
