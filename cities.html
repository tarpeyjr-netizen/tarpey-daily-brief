Seattle, WA

Crime & Public Safety

Seattle officials escalated their response to a prolonged public safety crisis along north Aurora Avenue on June 11, with Council President Joy Hollingsworth and Councilmember Debora Juarez joining Mayor Katie B. Wilson to announce a coordinated multi-agency crackdown. The joint effort — involving the Seattle Police Department, the City Attorney's office, and the King County Prosecutor — targets a surge in shootings and open prostitution that has frustrated residents for months. New measures include road closures near the corridor and stepped-up enforcement operations, with regional partners coordinating on prosecution strategy. Neighbors had previously raised alarms at council hearings, and the June 11 announcement signals the city is treating the situation as a regional emergency rather than a localized policing matter.

Read more: Seattle City Council Blog KOMO News

Government & Housing

A Seattle City Council committee advanced legislation this week giving the city's social housing developer greater financial flexibility to structure deals for future affordable housing projects. The move comes as Seattle faces a persistent housing shortage, and the social housing developer — created by a 2023 ballot initiative — has been working to assemble its first projects under a novel public development model. The expanded financing options are intended to make it easier for the developer to leverage public funds alongside other capital sources without being constrained by earlier restrictions. Supporters see the measure as a key step toward building the pipeline of deed-restricted housing the city needs to keep lower-income residents from being priced out.
