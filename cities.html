Week of Sat May 17 to Sat May 24, 2026

Northeast

New York–Newark–Jersey City

  • Flash flooding submerged vehicles across Queens and Brooklyn after torrential downpours dropped roughly 2 inches of rain in under an hour, raising questions about the city's long-term flood preparedness.
  • Governor Hochul and Albany legislators reached a general agreement on a $268 billion state budget, which includes an immigrant protection package negotiated between the governor's office and the Democrat-led legislature.

Philadelphia–Camden–Wilmington

  • Pennsylvania held its 2026 primary election with voter turnout appearing down in Philadelphia; the Board of Elections reported over 650 mail-in and absentee ballots submitted with errors as of May 17.
  • Philadelphia Housing Authority received authorization to pursue 40 units of deeply affordable family housing at long-vacant properties on the 2500 block of N. Fairhill Street under the Nayda Cintron Apartments project.

Boston–Cambridge–Newton

  • Boston convened its first-ever Air Quality Summit with 200 community leaders, researchers, and policymakers; Mayor Wu also announced the 2026 Open Streets Series transforming roadways into pedestrian spaces through fall.
  • The Boston Calling Music Festival is taking a break in 2026 due to hotel scarcity caused by 7 FIFA World Cup matches at nearby Gillette Stadium in June and July.

Baltimore–Columbia–Towson

  • The Independent Investigations Division identified all parties in a May 14 police-involved shooting on Wilkens Avenue, naming the man shot as 46-year-old Louis Jackson and the officers as Sharod Watson and Mark Rankine.
  • Maryland leaders are being called out as families and businesses flee to lower-cost states, with reporting highlighting a growing disconnect between the state's political narrative and residents' lived reality.

Pittsburgh

  • Pennsylvania held primary elections May 19, with Pittsburgh-area races including Rep. Summer Lee advancing in the 12th District and Rep. Chris Deluzio facing Beaver County Sheriff Tony Guy for the 17th.
  • The East Coast Volleyball Championships returned to the David L. Lawrence Convention Center over Memorial Day weekend, with 400+ teams and 3,600 athletes competing across 54 courts.

Washington–Arlington–Alexandria

  • A 21-year-old gunman was shot and killed by Secret Service agents after opening fire near a White House checkpoint on 17th Street and Pennsylvania Avenue Saturday evening — the third shooting incident near the president in one month.
  • Thousands packed the National Mall for a daylong "One Nation Under God" prayer rally as part of DC250 celebrations marking America's 250th anniversary.

Southeast

Atlanta–Sandy Springs–Roswell

  • Former Mayor Keisha Lance Bottoms won the Democratic gubernatorial primary, stunning rivals by clearing the 50% threshold to avoid a runoff; Lt. Gov. Burt Jones and Rick Jackson head to a Republican runoff.
  • Georgia Tech won the ACC Baseball Championship for the 10th time, defeating North Carolina 13-6; Hawks guard Dyson Daniels was also named second-team NBA All-Defense for the second year running.

Miami–Fort Lauderdale–West Palm Beach

  • Miami announced four free World Cup express shuttle routes from transit hubs including Golden Glades and Aventura Brightline for game days at Hard Rock Stadium in June and July.
  • Florida Power & Light's $781 million South Florida transmission line project was approved by the Public Service Commission to maintain reliability and meet growing Miami-Dade energy demand.

Charlotte–Concord–Gastonia

  • The Coca-Cola 600 NASCAR race took place at Charlotte Motor Speedway in Concord this weekend, with rain canceling qualifying for the Truck Series and the starting grid set via the rule book.
  • A person was fatally shot on N. Davidson Street near NoDa early Thursday; a separate shooting injured one person inside a northwest Charlotte Walmart Thursday night.

Jacksonville

  • The 2026 Jacksonville Jazz Festival returned to Riverfront Plaza over Memorial Day weekend with a star-studded lineup and a new Jazz in the Plaza stage experience at the heart of downtown.
  • Jacksonville opened 30 community pools for summer; separately, a burn ban is in effect citywide due to dangerous wildfire conditions across Duval County.

Raleigh–Cary

  • A woman is charged with two counts of attempted murder after shooting two Fox Rothschild attorneys outside the Wake County Courthouse; both lawyers are expected to survive, and Gwendolyn White, 57, is in custody.
  • Thousands of educators rallied at Halifax Mall demanding 25% pay increases; North Carolina currently ranks 43rd in teacher pay and is projected to drop to 46th for the current school year.

Richmond

  • NASCAR driver Kyle Busch died at 41, sending shockwaves through the Richmond racing community; Busch built a significant part of his legacy at Richmond Raceway over a Hall of Fame career.
  • The Virginia Museum of History and Culture is featuring the Thalhimer family in its "We the People" exhibit, spotlighting the department store dynasty's role in Richmond's history ahead of the nation's 250th birthday.

Memphis

  • State Rep. Antonio Parkinson called for Memphis to secede from Tennessee after the legislature passed maps that split the city's majority-Black population across three congressional districts stretching into rural areas near Nashville.
  • The Children's Museum of Memphis opened a new interactive Disney exhibit expected to draw tens of thousands of additional visitors to the Midtown facility over the summer season.

New Orleans–Metairie

  • New Orleans will host the largest-ever tall ships flotilla May 27–June 1, with vessels from around the world sailing the Mississippi River to celebrate America's Sestercentennial.
  • A Tulane University study warns "New Orleans is not forever," urging immediate long-term planning as climate-driven sea level rise, sinking land, and intensifying storms increasingly threaten the region.

Midwest

Detroit–Warren–Dearborn

Minneapolis–St. Paul–Bloomington

  • Mayor Jacob Frey nominated Reginald Freeman — a veteran fire leader with experience in Oakland and Hartford — to become Minneapolis's next fire chief, pending City Council approval.

St. Louis

Cleveland

Kansas City
