Cost of Living

Asheville runs somewhere between 4% and 11% above the national average depending on the index used, with the gap driven almost entirely by housing: shelter costs run about 13% above average while groceries track roughly even with the rest of the country and transportation actually comes in 8% cheaper thanks to short commutes. Healthcare services run about 15% above the national average, which is worth weighing against the strength of the local hospital system below. A single person's total monthly budget lands around $2,756, roughly 2% higher than a year ago.

Housing Market

The median Asheville home sits in the $462,000–$507,000 range depending on the data source and time window, with some measures showing prices down slightly year-over-year and others up modestly — a market that's cooled from its post-pandemic run but hasn't cracked. Three-bedroom homes are the most common size on the market (roughly 45% of listings) and, per recent tracking, 3- and 4-bedroom houses have actually ticked up about 3% even as the broader median softened. Inventory sits at a lean 3.2-month supply with homes selling in about 45 days on average, so it still leans toward sellers despite the headline price dip.

Sports Proximity

Asheville has no pro franchise of its own, but Charlotte — home to the Carolina Panthers (NFL) and Charlotte Hornets (NBA), plus MLS and NASCAR — is a straight shot 130 miles / about 2 hours east. Atlanta's Falcons and Braves are roughly 208 miles / 3.5 hours southwest. On the college side, UNC Asheville plays Division I in the Big South right in town, Clemson and Tennessee are each under two hours away, and the Triangle powerhouses (UNC, Duke, NC State) are a manageable 3–3.5 hour drive.

Healthcare & Hospitals

Mission Hospital, the region's flagship (part of HCA Healthcare), is rated a U.S. News Best Regional Hospital and was the only Western North Carolina hospital named to the 2025–2026 Best Hospitals list, ranking among the state's top 10 on patient safety and outcomes. Healthgrades separately named it one of America's 100 Best Hospitals for several specialties including stroke care, coronary intervention, and orthopedic surgery — solid marks for a metro this size.
