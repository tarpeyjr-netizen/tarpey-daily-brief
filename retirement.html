Cost of Living

Asheville runs somewhere between 4% and 11% above the national average depending on the index used, with the gap driven almost entirely by housing: shelter costs run about 13% above average while groceries track roughly even with the rest of the country and transportation actually comes in 8% cheaper thanks to short commutes. Healthcare services run about 15% above the national average, which is worth weighing against the strength of the local hospital system below. A single person's total monthly budget lands around $2,756, roughly 2% higher than a year ago.

Taxes on Retirement Income

North Carolina is notably retiree-friendly on Social Security: it is not taxed at all, regardless of income level. Traditional 401(k), IRA, and most pension withdrawals, however, are taxed as ordinary income under the state's flat 3.99% rate for 2026 — though certain vested government pensions are fully exempt under the Bailey Settlement, and a homestead exemption softens property tax for qualifying residents 65+ with income under about $36,700. On property tax specifically, Buncombe County's 2026 rate is 61.54¢ per $100 of assessed value, and the City of Asheville's adjusted rate is 50.78¢ per $100 — combining to roughly 1.12% effective for in-city properties, notably lower for county-only addresses outside city limits.

Housing Market

The median Asheville home sits in the $462,000–$507,000 range depending on the data source and time window, with some measures showing prices down slightly year-over-year and others up modestly — a market that's cooled from its post-pandemic run but hasn't cracked. Three-bedroom homes are the most common size on the market (roughly 45% of listings) and, per recent tracking, 3- and 4-bedroom houses have actually ticked up about 3% even as the broader median softened. Inventory sits at a lean 3.2-month supply with homes selling in about 45 days on average, so it still leans toward sellers despite the headline price dip.

Natural Disaster Risk & Insurance

Asheville's mountain setting long carried a "climate haven" reputation, but Hurricane Helene's catastrophic inland flooding in September 2024 — over 14 inches of rain, deadly flash floods and mudslides, and more than $50 billion in regional damage — exposed real flood and wildfire exposure that FEMA flood maps hadn't captured; less than 1% of the worst-hit inland areas actually carried flood insurance at the time. The home insurance market has tightened in response: the state's rate bureau sought a 42% increase last year and regulators approved 7.5%, and some Western NC policies have shifted roofs to actual-cash-value coverage following Helene-related losses. Buyers should budget for flood insurance even well inland and confirm current homeowners' coverage terms before closing.

Sports Proximity

Asheville has no pro franchise of its own, but Charlotte — home to the Carolina Panthers (NFL) and Charlotte Hornets (NBA), plus MLS and NASCAR — is a straight shot 130 miles / about 2 hours east. Atlanta's Falcons and Braves are roughly 208 miles / 3.5 hours southwest. On the college side, UNC Asheville plays Division I in the Big South right in town, Clemson and Tennessee are each under two hours away, and the Triangle powerhouses (UNC, Duke, NC State) are a manageable 3–3.5 hour drive.

Senior Services & Community

The area is well built-out for retirees: roughly 46 senior housing options span independent living, 55+ active-adult communities, assisted living, and continuing-care retirement communities, with monthly rates generally between $3,000 and $12,890. Givens Gerber Park is a notable example — an urban 55+ rental community with an on-site clinic and community nurse. UNC Asheville also runs continuing-education and lifelong-learning programs popular with retirees, and the city's dense nonprofit and arts scene offers ample volunteer opportunities.

Healthcare & Hospitals

Mission Hospital, the region's flagship (part of HCA Healthcare), is rated a U.S. News Best Regional Hospital and was the only Western North Carolina hospital named to the 2025–2026 Best Hospitals list, ranking among the state's top 10 on patient safety and outcomes. Healthgrades separately named it one of America's 100 Best Hospitals for several specialties including stroke care, coronary intervention, and orthopedic surgery — solid marks for a metro this size.
