Week ending Wednesday May 27, 2026

This Week

The biggest story of the week belongs to Sally Rooney, who announced a Hebrew translation of Intermezzo with a BDS-compliant Israeli publisher — a significant reversal of her long-standing boycott stance that drew wide coverage and a candid Guardian interview. Meanwhile, Paige Bueckers bounced back from a rough outing to pour in 24 points in the Wings' 91-76 win over the Liberty, and Ben Mezrich's chess-scandal book Checkmate is already a bestseller ahead of its June hardcover release.

Updates

Montana Fouts — Softball Player

No major breaking news this specific week, but Fouts remains on the 2026 USA Softball Women's National Team athlete pool (announced April), keeping her in contention for the 2028 Olympics. She continues to pitch for the Utah Talons in the AUSL, building on the team's inaugural championship run last July.

Magnus Carlsen — Chess Player

A big chess week for Carlsen: Norway Chess 2026 got underway and he dropped his first-round game to Alireza Firouzja at home — a rare stumble. This comes on the heels of a banner spring: he won the 2026 FIDE Freestyle World Championship (his 21st career world title), the TePe Sigeman tournament in Malmö, and the Chess.com Open Playoffs. Norway Chess will be the next real test of whether he can stay atop the standings on home soil.

John Grisham — Author

Two books loom on the horizon. First, Shaken: The Rush to Execute an Innocent Man — a nonfiction account of Texas death-row inmate Robert Roberson — arrives June 9. Then, a new international thriller, The French Illusion, publishes September 29; it follows an American couple whose Paris honeymoon turns into a kidnapping ordeal, marking a new genre direction for Grisham.

Michael Lewis — Author

Who Is Government? — Lewis's anthology of essays by writers including Dave Eggers and Sarah Vowell about overlooked public servants — published May 5 and has been generating strong press. Lewis appeared on PBS News this month to discuss it and also revealed he has a verbal deal to write a Sam Altman biography, but only once ChatGPT can write a competing draft.

Fredrik Backman — Author

No major updates this week. Backman's most recent novel, My Friends (June 2025), continues to draw strong reader response — it was the top pick on the May 2025 Indie Next List — but no new book has been announced for 2026.

Sally Rooney — Author

The news of the week in literary circles: Rooney announced on May 19 that she will publish a Hebrew translation of Intermezzo through November Books, an Israeli publisher that endorses Palestinian rights and receives no Israeli government funding — making it, in her view, compatible with BDS. She paired the announcement with a Guardian interview in which she acknowledged past inconsistencies: "How could my actions be so inconsistent with my beliefs?" A significant public statement from one of fiction's most watched voices.

Lee Child — Author

Lee and Andrew Child announced Chain Reaction, the 31st Jack Reacher novel, due October 20, 2026. The book brings back Susan Turner — Reacher's former ally, last seen in Never Go Back — alongside a secretive Washington, D.C. plot. It's the first time co-author Andrew Child has leaned into established continuity.

Mark Bowden — Author

No major updates this week. Bowden continues to contribute long-form journalism to The Atlantic and Vanity Fair, but no new book or major article surfaced in the May 20–26 window.

Chuck Klosterman — Author

No major updates this week. Klosterman's book Football (January 2026) generated significant coverage earlier in the year — including an NPR interview and a New York Times essay arguing Jim Thorpe, not Tom Brady, is the greatest football player ever — but the news cycle has moved on for now.

Tyler Cowen — Economist & Podcaster

Cowen appeared on the Outrageous Fortune podcast (May 11) for a wide-ranging conversation covering aesthetic convergence, the probability he lives for centuries, and whether Spain was the optimal colonial culinary power in 16th-century Mexico. Conversations with Tyler continues its regular run with new episodes on economics and culture.

Tana French — Author

No major updates this week. The Keeper — the conclusion to her Cal Hooper trilogy — released March 31 and received strong reviews, with a major profile in Cultured Magazine on April 1 and an Irish Times interview in late March. The tour and press cycle appears to be winding down.

Ben Mezrich — Author

Checkmate: Genius, Lies, Ambition, and the Biggest Scandal in Chess is having a moment: the ebook dropped April 7 and has been a top-selling chess title; the hardcover arrives June 2. The book covers the Carlsen–Niemann cheating controversy and the rise of Chess.com. A Hollywood adaptation is already in development for 2027. Vanity Fair ran an excerpt this month.

Scott Galloway — Professor & Podcaster

An active week on the Prof G Pod. May 19: Galloway and guests broke down the Trump–Xi summit, from trade promises to Taiwan tensions and the roles played by Musk and Jensen Huang. May 18: a two-part Office Hours with Designing Your Life authors Bill Burnett and Dave Evans on fighting the attention economy. Galloway is in full commentary mode as trade and tech policy dominate the news cycle.

Malcolm Gladwell — Author

Gladwell announced The American Way of Killing, a new book on the U.S. gun violence epidemic, due September 29, 2026. It expands on reporting from his Revisionist History podcast with new interviews and archival audio. Separately, Revisionist History returns June 27 with a nine-part series called Hitler's Olympics on the 1936 Berlin Games.

Paige Bueckers — Basketball

Bueckers bounced back in style on May 24: 24 points on 53.8% shooting as the Dallas Wings routed the New York Liberty 91–76. The recovery came after a rough stretch that included a career-low 7-point game against the Atlanta Dream. Her WNBA technical foul (for clapping) was subsequently rescinded. She's averaging 20.0 points and 5.5 assists on the young season and has already won the season-opening showdown against Caitlin Clark 107–104.

Week ending Friday May 22, 2026

This Week

Sally Rooney made international headlines by announcing a Hebrew translation of Intermezzo through a BDS-compliant Israeli publisher — a nuanced move drawing both praise and backlash — while Tyler Cowen published a high-profile piece arguing AI’s biggest threat isn’t unemployment but the psychological cost of adjustment, and Paige Bueckers continued her breakout WNBA sophomore season with 24 points in a Wings win over Chicago.

Updates

Montana Fouts — Softball Player

Fouts, currently playing for the Utah Talons in Athletes Unlimited, was named to the USA Softball Women’s National Team 2026 athlete pool — one of 36 players selected. The pool will compete at the WBSC World Cup Group Stage (September 12–16 in Oklahoma City) and the 2026 USA Softball International Cup, with all players also in consideration for the 2028 Olympic team.

Magnus Carlsen — Chess Player

Carlsen has been dominant across formats in 2026. He won the FIDE Freestyle Chess World Championship, defeating Fabiano Caruana 2.5–1.5 in Weissenhaus, Germany — his 21st world title across formats. He also won the TePe Sigeman tournament in Malmö, Sweden, and the Chess.com Open Playoffs (defeating GM Jan-Krzysztof Duda in the Grand Final for $50,000). He will defend his title at the 2026 Esports World Cup.

John Grisham — Author

Grisham has two major 2026 releases on deck. His nonfiction Shaken: The Rush to Execute an Innocent Man — about death-row inmate Robert Roberson in Texas — publishes June 9 from Doubleday. His espionage thriller The French Illusion, about a Paris honeymoon turned kidnapping, publishes September 29. Pre-publication buzz for Shaken is building with just weeks to go.

Michael Lewis — Author

Lewis has been promoting Who Is Government?: The Untold Story of Public Service, appearing on PBS NewsHour and CBS Evening News to discuss the crucial work of federal employees. He also revealed in February that he has a verbal deal to write a Sam Altman biography — but only once ChatGPT is capable of writing a competing draft. “I want to challenge it to a contest,” Lewis said. No major news in the past week specifically.

Frederik Backman — Author

No major updates this week. Backman’s most recent release is My Friends (2025), a coming-of-age story that was the May 2025 Indie Next List top pick. He has one new title listed for 2026–27 with details not yet announced.

Sally Rooney — Author

The biggest literary news of the week: Rooney announced that a Hebrew translation of Intermezzo will be published by November Books, an Israeli publisher that supports Palestinian rights, does not operate in West Bank settlements, and receives no Israeli government funding — making it BDS-compliant in her view. The deal was announced alongside a Guardian interview with Palestinian-Irish activist Samir Eskanda. “I was, of course, never boycotting the Hebrew language or any language,” Rooney said. The move generated significant coverage in the Irish Times, Jerusalem Post, Haaretz, and Middle East Eye.

Lee Child — Author

The next Jack Reacher novel, Chain Reaction (Book 31, co-written with Andrew Child), releases October 20, 2026. It brings back Susan Turner — last seen in Never Go Back — in a D.C.-set thriller involving a secretive invitation. Lee Child himself is reportedly working on a new solo project with a possible Reacher connection, though he’s stayed coy. No specific news this past week.

Mark Bowden — Author

No major updates this week. Bowden, best known for Black Hawk Down, continues to write for Vanity Fair, The Atlantic, and other outlets. No new book announcements or notable articles surfaced in the past 7 days.

Chuck Klosterman — Author

Klosterman’s book Football — released January 20 and an instant bestseller — continues to circulate. The book reached #2 on the New York Times nonfiction list in February. In it, Klosterman argues football may be doomed, citing its TV/streaming dependency, parental hesitation about youth participation, and NIL economics. He toured with Nora Princiotti of The Ringer at launch. Quiet this specific week.

Tyler Cowen — Economist & Podcaster

A Fortune piece published May 22 — yesterday — featured Cowen’s keynote at the Sana AI summit at the New York Public Library. His argument: AI will not cause mass unemployment, but will “change most jobs.” The harder problem, he said, is the psychological, social, and institutional cost of adjustment. His Conversations with Tyler podcast continues with new episodes, and he appeared on the Washington Post podcast in February discussing why he’s still “bullish on America.”

Tana French — Author

French’s The Keeper — the conclusion to her Cal Hooper trilogy — published March 31 and became a New York Times bestseller. She gave a long interview to Cultured Mag discussing crime fiction’s literary range and the cultural codes of rural Ireland. “Mystery is not as limited as some people think when it comes to good writing, characterization, or theme,” she said. No specific news in the past 7 days, but the book remains widely reviewed.

Ben Mezrich — Author

Mezrich’s Checkmate: Genius, Lies, Ambition, and the Biggest Scandal in Chess — covering the Carlsen vs. Niemann cheating controversy and the rise of Chess.com — had its ebook out April 7 and its hardcover arriving June 2. The ebook has been among Amazon’s top-selling new chess titles. A Hollywood adaptation is in development for 2027.

Scott Galloway — Professor & Podcaster

Galloway announced a five-city national tour for Prof G Markets, starting May 27 at San Francisco’s Castro Theater, then Los Angeles (May 28), Miami (May 30), Chicago (June 1), and New York (June 2). The podcast was Apple’s #1 business show in Q4 2025. The tour comes as Vox Media — which distributes the podcast — is reported to be nearing a deal to sell its podcast network.

Malcolm Gladwell — Author

Gladwell announced The American Way of Killing, a new book exploring America’s gun violence epidemic, due September 29, 2026. It expands reporting from his Revisionist History podcast with new interviews and research. The audiobook will feature his trademark audio-first production. He also appeared on PBS NewsHour’s Settle In podcast, reflecting on The Tipping Point 25 years later.

Paige Bueckers — Basketball

Bueckers is off to a strong start in her WNBA sophomore season with the Dallas Wings, averaging 20.7 points and 5.0 assists through her first three games. This week she scored 24 points (11-19 FG) with three rebounds and four assists in a 99–89 win over the Chicago Sky. Earlier, her Wings edged Caitlin Clark’s Indiana Fever 107–104 in the season opener. The Wings are being talked about as potential playoff — and even title — contenders.
