Week ending Wednesday, June 3, 2026

This Week

Ben Mezrich's "Checkmate" landed in bookstores June 2 — a deep dive into the 2022 Magnus Carlsen-Hans Niemann chess cheating scandal — while Carlsen himself is simultaneously competing at Norway Chess 2026 in Oslo, making for a remarkable (and presumably awkward) week of parallel chess narratives. Meanwhile, Pope Leo XIV begins his apostolic journey to Spain on Friday to inaugurate the new Tower of Jesus Christ at the Sagrada Familia, which will make it the world's tallest church.

Updates

Montana Fouts — Softball Player

Fouts was named to the USA Softball Women's National Team 2026 Athlete Pool, one of 36 players who will compete at the WBSC World Cup Group Stage and 2026 USA Softball International Cup, and who are in consideration for the 2028 Olympics. Magnus Carlsen — Chess Player

Carlsen is mid-tournament at Norway Chess 2026 (May 25-June 5, Oslo). After a rough start — losing to Firouzja in Round 1 and suffering three losses in five rounds — he bounced back with a classical win over World Champion Gukesh Dommaraju in Round 4, then avenged the Firouzja loss in Round 6. Meanwhile, Ben Mezrich's "Checkmate," covering the 2022 Carlsen-Niemann cheating scandal, hit shelves this week with an A24 film adaptation (produced by Emma Stone, directed by Nathan Fielder) in the works.

John Grisham — Author

Grisham's next book drops June 9: "Shaken: The Rush to Execute an Innocent Man," a nonfiction account of Robert Roberson, a Texas man on death row for over twenty years. A second 2026 title — "The French Illusion," a globe-trotting espionage thriller about a honeymoon in Paris gone wrong — is scheduled for September 29.

Michael Lewis — Author

Lewis continues promoting "Who Is Government?: The Untold Story of Public Service," appearing on PBS NewsHour on May 4 and in a conversation with Robert Reich on Substack. The book spotlights essential federal workers — a pointed subject given ongoing federal workforce cuts in 2026.

Frederik Backman — Author
