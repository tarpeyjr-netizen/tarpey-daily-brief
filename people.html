Week ending Wednesday, June 9, 2026

This Week

The week's biggest splash came in publishing: John Grisham dropped Shaken — a nonfiction account of a wrongful death-penalty case — on June 9, the same week Ben Mezrich's chess-scandal hardcover Checkmate hit shelves and Malcolm Gladwell launched a new Obama-joined podcast series. On the court, Paige Bueckers recorded a franchise-record 14 assists in a single game.

Updates

John Grisham — Author

Grisham published Shaken: The Rush to Execute an Innocent Man on June 9, 2026 — his first nonfiction book in years. It chronicles the case of Robert Roberson, convicted in 2003 of murdering his 2-year-old daughter in a controversial Texas death-penalty case. A second book, the espionage thriller The French Illusion, is due September 29.

Michael Lewis — Author

Lewis's upcoming book Blockers (October 6, 2026) — an inside account of Trump's Department of Government Efficiency told through the public servants it targeted — continued to generate buzz. He appeared on the Acquired podcast's 10-year anniversary episode and revealed a verbal deal to eventually write a Sam Altman biography, but only once ChatGPT is capable of writing a competing draft.

Frederik Backman — Author

No major updates this week. His 2025 novel My Friends, which won a Goodreads Choice Award, remains in circulation. Backman has suggested it may be his final book.

Sally Rooney — Author

Rooney made international headlines in May by announcing she will publish a Hebrew translation of Intermezzo through November Books, a small Israeli publisher that endorses Palestinian rights and does not receive Israeli government funding — fulfilling her BDS-compatible publishing commitment. In a Guardian interview she said: "Though my refusal to work with complicit Israeli publishing houses made the contractual side of things more complex, I was, of course, never boycotting the Hebrew language or any language."

Lee Child — Author

Lee and Andrew Child announced Chain Reaction, the 31st Jack Reacher novel, due October 20, 2026. Set in Washington, D.C., it notably brings back Susan Turner — Reacher's former ally — for the first time since Never Go Back.

Mark Bowden — Author

No major updates this week. Bowden, best known for Black Hawk Down, continues to contribute to The Atlantic and Vanity Fair.

Chuck Klosterman — Author

Klosterman's essay collection Football — a wide-ranging cultural critique of the sport — has been making the podcast rounds since its January 2026 release. He appeared on NPR and The New York Times Book Review podcast arguing football remains a net positive for American society, "but it's close."

Tana French — Author

French's The Keeper (March 31, 2026), the conclusion to her Cal Hooper trilogy, continues to receive strong coverage. In an April Cultured Magazine interview she described it as Cal's final transition "from being a detective in detective terms to being this guy in Western terms, who provides the kind of justice and law that the community he's become part of is demanding."

Ben Mezrich — Author

Mezrich's Checkmate: Genius, Lies, Ambition, and the Biggest Scandal in Chess hit shelves in hardcover on June 2. The book covers the explosive Carlsen-Niemann cheating controversy from the inside. An A24 film adaptation is already in development, with Nathan Fielder set to direct and Emma Stone producing — potentially shooting this summer for a 2027 release.

Malcolm Gladwell — Author

Gladwell launched a new eight-part podcast series, Reconstruction: The Unfinished Promise, on June 4 — co-produced with Barack and Michelle Obama's Higher Ground and Audible. The series begins and ends with a direct conversation between Obama and Gladwell. His next book, The American Way of Killing (on gun violence in America), is due September 29.

Colm Tóibín — Author

Tóibín's first short story collection in 15 years, The News from Dublin (Scribner, March 31, 2026), has drawn strong critical attention. In a May Harvard Crimson profile he reflected: "Literary fiction is the only way we have, really, of seeing somebody thinking something and saying something else, and the distance between the concealment and revelation."

Thomas Friedman — Author

Friedman continued his role as a prominent foreign affairs commentator. He appeared on Fox News in April saying he feels "torn" about defeating Iran's regime if it politically strengthens Trump and Netanyahu. He also appeared on the Opinions NYT podcast discussing what he calls "the Polycene" — his term for a new geopolitical era of multipolar disorder.

Paige Bueckers — Basketball

Bueckers is having a breakout sophomore WNBA season with the Dallas Wings, averaging 18.3 points and 5.3 assists. On June 5, she had 18 points and a career-high 14 assists in a 104–96 win over the LA Sparks, tying a franchise record. Off the court, her debut-game jersey sold for $64,720 — the highest price ever for a WNBA jersey.

Robert Sapolsky — Biologist / Author

Sapolsky was elected to the National Academy of Sciences in 2026. He gave a May Big Think interview expanding on Determined: A Science of Life Without Free Will — specifically how early childhood experiences, cultural upbringing, and brain trauma shape unconscious decisions without our awareness.

He also flagged a new fraud threat: token thieves now account for one in six new Stripe customer signups, defrauding AI firms by signing up for accounts to steal compute credits.
