Next Steps

  • Contact landlords now even for September. Email each listing expressing strong interest and ask whether they would consider a September start date, or if a similar unit in the same development will be available in September.
  • Request a video viewing. Many Galway landlords will accommodate FaceTime/WhatsApp video tours for overseas applicants. Prepare a short introduction about yourself (grad student, program, income/funding source) to include in your first message.
  • Prepare a rental profile. Include: proof of enrollment or acceptance letter, funding confirmation (scholarship/stipend/loan), references from previous landlords or academic supervisors, and a government-issued ID. Irish landlords increasingly screen applicants given demand.
  • Understand the deposit process. Standard Irish deposits are 1–2 months’ rent. From abroad, wire transfer is typical. Confirm the landlord’s bank details via a secondary channel (phone call) before sending funds to avoid fraud.
  • Review the lease before signing. Ensure it specifies the exact start date, rent amount, deposit return conditions, and notice period. Irish tenancy law (Residential Tenancies Act) gives tenants strong protections after 6 months.
  • Set up Daft.ie alerts for Salthill, Newcastle, and Rahoon apartments with your price ceiling. September listings will begin appearing in July — being among the first to enquire is critical in Galway’s competitive market.
  • Check University of Galway’s accommodation office at universityofgalway.ie/accommodation for campus housing options and their off-campus noticeboard, which sometimes lists private rentals not on Daft.
