Newcastle offers the best combination of proximity and value for a single grad student — the studio on Newcastle Road (€925/mo) is a standout at 3 minutes' walk from both NUIG and University Hospital Galway. Salthill is the next best option with two apartments under the €1,800 target and a pleasant coastal lifestyle. Rahoon supply is currently limited to larger, pricier apartments, making it the least practical for a solo student on budget.

Next Steps

  • Request video viewings immediately for the Newcastle Road studio (€925) and Lower Salthill Road (€1,350) — these are the only sub-€1,500 options and will move fast.
  • Set up saved searches and email alerts on Daft.ie, Rent.ie, and Property.ie for all three areas to catch new listings as they post (September leases typically appear July–August).
  • Prepare a reference pack in advance: proof of enrollment / acceptance letter from University of Galway, employment/income proof of a guarantor, a short personal introduction (landlords often ask who will be living there and for how long).
  • Budget for a standard Irish deposit of 1–2 months' rent, paid upfront; international students may be asked for 2 months. Check if your bank supports fast SEPA transfers to Ireland.
  • Contact the University of Galway Accommodation Office at universityofgalway.ie/accommodation — they maintain a private rental listings board and can sometimes connect students with landlords before properties go public.
  • For short-term bridge housing (if you arrive before your lease starts), check NUIG's on-campus residences or Galway city centre B&Bs / serviced apartments for a 1–2 week stay.
