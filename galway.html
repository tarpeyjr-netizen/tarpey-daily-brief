Newcastle offers the best combination of proximity and affordability for a single grad student, anchored by a €925 studio and a €1,450 one-bed both within a 5-minute walk of campus. Salthill has more character (seaside, promenade) and a couple of solid budget 1-bed/studio options under €1,800, but sits roughly twice as far from campus. Rahoon currently has the weakest fit for a single student on a budget — nothing under €2,200 was found active, and most units are larger 2–3 bed apartments better suited to sharers.

Next Steps

  • Request video viewings for any Rahoon or Newcastle listings before travel, since several agents note "email enquiries only" or "daft enquiries only."
  • Set up email alerts on Daft.ie, Rent.ie, and Property.ie for Salthill, Newcastle, and Rahoon apartments – September listings typically post 4–8 weeks ahead, so new supply should appear through late July and August.
  • Prepare a deposit and first month's rent (commonly €2,000–€4,600 combined depending on the unit) and confirm whether landlords accept international bank transfers.
  • Put together a reference pack: prior landlord reference, proof of income/scholarship funding, photo ID, and a short personal introduction email, since several listings request an "expression of interest" before offering a viewing.
  • Contact the University of Galway Accommodation Office (accommodation@universityofgalway.ie) for verified off-campus listings and advice for international/graduate students.
