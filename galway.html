Next Steps

  • Request video viewings immediately: Email each landlord through Daft.ie explaining you are an international grad student starting in September 2026. Ask for a WhatsApp or Zoom video tour. Many Galway landlords will accommodate remote viewing for international students.
  • Set Daft.ie alerts now: Create saved searches on Daft.ie for Salthill, Newcastle, and Rahoon apartments with your budget filter. Alerts are sent by email the moment a new listing is posted — critical in a tight market where good properties lease within 24–48 hours of listing.
  • Ask about September availability directly: Even if a listing says “available now,” email the landlord and ask if they would consider a September 1 start date — some landlords prefer a later start if current tenants are staying through summer.
  • Understand the deposit and lease process: Standard Irish deposits equal one month’s rent, paid before move-in. Request a draft lease (RTB-compliant tenancy agreement) before sending any money. Deposits and rent can be transferred internationally via SEPA bank transfer.
  • Prepare your reference pack early: Irish landlords require: university acceptance letter, proof of funding/income or guarantor, photo ID (passport), and a reference from a prior landlord or employer. Have a PDF folder ready to attach to your first enquiry email.
  • Contact University of Galway accommodation office: Email accommodation@universityofgalway.ie — they maintain a vetted landlord list and can advise on tenant rights, typical lease terms, and student-specific housing options including on-campus accommodation.
  • Consider house-sharing as a backup: If budget is a hard constraint, rooms in Salthill or Newcastle house-shares typically run €700–€950/month (search Daft.ie > Sharing > Galway). This is significantly cheaper and can be secured with a shorter lead time than a full apartment lease.
