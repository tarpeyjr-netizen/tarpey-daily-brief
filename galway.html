Next Steps

  • Contact landlords now for the listings within or closest to budget — explain you are an incoming graduate student and ask whether they would consider holding a lease start date of September 1, 2026 (or negotiate an August 15 start with 2 months at reduced rate).
  • Request a video viewing via WhatsApp or Zoom — most Galway landlords are accustomed to this for international/remote applicants. Ask for a walkthrough showing all rooms, storage, heating controls, and the building entrance.
  • Prepare a renter profile in advance: a one-page PDF with your name, University of Galway acceptance letter/enrollment confirmation, home country references, bank statement showing funds, and contact details. Landlords respond much faster to prepared applicants.
  • Understand the Irish deposit process: standard deposit is typically 1 month's rent, paid by bank transfer. For international students, some landlords accept a larger deposit (2 months) in lieu of Irish bank references. Revolut or Wise accounts are widely accepted.
  • Sign a lease before arriving: ask for a draft lease agreement (AST or standard Irish tenancy agreement) to review before signing electronically. Confirm it complies with Residential Tenancies Board (RTB) standards.
  • Check Daft.ie again in late July/August: September-specific listings (especially student-targeted) will appear 4–8 weeks before the academic year. Set up Daft email alerts for Salthill, Newcastle, and Rahoon filtered to €1,800 max.
  • Explore University of Galway student accommodation as a fallback: the university offers on-campus housing and has a housing office that assists postgraduate students — contact them at accommodation@universityofgalway.ie.
  • Consider room-sharing: Daft.ie's "Sharing" section often has budget-friendly rooms in shared houses in all three neighborhoods for €700–€1,100/mo — a strong budget option while waiting for the right apartment.
