🚨 Raleigh moves toward teen curfew as more arrests surface from July 4 chaos

Raleigh City Council is pushing ahead with plans for a citywide teen curfew after "teen takeover" gatherings that officials say drew as many as 8,000 young people to Brier Creek and Glenwood South over July 4 weekend, triggering multiple shootings and injuries. Police Chief Rico Boyce told council members the department is drafting curfew language covering minors 17 and under, along with possible backpack restrictions and expanded youth programs; a formal proposal is expected Aug. 18. A review of Wake County arrest records found more than 23 adults were arrested in and around Glenwood South between midnight and 3 a.m. the Sunday after the unrest, on charges including resisting arrest and carrying a concealed weapon. Mayor Janet Cowell said the city attorney has been directed to draft curfew language, and officials said a full breakdown of arrests will be released soon.

🎓 UNC clears Kenan Stadium after bomb threat prompts evacuation

UNC Campus Police determined there was no credible threat at Kenan Stadium in Chapel Hill Thursday afternoon after a 911 call around 3:11 p.m. reported a bomb threat involving the stadium. Police evacuated the stadium out of caution and asked Bell Tower-area employees to move their cars and leave the vicinity while K-9 units searched the stadium, including the bleachers. Emergency responders followed established safety protocols during the search before clearing the scene.

🏛️ State Health Plan overhauls provider tiers and premiums for 750,000 members

The State Health Plan Board of Trustees approved sweeping changes Friday to coverage for hundreds of thousands of state employees, retirees and dependents, introducing four provider tiers — Preferred, Access, Non-Preferred and Out-of-Network — with costs varying by where members seek care. UNC Health and Novant Health were named Preferred providers, meaning members who use those systems starting Jan. 1, 2027, get the lowest costs; WakeMed and Duke Health are still being considered for Access-tier status in the Triangle but won't qualify for Preferred status. The board also approved premium increases — individual coverage rises from $1.76 to $8.05 a month — and picked Blue Cross NC as the new pharmacy benefit manager and third-party administrator starting in 2028, a shift state Treasurer Brad Briner said should save more than $1 billion over the contract term.

🌡️ Exceptional drought grips Orange County despite recent rain

Much of Orange County remains in exceptional drought, the highest category on the U.S. Drought Monitor, according to a map released Thursday, even after recent rainfall offered some relief elsewhere in the region. The Eno River is drying, and residents describe dying trees and fire concerns they've never had before in North Carolina. Chapel Hill and Carrboro's utility, OWASA, says its reservoirs — fed by University Lake, Cane Creek and the Quarry Reservoir — are about 73% full, just 3 points above the threshold that would trigger formal water restrictions. Local farms report shortened growing seasons and reduced yields, while roughly 40% of county residents on private wells say their supply remains unaffected so far.

🚌 GoCary suspends Downtown Loop bus service amid driver shortage

The town of Cary suspended GoCary's Downtown Loop bus route indefinitely starting Thursday, citing an ongoing shortage of drivers. Most stops previously served by the loop will now be covered by other GoCary fixed routes, and riders can find updated route maps through the GoCary app. "We appreciate our riders' patience and understanding as we make this service adjustment," said Cary transit director Kelly Blazey, adding the change is meant to help the agency maintain reliable service across the rest of its transit system.

💡 Vehicle strikes knock out power to 3,000+ Durham customers

More than 3,000 Duke Energy customers in east Durham lost power Wednesday morning after two separate vehicles struck utility poles and lines, including a dump truck that became tangled in power lines and pulled down a pole. The outages delayed the opening of Durham City Hall as crews worked to make repairs. Duke Energy restored power to all affected customers by 5:20 p.m. that evening.

🚨 Raleigh weighs teen curfew after violent July 4th "takeovers"

Raleigh leaders are drafting a citywide curfew for residents 17 and younger after large, unpermitted "teen takeover" gatherings in Brier Creek and Glenwood South over the July 4th weekend turned violent, with nine people injured in related shootings. A 16-year-old is now in custody, facing felony riot, unlawful firearm possession by a minor, and discharging a weapon within city limits, according to ABC11. Police, city leaders, nonprofits and youth program representatives met this week to discuss connecting teens with safe spaces, and WUNC reports a draft curfew proposal is headed to Raleigh City Council on Aug. 18. Some teens pushed back on the idea at a community meeting, arguing a curfew punishes the wrong group, per Spectrum News.

🏛️ Stein signs $34 billion NC budget after year-long stalemate

Gov. Josh Stein signed North Carolina's $34.3 billion state budget into law Tuesday, ending a standoff between House and Senate Republicans that had left the state without a full spending plan for more than 1,000 days, NC Newsline reports. The package includes historic pay raises for teachers and law enforcement, more than $1 billion in added Medicaid funding, and $700 million for Hurricane Helene recovery, according to a release from the governor's office. Stein objected to provisions cutting his board-appointment powers and lowering the personal income tax rate to 3.49% by 2027, and told reporters a lawsuit remains on the table.

🌡️ Storms ease drought as heat wave lingers across the Triangle

Several inches of rain fell across Raleigh and Durham this week, and ABC11's drought tracker shows most of Wake and Franklin counties improving a full category, from exceptional to extreme drought. The rain brought its own hazards: CBS17 reports lightning strikes sparked two house fires on Althorp Drive in Raleigh Wednesday evening. Despite the rain, triple-digit heat has persisted into this week, and Wake County's temporary cooling stations will stay open through at least Saturday, per ABC11's heat tracker.

🚨 Raleigh moves toward youth curfew after violent teen takeovers

The Raleigh City Council voted Tuesday to have city attorneys begin drafting a citywide curfew for residents 17 and younger, responding to a chaotic Fourth of July weekend in which an estimated 8,000 young people converged on Brier Creek and Glenwood South. Nine people were shot across the two "teen takeovers" and a related fight, and police made 29 arrests and recovered 11 firearms. A 16-year-old has been charged with felony riot and firearm offenses, and some teens are pushing back on the proposed curfew. Council members also floated backpack restrictions and expanded youth programs; a formal vote on the ordinance isn't expected until the council reconvenes in mid-August.

🌡️ Severe drought tightens grip as Raleigh starts fining water wasters

Central North Carolina remains in severe-to-exceptional drought, and Falls Lake — the primary drinking-water source for more than 650,000 Wake County residents — is now 5.9 feet below normal, with supply storage down to 62% and dropping 2-3% a week. The city has begun issuing fines to residents violating Stage 1 water restrictions, and the city council has given the city manager authority to move into Stage 2 restrictions early if compliance doesn't improve. Councilman Mitchell Silver warned Monday there's "a sense of urgency" needed now, evoking memories of the 2007-2009 drought crisis. Neighboring Burlington announced voluntary conservation measures this week as a precaution.

⛈️ Storms knock out power for thousands, spark house fires

A line of strong storms swept through the Triangle Wednesday evening, knocking out power for more than 3,000 customers in Wake County and 6,453 in Durham County at the peak of the outages. In Raleigh, lightning struck two homes in the 3500 block of Althorp Drive, sparking house fires that fire crews were able to knock down. Holly Springs saw hail and a lightning-caused electrical issue on Thorndale Drive. The storms offered only brief relief from the region's ongoing drought before conditions turned dry and hot again.

🚨 12-year-old girl dies after Raleigh Beach swimming incident

The Wake County Sheriff's Office confirmed a 12-year-old girl died from injuries suffered Tuesday at Raleigh Beach, a popular swimming and kayaking stretch of the Neuse River, after witnesses said she went underwater and didn't resurface around 11:30 a.m. Sheriff Willie Rowe extended condolences to the family. The site has a troubled safety history — at least four people drowned there in 2024 — prompting the city to add warning signs and a vehicle-access gate, measures that weren't enough to prevent this week's tragedy.

🚨 Raleigh moves toward teen curfew after July 4 chaos

Raleigh's mayor and police chief threw their support behind a proposed curfew for teenagers after a chaotic Fourth of July weekend saw roughly 3,000 teens converge on Brier Creek and 5,000 more on Glenwood South, with gunfire breaking out at both scenes. Mayor Janet Cowell said Tuesday she expects the City Council to vote on a curfew running from 11 p.m. to 6 a.m. at its Aug. 18 meeting, after Police Chief Rico Boyce described the scene to council members. A companion ordinance could also put parents on the hook if their children are caught violating it, and city leaders have pointed to curfews already in place in Charlotte and Winston-Salem as models. One Raleigh teen was arrested and charged with felony riot and firearm offenses tied to the Glenwood South unrest.

🚨 Husband and wife killed as crash flips RDU shuttle bus

A married couple died after a car crash flipped an RDU airport shuttle bus on Glenwood Avenue on July 4th, with police saying the driver of the car is now facing felony charges. Four other people were injured in the crash, two of them critically, according to initial reporting from the scene. The wreck happened the same holiday weekend that drew heavy police attention to Glenwood South for youth crowds and gunfire, adding to a rough stretch for the corridor.

🏛️ Durham launches three-day summit to curb gun violence

Durham kicked off its "Summit on Saving Lives" this week, a data-driven push to address gun violence following a six-month analysis of shootings and homicides in the city. Facilitated by violence-reduction researcher Thomas Abt, the summit comes after 94 shootings in Durham so far this year, including 13 involving victims under 18, with city data showing more than half of homicides tied to gang involvement. Organizers say participants will hand city and county leaders a set of recommendations at the summit's close, and officials will then have 60 days to act on them.

🌡️ Storms dump rain on parched Triangle, but spark house explosions

Heavy storms rolled through central North Carolina Tuesday, dropping more than 3 inches of rain in parts of Wake County and offering some relief to a region where Durham remains in an exceptional drought. But the same storms brought trouble: lightning strikes ignited gas and propane lines at two separate Wake County homes, triggering explosions, though no injuries were reported in either incident. Even with the rain, Cary, Apex, and Morrisville asked residents to keep using water wisely as the drought and summer demand continue to strain supplies.
