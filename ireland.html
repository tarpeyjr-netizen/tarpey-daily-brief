Week of Sat 12 to Sat 18, 2026

Politics

  • The Dáil rose for summer recess after a final round of Leaders' Questions and statements on the cost of a united Ireland; Taoiseach Micheál Martin bid the chamber farewell until autumn.
  • Martin said he is "leaning towards" a full ban on e-scooters after a string of deaths and serious injuries prompted debate in the Dáil.
  • Minister for Justice Jim O'Callaghan is holding firm in a dispute over criminal legal aid solicitor fees, with more than 100 solicitors threatening to resign from the panel.
  • Figures given to the Public Accounts Committee showed the State paid over €1 million for a single deportation flight returning 42 people to South Africa in June.
  • Sinn Féin has opened disciplinary proceedings against a member who publicly criticised the party's selection of Galway West candidate Mark Lohan.

Economics

  • CSO figures show the domestic economy grew 4.7% in 2025 on the modified domestic demand measure, with GDP up 8%.
  • The economy contracted 7.1% in the first quarter of 2026 as a tariff-driven export surge from late 2025 unwound.
  • PwC expects growth to settle at a "steadier" pace for the rest of 2026 as global risks weigh on the outlook.
  • The Central Bank raised its inflation forecasts through 2028, citing energy and food price pressure tied to Middle East volatility since the US-Israeli strikes on Iran.

Military / Defence Forces

  • Ireland is buying hundreds of armoured vehicles and artillery from France in a deal worth up to €600 million, the biggest Army equipment investment in the history of the State.
  • Ukraine has rejected an Irish offer of 27 mothballed light tactical armoured vehicles; they may now be scrapped.
  • 344 Irish troops are training ahead of deployment to Lebanon with UNIFIL, described as the end of an era as the mission's mandate winds down next year.
  • Minister for Defence Helen McEntee says anti-drone technology will be in place by next summer, with a radar system to follow by 2028.

Technology

  • Intel is investing €5 billion in its Leixlip site to meet AI chip demand, adding to roughly €30 billion invested there over 35 years and creating several hundred new permanent roles.
  • The Irish unit of US software firm PTC paid just $151,000 in tax after pre-tax profit soared to $240 million.
  • A survey found 70% of US multinationals' headquarters plan further Irish investment over five years, with two-thirds planning to boost AI and automation spend.

Sports

  • Galway name an unchanged team for Sunday's All-Ireland hurling final against Limerick, who recall captain Cian Lynch.
  • Galway face Dublin in the All-Ireland football quarter-finals this weekend, while Kerry meet Tyrone in the other tie.

Music

  • Forest Fest runs this week with Irish acts including The Boomtown Rats, The Undertones, Dea Matrona and Something Happens alongside international headliners.
  • The Forest Fleadh stage features Dervish, Kíla, Cherish the Ladies and The Henry Girls.
  • Last week's event guide flagged Dermot Kennedy's gig and the Galway arts festival among the best things to catch.

Books

  • The Society of Authors' Awards shortlists, featuring several Irish authors, were announced at a ceremony in Merrion Square with International Literature Festival Dublin.

Galway

  • Just over 500 new homes have commenced in Galway city and county so far this year — 133 in the city and 373 in the county, up 60% on last year.

🔥 Trending in Ireland
Pale Harbour
Music · Enniscorthy, Co. Wexford

Pale Harbour, a band from Enniscorthy, have become one of the most talked-about names in Ireland's indie rock scene after recording their debut LP in a converted shed outside town and releasing it on Faction Records, a small Dublin imprint that has quietly become the most-watched label in the country. The record's DIY origin story — homemade, low-budget, made far from any major studio — has resonated with listeners tired of over-produced pop, and it has since picked up coverage in the UK and continental European press. Faction's willingness to back unproven acts is part of a wider pattern this year: a genuine cluster of small Irish bands rising together rather than a single manufactured breakout, which is part of why the momentum feels organic rather than industry-engineered.

Week of Sat 04 to Sat 11, 2026

Politics

Economics

  • The Irish economy contracted 7.1% in Q1 2026 as last year's pharma export front-loading ahead of US tariffs unwound.
  • Despite the headline GDP drop, modified domestic demand grew 0.3%, with construction up 7.2% and real estate up 4.9%.
  • Government tax revenue to end of May reached almost €39 billion, up 6% year on year, with half-year exchequer figures due this week.

Military / Defence Forces

  • Ireland is finalising a deal worth up to €600m for French armoured vehicles, the biggest Army equipment investment in the history of the State.
  • A €1.7 billion Defence Forces development plan is now in motion, a 55% increase on the previous plan.
  • The UNIFIL mission in Lebanon ends December 31; the 128th Infantry Battalion's deployment marks the close of an almost 50-year commitment.
  • Anti-drone technology is due in place by next summer to protect against threats ahead of a major EU meeting in Dublin.

Technology

🔥 Trending in Ireland
The Belle Brush
Beauty / Fashion · Galway

The Belle Brush, a hairbrush and beauty-tool line founded in Galway back in 2017, has seen its reach explode since the company leaned into TikTok in early 2025, riding a broader wave of Irish shoppers rediscovering homegrown brands. It's part of a genuine grassroots shift: the #ShopIrish hashtag has doubled on the platform over the past year as small Irish makers, from jewellery to beauty tools, get discovered and championed by ordinary users rather than celebrity endorsements. Affiliate creators and word-of-mouth demos — not paid ads — have driven much of the brush's growth, echoing the same organic momentum that sent Galway jewellery label Betty and Biddy's Claddagh collection viral this year.
