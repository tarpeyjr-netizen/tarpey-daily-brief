Week of Sat 04 to Sat 11, 2026

Politics

  • Sinn Féin and Government are on completely different timetables for Irish unity, according to the Irish Times's Inside Politics podcast.
  • Leo Varadkar remarked that the old Dáil culture of political rivals arguing then having a drink together has faded from Irish political life.
  • A new report flagged "worsening conditions" in Irish prisons that have triggered national and international concern.
  • Legislation removing the Triple Lock on overseas troop deployments is expected to pass by year end, despite opposition warnings over neutrality.

Economics

  • The Irish economy contracted 7.1% in Q1 2026 as last year's pharma export front-loading ahead of US tariffs unwound.
  • Despite the headline GDP drop, modified domestic demand grew 0.3%, with construction up 7.2% and real estate up 4.9%.
  • Government tax revenue to end of May reached almost €39 billion, up 6% year on year, with half-year exchequer figures due this week.

Military / Defence Forces

  • Ireland is finalising a deal worth up to €600m for French armoured vehicles, the biggest Army equipment investment in the history of the State.
  • A €1.7 billion Defence Forces development plan is now in motion, a 55% increase on the previous plan.
  • The UNIFIL mission in Lebanon ends December 31; the 128th Infantry Battalion's deployment marks the close of an almost 50-year commitment.
  • Anti-drone technology is due in place by next summer to protect against threats ahead of a major EU meeting in Dublin.

Technology

  • IDA Ireland backed 190 investments in the first half of 2026, with the potential to support 10,400 new jobs.
  • Recent wins include Novo Nordisk's €432m Athlone expansion, Qualcomm's €125m AI-focused investment in Cork, and OpenText's €105m plan for 400 new jobs.
  • Ireland hosts 16 of the world's top 20 tech firms and is emerging as Europe's leading AI investment hub, even as a domestic AI readiness gap persists.

Sports

Music

Books

🔥 Trending in Ireland
The Belle Brush
Beauty / Fashion · Galway

The Belle Brush, a hairbrush and beauty-tool line founded in Galway back in 2017, has seen its reach explode since the company leaned into TikTok in early 2025, riding a broader wave of Irish shoppers rediscovering homegrown brands. It's part of a genuine grassroots shift: the #ShopIrish hashtag has doubled on the platform over the past year as small Irish makers, from jewellery to beauty tools, get discovered and championed by ordinary users rather than celebrity endorsements. Affiliate creators and word-of-mouth demos — not paid ads — have driven much of the brush's growth, echoing the same organic momentum that sent Galway jewellery label Betty and Biddy's Claddagh collection viral this year.

Betty and Biddy
Fashion / Jewellery · Ireland
