Week of Sat 30 May to Sat 6 Jun, 2026

🏛 Politics

  • Taoiseach Micheál Martin floated the possibility of a referendum to increase the number of Government ministers, citing serious maritime security challenges that he argued warranted a dedicated department.
  • Martin visited Budapest this week to pledge support for Hungary's new reform-minded PM Peter Magyar, ahead of Ireland taking over the EU Council Presidency on 1 July.
  • Sinn Féin and the Social Democrats are set to bring motions calling for a boycott of Israeli sporting matches when the Dáil resumes on 9 June, following Dáil passage of the Occupied Territories Bill.
  • The Galway West bye-election concluded last week with Fine Gael's candidate Thomas leading; analysts are already eyeing the seat's implications for the next general election.

💶 Economics

  • Bank of Ireland forecasts no recession but warns of slowing growth and elevated inflation averaging around 4% for 2026, with real wage growth likely wiped out by rising prices.
  • AIB economists warned that Irish inflation could hit 6–7% by year-end if the Strait of Hormuz blockade continues to push up commodity prices.
  • The IMF cautioned that AI could affect more than 40% of all Irish jobs, with Ireland more exposed than most countries due to its concentration of tech and financial services multinationals.
  • Property asking-price inflation hit its lowest rate in over two years, with new home completions forecast to reach 39,000 units in 2026, a sign the housing market is gradually easing.

🪖 Military / Defence Forces

  • Ireland's first National Maritime Security Strategy is now in place, committing the state to deeper cooperation with the UK, France, and links to the UK-led Joint Expeditionary Force (JEF) while maintaining military neutrality.
  • Defence spending for 2026 is up 11% on the previous year, funding new sonar and radar systems, replenished ammunition stocks, body armour, and a new uniform for the Defence Forces as part of the plan to increase spending by 55% by 2030.
  • Ireland is not seeking NATO membership but is deepening structured defence cooperation with European partners through EU frameworks and the JEF partnership arrangement.

💻 Technology

  • Tech employment rose at its fastest rate in a year in May, even as Meta cut approximately 350 Irish jobs — about 20% of its Irish workforce — as part of global AI-driven restructuring.
  • Analysts say the tech jobs market is more nuanced than headlines suggest, with technology, media and telecoms posting the highest PMI of any sector at 54.1 in May.
  • Cork's Tyndall National Institute is targeting ten new company spin-outs from its research pipeline this year, aiming to commercialise Irish-developed technology across semiconductors and photonics.
  • Three quarters of Irish companies are now actively using AI tools, according to a recent survey, with Ireland ranked first globally by the IMF for AI workforce readiness.

  • Galway United sit three points clear at the top of the League of Ireland following their win over Athlone Town, with the league resuming on June 20 for round nine.
  • The Connacht Backyard Ultra was hosted by Galway Trail Running over the June bank holiday weekend, while the Connacht Juvenile Track and Field Championships opened on June 6 in Claremorris.

Week of Sat 23 to Sat 30 May, 2026

🏛 Politics

  • Fine Gael and the Social Democrats each won a Dáil seat in last week's bye-elections — Seán Kyne took Galway West for Fine Gael (the first government party to win a bye-election since 2014) and Daniel Ennis won Dublin Central for the Social Democrats.
  • Parties assessed the bye-election fallout this week: Sinn Féin failed to win either seat, while Independent Ireland's Noel Thomas polled a striking 20.8% in Galway West, nearly doubling his vote.
  • Fianna Fáil faces internal reckoning after a dismal performance in both constituencies; analysts note a leadership change during Ireland's upcoming EU Council presidency would be impractical.
  • A Garda "exceptional event" declaration remains in effect following fuel-price protests driven by surging home heating oil costs, up over 80% in the past year.

💶 Economics

  • Finance Minister Harris warned that Ireland's 2026 inflation rate could reach 3.75%, after consumer prices rose 3.7% in the year to April — the highest rate since early 2024, driven largely by energy costs.
  • AIB cautioned that a sustained Strait of Hormuz blockade could push inflation to a 7% peak this year; its base-case forecast stands at 4%.
  • The EU Commission forecast Irish GDP to fall 1.2% in 2026, though mainly due to a 2025 pharmaceutical export pull-forward effect, while EY projects domestic growth of 2.7%.
  • The IMF warned Ireland is "relatively more exposed" than most advanced economies to AI-driven disruption, with over 40% of all jobs at potential risk.

🪖 Military / Defence Forces

  • The Defence Forces is ramping up counter-drone capabilities ahead of Ireland's EU Council presidency, including AI-powered long-range drones and Bushmaster-fitted APCs receiving anti-drone ammunition.
  • An information security concern emerged after the EU published online the names of dozens of Defence Forces members, including intelligence and counter-drug personnel, prompting safety fears.
  • Troops remain "frustrated" after the government refused to pay €3.7 million in owed allowances despite an agreed negotiation outcome.
  • AI systems are being deployed to compensate for personnel shortfalls — including monitoring Russian shadow-fleet vessels (estimated 500 ships) transiting Irish-controlled waters.

💻 Technology

  • AI-driven job cuts at multinationals are threatening Ireland's public finances; Meta is set to cut ~8,000 jobs globally (some from Ireland), while Cisco and Oracle are also trimming Irish workforces.
  • The Dáil was told Ireland's economy is "dangerously dependent" on a handful of tech giants and that the government is reluctant to challenge them for fear of losing investment.
  • Irish automation firm Tines raised $125 million, achieving unicorn status after doubling revenue to $39.6m in the year to January 2025.
  • Ireland risks losing data centre investment to other locations, with analysts warning this could cascade into broader shifts of key tech activities — and a hit to corporation tax revenues.

🏐 Sports

  • Galway hurlers staged a remarkable comeback against Wexford in the Leinster Senior Hurling Championship on May 24, overturning a 13-point deficit to win by eight points.
  • In club GAA football, Corofin beat Salthill-Knocknacarra 2-15 to 2-12, Moycullen defeated Milltown 2-18 to 2-12, and Naomh Anna (Leitir Mór) overcame Oughterard 0-19 to 2-7 in the Division 1 Football League.
  • Tuam Stars edged out Claregalway 1-12 to 0-16 in a tight Division 1 league clash last weekend.

🎵 Music

  • New Irish releases this month also include albums from Goodtime John, Molly O'Mahony, Tony St Ledger, and Mac Giolla Ghunna, reviewed in the Irish Times.
  • Summer 2026 Irish festival season kicks off with CMAT, Fontaines D.C., and The Cure all headlining; Forbidden Fruit and Electric Picnic are among the major events on the calendar.

📚 Books

  • Ali Smith won the Dublin Literary Award (€100,000) for her novel Gliff; Irish nominees Sally Rooney (Intermezzo) and Niamh Ní Mhaoileoin (Ordinary Saints) were longlisted.
