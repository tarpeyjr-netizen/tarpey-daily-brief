Week of Sat 17 to Sat 23, 2026

Politics

  • Bye-elections were held on May 22 in Dublin Central and Galway West; Social Democrats' Daniel Ennis won Dublin Central, putting the party ahead of Labour in the Dáil, while Fine Gael's Seán Kyne took Galway West. Sinn Féin failed to gain from public discontent over the cost of living despite raising it regularly in the Dáil.
  • Analysis highlighted five key takeaways from the results — with Fine Gael and Social Democrats the chief beneficiaries and the independent Ireland party also showing rising poll numbers in recent weeks.
  • Minister for Communications Patrick O'Donovan said Ireland will "go it alone" to ban social media for under-16s if no EU-wide agreement is reached, and will seek Government approval to act nationally.

Economics

  • The EU Commission forecast Irish GDP to fall 1.2% in 2026 due to frontloading of pharmaceutical exports in 2025, though it expects a recovery to +3.4% in 2027. EY separately forecasts 2.7% domestic growth this year.
  • The NTMA warned Ireland's national debt could approach €250 billion by the 2030s, cautioning that the era of low borrowing costs is over and that rising debt levels carry real risk.
  • CSO data showed migrant workers accounted for 61% of Ireland's jobs growth between 2019 and 2024, representing 218,000 of 355,000 new jobs as total employment reached approximately 2.8 million.

Military / Defence Forces

  • The Defence Forces is deploying AI to monitor Russian shadow-fleet vessels — an estimated 500 ships — passing through Irish-controlled waters, and has funding for long-range AI-powered drones to detect subsea cable threats.
  • Minister Helen McEntee announced a government-to-government deal with France worth ~€600 million to purchase hundreds of armoured vehicles under France's Scorpion Programme, upgrading the Defence Forces' fleet.
  • An Irish Army Cavalry Corps crew beat six US military teams in a Bradley armoured vehicle competition — a remarkable result given Ireland does not operate tracked vehicles.
  • The 128th Infantry Battalion is completing final training ahead of deployment to the UN Interim Force in Lebanon (UNIFIL).

Technology

  • Meta announced 350 Irish job cuts — 20% of its local workforce, disproportionately higher than its global 10% target, following Oracle's 15% cut in Ireland. The ICT sector has shed over 20,000 jobs in the past year.
  • Dublin Tech Week (May 22–29) is underway with 30+ events spanning AI, cybersecurity, fintech, and smart cities, anchored by the Dublin Tech Summit.
  • Medtronic is creating 85 new jobs in Galway with a software development hub, a rare bright spot amid widespread tech-sector redundancies nationally.

Sports

  • Galway outclassed Kildare at Pearse Stadium in the opening round of the All-Ireland SFC, with Rob Finnerty particularly impressive in a dominant team display.
  • Kerry claimed the TG4 Munster SFC title for the third successive year, edging out a spirited Waterford side by one point at Kilmallock.
  • The All-Ireland series and Tailteann Cup are both underway, with Irish-language commentary on approximately 35 fixtures this summer on RTÉ Player and RTÉ News channel.

Music

  • CMAT won Best Album at the Ivor Novello Awards for her Euro-Country record — "It's nice to finally win an award in England," she quipped, also taking the moment to call out Bertie Ahern's immigration remarks.
  • RTÉ 2FM launched Sessions from Oblivion, a 10-part series featuring 20 new Irish acts recording live at Whelan's, Galway's Róisín Dubh, and Oblivion Studios.
  • New Irish albums reviewed this week include Mick Flannery's Man on a Bridge and Molly O'Mahony's Waiting on the World, along with releases from Goodtime John and Pól Mac Giolla Ghunna.

Books

  • Ali Smith won the 2026 Dublin Literary Award — worth €100,000 and the world's largest prize for a single English-language novel — for her book Gliff, in the award's 31st year.
  • The Children's Books Ireland Awards were announced, with Kevin Moran's debut The Doomsday Club taking the Eilís Dillon Award and six prizes distributed across fiction, illustration, and non-fiction at a ceremony in Merrion Square.
  • Fiona McCann's debut novel Love & Other Liabilities — set on a west Cork island — won the Joan Hessayon Award for New Writers from the Romantic Novelists' Association.

Galway

  • Fine Gael's Seán Kyne won the Galway West by-election, called after independent TD Catherine Connolly was elected President of Ireland; 17 candidates contested the seat with a turnout of approximately 44%.
  • Over 500 new homes have commenced in Galway city and county in 2026 to date, with county commencements up 60% on the same period last year.
  • Galway's footballers began their All-Ireland SFC campaign with a commanding win over Kildare at Pearse Stadium, and Medtronic announced 85 new software jobs at their Galway hub.

Kilkelly / East Mayo

  • An East Mayo councillor called for additional funding for pedestrian footpaths in Swinford and Charlestown after several residents reported falls on deteriorating surfaces — the issue has been raised at Claremorris-Swinford Municipal District level.
  • Swinford community notes: local sports clubs including Swinford GAA and Swinford Killasser LGFA continue preparations for the summer season; Mayo Fleadh Cheoil Mhaigh Eo 2026 entries are open via the Mayo Comhaltas website.
  • No major breaking news from Kilkelly parish this week; the next scheduled community events are expected through June.

Tarpey News

  • John Tarpey received the Best Contribution to Heritage Award at the 2026 Cathaoirleach's Awards in Galway, recognised for founding the Kilskeagh Heritage Committee, which uncovered and restored a historic medieval church and graveyard that had been hidden by overgrowth for decades.
  • Tarpey & Associates (Ardrahan, Co. Galway) and Brendan Tarpey Plumbing & Heating remain active in the county; the Liam Tarpey Band has 2026 tour dates listed on Ents24.
