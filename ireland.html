Week of Sat 23 to Sat 30 May, 2026

🏛 Politics

  • Fine Gael and the Social Democrats each won a Dáil seat in last week's bye-elections — Seán Kyne took Galway West for Fine Gael (the first government party to win a bye-election since 2014) and Daniel Ennis won Dublin Central for the Social Democrats.
  • Parties assessed the bye-election fallout this week: Sinn Féin failed to win either seat, while Independent Ireland's Noel Thomas polled a striking 20.8% in Galway West, nearly doubling his vote.
  • Fianna Fáil faces internal reckoning after a dismal performance in both constituencies; analysts note a leadership change during Ireland's upcoming EU Council presidency would be impractical.
  • A Garda "exceptional event" declaration remains in effect following fuel-price protests driven by surging home heating oil costs, up over 80% in the past year.

💶 Economics

  • Finance Minister Harris warned that Ireland's 2026 inflation rate could reach 3.75%, after consumer prices rose 3.7% in the year to April — the highest rate since early 2024, driven largely by energy costs.
  • AIB cautioned that a sustained Strait of Hormuz blockade could push inflation to a 7% peak this year; its base-case forecast stands at 4%.
  • The EU Commission forecast Irish GDP to fall 1.2% in 2026, though mainly due to a 2025 pharmaceutical export pull-forward effect, while EY projects domestic growth of 2.7%.
  • The IMF warned Ireland is "relatively more exposed" than most advanced economies to AI-driven disruption, with over 40% of all jobs at potential risk.

🪖 Military / Defence Forces

  • The Defence Forces is ramping up counter-drone capabilities ahead of Ireland's EU Council presidency, including AI-powered long-range drones and Bushmaster-fitted APCs receiving anti-drone ammunition.
  • An information security concern emerged after the EU published online the names of dozens of Defence Forces members, including intelligence and counter-drug personnel, prompting safety fears.
  • Troops remain "frustrated" after the government refused to pay €3.7 million in owed allowances despite an agreed negotiation outcome.
  • AI systems are being deployed to compensate for personnel shortfalls — including monitoring Russian shadow-fleet vessels (estimated 500 ships) transiting Irish-controlled waters.

💻 Technology

  • AI-driven job cuts at multinationals are threatening Ireland's public finances; Meta is set to cut ~8,000 jobs globally (some from Ireland), while Cisco and Oracle are also trimming Irish workforces.
  • The Dáil was told Ireland's economy is "dangerously dependent" on a handful of tech giants and that the government is reluctant to challenge them for fear of losing investment.
  • Irish automation firm Tines raised $125 million, achieving unicorn status after doubling revenue to $39.6m in the year to January 2025.
  • Ireland risks losing data centre investment to other locations, with analysts warning this could cascade into broader shifts of key tech activities — and a hit to corporation tax revenues.

🏐 Sports

  • Galway hurlers staged a remarkable comeback against Wexford in the Leinster Senior Hurling Championship on May 24, overturning a 13-point deficit to win by eight points.
  • In club GAA football, Corofin beat Salthill-Knocknacarra 2-15 to 2-12, Moycullen defeated Milltown 2-18 to 2-12, and Naomh Anna (Leitir Mór) overcame Oughterard 0-19 to 2-7 in the Division 1 Football League.
  • Tuam Stars edged out Claregalway 1-12 to 0-16 in a tight Division 1 league clash last weekend.

🎵 Music

  • Mick Flannery released a double album, The House Must Win, featuring newly recorded versions of tracks from his 2005 debut Evening Train alongside 10 original songs.
  • New Irish releases this month also include albums from Goodtime John, Molly O'Mahony, Tony St Ledger, and Mac Giolla Ghunna, reviewed in the Irish Times.
  • Summer 2026 Irish festival season kicks off with CMAT, Fontaines D.C., and The Cure all headlining; Forbidden Fruit and Electric Picnic are among the major events on the calendar.

📚 Books

  • Gráinne O'Hare won the Kerry Group Irish Novel of the Year (€20,000) at Listowel Writers' Week on May 29 for her novel Thirst Trap.
  • Ali Smith won the Dublin Literary Award (€100,000) for her novel Gliff; Irish nominees Sally Rooney (Intermezzo) and Niamh Ní Mhaoileoin (Ordinary Saints) were longlisted.
  • Fintan O'Toole and Sam McBride won the Christopher Ewart-Biggs Literary Prize (£7,500) for For and Against a United Ireland.

🌊 Galway

  • Fine Gael's Seán Kyne won the Galway West bye-election on May 24, though the 44% turnout was the lowest for a Connacht-Ulster by-election since 1980. Independent Ireland's Noel Thomas made a strong showing at nearly 21%.
  • The Galway City Ring Road was approved after decades of delays, a major development for the city's traffic infrastructure.
  • Five Galway music venues received funding under the 2026 Grassroots Venues Support Scheme: Róisín Dubh and Monroe's Live each get €20,000, as does The Cornerstone Loughrea, while Seven Bridgestreet gets €15,000 and Mary Mullens Bar €8,000.
  • A public consultation on a new joint economic strategy for Galway city and county is underway, aimed at shaping growth in job creation, enterprise and cluster development over the next decade.

🏘 Kilkelly / East Mayo

  • No specific Kilkelly parish news was found this week. Mayo GAA activity continues ahead of the summer provincial championship, with east Mayo clubs preparing for competitive fixtures in the coming weeks.
  • The east Mayo / Charlestown / Swinford area remains focused on the broader county football picture as Mayo look to challenge in Connacht this summer.

🔎 Tarpey News

  • Neil Tarpey continues in his role as Business Intelligence Manager at SIRO Ireland, the fibre broadband company.
  • David Tarpey of Connaught Property remains a noted figure in east Mayo property and land dealings, per current listings.
  • No other notable Tarpey-related news in Ireland was found this week.
Week of Sat 17 to Sat 23, 2026

Politics

  • Bye-elections were held on May 22 in Dublin Central and Galway West; Social Democrats' Daniel Ennis won Dublin Central, putting the party ahead of Labour in the Dáil, while Fine Gael's Seán Kyne took Galway West. Sinn Féin failed to gain from public discontent over the cost of living despite raising it regularly in the Dáil.
  • Analysis highlighted five key takeaways from the results — with Fine Gael and Social Democrats the chief beneficiaries and the independent Ireland party also showing rising poll numbers in recent weeks.
  • Minister for Communications Patrick O'Donovan said Ireland will "go it alone" to ban social media for under-16s if no EU-wide agreement is reached, and will seek Government approval to act nationally.

Economics

  • The EU Commission forecast Irish GDP to fall 1.2% in 2026 due to frontloading of pharmaceutical exports in 2025, though it expects a recovery to +3.4% in 2027. EY separately forecasts 2.7% domestic growth this year.
  • The NTMA warned Ireland's national debt could approach €250 billion by the 2030s, cautioning that the era of low borrowing costs is over and that rising debt levels carry real risk.
  • CSO data showed migrant workers accounted for 61% of Ireland's jobs growth between 2019 and 2024, representing 218,000 of 355,000 new jobs as total employment reached approximately 2.8 million.

Military / Defence Forces

  • The Defence Forces is deploying AI to monitor Russian shadow-fleet vessels — an estimated 500 ships — passing through Irish-controlled waters, and has funding for long-range AI-powered drones to detect subsea cable threats.
  • Minister Helen McEntee announced a government-to-government deal with France worth ~€600 million to purchase hundreds of armoured vehicles under France's Scorpion Programme, upgrading the Defence Forces' fleet.
  • An Irish Army Cavalry Corps crew beat six US military teams in a Bradley armoured vehicle competition — a remarkable result given Ireland does not operate tracked vehicles.
  • The 128th Infantry Battalion is completing final training ahead of deployment to the UN Interim Force in Lebanon (UNIFIL).

Technology

  • Meta announced 350 Irish job cuts — 20% of its local workforce, disproportionately higher than its global 10% target, following Oracle's 15% cut in Ireland. The ICT sector has shed over 20,000 jobs in the past year.
  • Dublin Tech Week (May 22–29) is underway with 30+ events spanning AI, cybersecurity, fintech, and smart cities, anchored by the Dublin Tech Summit.
  • Medtronic is creating 85 new jobs in Galway with a software development hub, a rare bright spot amid widespread tech-sector redundancies nationally.

Sports

  • Galway outclassed Kildare at Pearse Stadium in the opening round of the All-Ireland SFC, with Rob Finnerty particularly impressive in a dominant team display.
  • Kerry claimed the TG4 Munster SFC title for the third successive year, edging out a spirited Waterford side by one point at Kilmallock.
  • The All-Ireland series and Tailteann Cup are both underway, with Irish-language commentary on approximately 35 fixtures this summer on RTÉ Player and RTÉ News channel.

Music

  • CMAT won Best Album at the Ivor Novello Awards for her Euro-Country record — "It's nice to finally win an award in England," she quipped, also taking the moment to call out Bertie Ahern's immigration remarks.
  • RTÉ 2FM launched Sessions from Oblivion, a 10-part series featuring 20 new Irish acts recording live at Whelan's, Galway's Róisín Dubh, and Oblivion Studios.
  • New Irish albums reviewed this week include Mick Flannery's Man on a Bridge and Molly O'Mahony's Waiting on the World, along with releases from Goodtime John and Pól Mac Giolla Ghunna.

Books

  • Ali Smith won the 2026 Dublin Literary Award — worth €100,000 and the world's largest prize for a single English-language novel — for her book Gliff, in the award's 31st year.
  • The Children's Books Ireland Awards were announced, with Kevin Moran's debut The Doomsday Club taking the Eilís Dillon Award and six prizes distributed across fiction, illustration, and non-fiction at a ceremony in Merrion Square.
  • Fiona McCann's debut novel Love & Other Liabilities — set on a west Cork island — won the Joan Hessayon Award for New Writers from the Romantic Novelists' Association.

Galway

  • Fine Gael's Seán Kyne won the Galway West by-election, called after independent TD Catherine Connolly was elected President of Ireland; 17 candidates contested the seat with a turnout of approximately 44%.
  • Over 500 new homes have commenced in Galway city and county in 2026 to date, with county commencements up 60% on the same period last year.
  • Galway's footballers began their All-Ireland SFC campaign with a commanding win over Kildare at Pearse Stadium, and Medtronic announced 85 new software jobs at their Galway hub.

Kilkelly / East Mayo

  • Swinford community notes: local sports clubs including Swinford GAA and Swinford Killasser LGFA continue preparations for the summer season; Mayo Fleadh Cheoil Mhaigh Eo 2026 entries are open via the Mayo Comhaltas website.
  • No major breaking news from Kilkelly parish this week; the next scheduled community events are expected through June.

Tarpey News

  • John Tarpey received the Best Contribution to Heritage Award at the 2026 Cathaoirleach's Awards in Galway, recognised for founding the Kilskeagh Heritage Committee, which uncovered and restored a historic medieval church and graveyard that had been hidden by overgrowth for decades.
  • Tarpey & Associates (Ardrahan, Co. Galway) and Brendan Tarpey Plumbing & Heating remain active in the county; the Liam Tarpey Band has 2026 tour dates listed on Ents24.
