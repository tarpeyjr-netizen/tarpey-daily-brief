🔥 Trending in Ireland
The Grassroots Gaeilge Revival
Cultural Movement · Nationwide (origin: rural towns & Gaeltacht communities)

A grassroots revival of the Irish language is quietly spreading through towns, villages and cities — driven not by government programmes but by coffee shops offering discounts for ordering as Gaeilge, local "ciorcal comhrá" conversation meetups, and a surge in online classes. The movement draws energy from cultural flashpoints like the Oscar-nominated film An Cailín Ciúin and the so-called "Kneecap effect," with the bilingual Belfast rap group normalising Irish among younger urban audiences. Commentators note that about one in five people not currently learning Irish say they plan to start — and Irish-language classes at the Irish Arts Center in Manhattan have expanded to ten weekly sessions to meet demand. What makes this wave different is its bottom-up, social character: communities are choosing the language, not just being told to learn it.

🔥 Trending in Ireland
F3miii
Music · Dublin

F3miii (pronounced "Femme-ee") is a Nigerian-Irish singer-songwriter from Dublin whose single "Noble" has become one of the breakout Irish tracks of 2026, racking up millions of uses on TikTok and introducing his blended Afropop and R&B sound to listeners well beyond Ireland. The son of Nigerian parents raised in Dublin, F3miii writes lyrics that explore identity, belonging, and pride — themes that have resonated with a generation of young Irish people with roots across Africa, Asia, and beyond. His rise is part of a broader shift in Irish music: the 2FM Rising 2026 list and Hotpress's acts to watch both flagged him early in the year, but it was the organic spread of "Noble" on social media — rather than radio or label push — that turned him into a genuine cultural talking point.
