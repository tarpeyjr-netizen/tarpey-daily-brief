A Farewell to Arms

AuthorErnest Hemingway
StyleModernist War Novel / Lost Generation
Year1929
Pages332
Plot Synopsis

Set against the grinding catastrophe of World War I in Italy, the novel follows Frederic Henry, an American lieutenant serving as an ambulance driver for the Italian army, who falls desperately in love with Catherine Barkley, a British nurse. Their affair deepens as the Italian front collapses around them — retreat, desertion, and flight across a rain-soaked lake into Switzerland. It is a story about two people trying to carve out a private world inside a public one that keeps destroying everything tender. The ending arrives with a brutality that feels both inevitable and unjust, the way war always does.

Why You Should Read It

You've already read Hemingway's short declarative sentences in The Sun Also Rises — but this is where that style earns its keep, where the restraint becomes devastating rather than just cool. The prose is so stripped down that when emotion finally surfaces, it hits like cold water. If you're drawn to books about men trying to hold onto something real while the world falls apart around them, this is the essential text. It's short, it moves fast, and the last page will stay with you for days.
