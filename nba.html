June 25, 2026 Update

Roster Players

Jalen Brunson — New York Knicks

🏆 NBA Champion & Finals MVP. Brunson led the Knicks to their first title since 1973, closing out the San Antonio Spurs 4-1 in the NBA Finals. In the clinching Game 5 on June 13, he scored 45 points on 14-of-27 shooting (4-of-7 from three) with 13-of-16 free throws — a Knicks Finals record — earning the Bill Russell Trophy as Finals MVP. He also posted 36 points in Game 1 (June 9) and 32 points in Game 3 (June 10). His father Rick Brunson, an assistant coach for the Knicks who never won a title as a player, was part of the celebration; Jalen called sharing the moment with his dad "10 times more special."

Josh Hart — New York Knicks

🏆 NBA Champion. Hart contributed 13 points, 11 rebounds, and 2 assists in the Game 5 clincher. At the post-game trophy ceremony, Hart emphatically fired back at critics who questioned the Knicks' strategy of trading away multiple first-round picks to build the roster: "Forget those picks. Forget those picks, dawg! We're here!" He tapped teammate Mikal Bridges on the head in celebration. Hart, Brunson, and Bridges became the first trio of teammates to win both an NCAA title (2016 Villanova) and an NBA championship together.

Mikal Bridges — New York Knicks

🏆 NBA Champion. Bridges finished Game 5 with 14 points, 4 assists, and 2 rebounds on 5-of-10 shooting (3-of-7 from three). As part of the "Nova Knicks," Bridges, Brunson, and Hart made history as the first trio of college teammates to win both an NCAA and NBA title. Their Villanova bond has drawn attention as a potential blueprint for USA Basketball heading into the 2028 LA Olympics. Bridges delivered consistent two-way play throughout the Finals after his mid-season arrival elevated the Knicks' defense.

Donte DiVincenzo — Minnesota Timberwolves

DiVincenzo suffered a ruptured right Achilles tendon during the Timberwolves' playoff run — he went down in Game 4 of Minnesota's series and underwent surgery. It was a brutal end to an encouraging season in which he started all 82 regular-season games, averaging 12.2 points on 37.9% from three on 7.9 attempts per game. With one year remaining on his deal and his availability for 2026-27 uncertain, Minnesota has been gauging trade market interest in DiVincenzo alongside Julius Randle and the No. 28 overall pick in the 2026 draft, per HoopsHype's Michael Scotto.

Collin Gillespie — Phoenix Suns

Re-signing with Suns on 4-year, $48 million deal, per ESPN's Shams Charania. The contract — fully guaranteed at roughly $12M per year — is a massive milestone for Gillespie, who spent three straight seasons on two-way contracts after going undrafted out of Villanova in 2022. This is his first meaningful long-term NBA deal. He earned it with a breakout 2025-26 season: 12.7 points, 4.1 rebounds, and 4.6 assists over 80 games, including a team-leading 232 three-pointers made as Phoenix's No. 8 seed in the West. The deal is considered team-friendly given his production.

Cam Whitmore — Washington Wizards

Whitmore was traded from the Houston Rockets to the Washington Wizards earlier this season in exchange for a 2026 Chicago Bulls second-round pick and a 2029 Sacramento Kings second-round pick, per Shams Charania. The 21-year-old (he turned 21 around the trade) has averaged 10.8 points on 44.9% shooting in 17.4 minutes per game across his two-year career. The Wizards took the bet on increased minutes and a homecoming to the DMV area. No major news in the past 15 days specifically, but Whitmore heads into the offseason on a rebuilding Washington roster with opportunity to take a larger role.

Saddiq Bey — New Orleans Pelicans

Bey wrapped up a career-best season with the Pelicans and is a prime extension candidate this offseason. He averaged 17.7 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 2.5 assists on 45.1/36.7/84.1 shooting splits — including five 30-point games and a season-high 42 — all on a sub-$7M salary, making him arguably the best value contract in the NBA. New Orleans is expected to move quickly on a long-term extension with Bey, who has one year remaining at roughly $6.6M. No specific deal announcement yet as of June 25.

Jermaine Samuels — Houston Rockets

Samuels is with the Rockets on a two-way deal and had an outstanding G League season with the Rio Grande Valley Vipers, earning an All-Star nod after averaging a career-best 21.1 points on 49.7% shooting, 8.7 rebounds, 3.9 assists, and 1.3 steals. He was recalled to Houston's NBA roster and appeared in three games, averaging 6.7 minutes. Now in the offseason, Samuels is a candidate for a standard contract upgrade if the Rockets choose to retain him. No formal contract news has emerged in the past 15 days.

Jeremiah Robinson-Earl — Texas Legends (G League)

The G League season concluded months ago. Robinson-Earl was with the Texas Legends this season and earned a 10-day contract with the Dallas Mavericks (hardship exception) in January 2026 — his 47th-career NBA call-up for the Legends' franchise. In his Texas Legends stint he averaged 19.3 points, 11.7 rebounds, and 2.6 assists on .522/.347/.944 splits. He is now a free agent heading into the summer. No June 2026 signing news has emerged yet; Robinson-Earl, a former 32nd overall pick (2021), will look to land an NBA deal or two-way contract before training camp.

Eric Dixon — Memphis Hustle (G League)

The G League season is over, and Dixon heads into the offseason hunting an NBA opportunity. After being acquired from the Greensboro Swarm by the Memphis Hustle in February 2026, he had a standout stretch with Memphis — his 48-point game on March 15 is tied for the second-highest single-game total in Hustle franchise history. Dixon entered the league as the 2024-25 NCAA scoring leader at Villanova (23.3 ppg) and went undrafted in 2025. No NBA signing or workout news has been reported in the past 15 days, but with the NBA Draft approaching and Summer League on the horizon, his productivity should attract attention.
