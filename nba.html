Roster Players

Jalen Brunson — New York Knicks

Brunson is the engine of the Knicks' first Finals run since 1994. He scored 30 points in Game 1, including 13 in the fourth quarter, and has been the unquestioned leader of New York's campaign. The Villanova trio of Brunson, Hart, and Bridges could become just the fifth set of teammates to win both an NCAA championship and an NBA title together. Game 5 is tonight in San Antonio.

Josh Hart — New York Knicks

Hart's all-around play has been a defining factor in the Finals — he contributed 15 rebounds, 6 assists, and 4 steals in Game 1. Hart humorously revealed at Finals media day that he initially couldn't stand Brunson or Bridges in college, adding a fun storyline to the Villanova reunion. He's been a relentless energy piece for New York throughout the run.

Mikal Bridges — New York Knicks

Bridges is in the NBA Finals with his two Villanova teammates. While his Game 1 stat line (9 pts) was modest, he provides two-way value that the Knicks rely on in crunch time. The narrative around the Villanova trio potentially winning a ring together has dominated Finals coverage.

Donte DiVincenzo — Minnesota Timberwolves

Tough situation for DiVincenzo: he tore his Achilles in Game 4 of the Timberwolves' playoff series against Denver on April 25, ending his season. He had been averaging 12.2 pts/4.1 reb/3.8 ast in his best year as a Wolf. Recovery is expected to take 8–12 months, likely meaning he misses most of 2026–27. With the final year of his $46.8M deal worth ~$12.5M, Minnesota must decide whether to retain an injured vet or pivot to younger options like Ayo Dosunmu. ESPN's Tim Bontemps has noted the Wolves may part ways.

Collin Gillespie — Phoenix Suns

Gillespie had an outstanding 2025–26 season, averaging 12.7 points, 4.6 assists, and 4.1 rebounds across 80 games, while shooting 40.1% from three and breaking the Suns' franchise record for three-pointers in a season. He enters unrestricted free agency June 30, but has stated he has no desire to leave Phoenix. The Suns hold Early Bird rights and can offer up to four years starting at ~$14.9M annually. ESPN listed him as a top "free agent to watch."

Cam Whitmore — Washington Wizards

Whitmore's first season in Washington was derailed by upper-extremity deep vein thrombosis. After appearing in just 21 games and averaging 9.2 points, he was shut down for the year. The Wizards applied for a Disabled Player Exception and will not extend him this offseason, though he remains under contract at $5.5M for next season. Full recovery is expected ahead of 2026–27.

Saddiq Bey — New Orleans Pelicans

Bey put together the best season of his career in New Orleans — 17.7 ppg, 5.6 reb, 2.5 ast with efficient shooting splits (45.1/36.7/84.1). The Pelicans are making extension talks a top offseason priority. Bey has been vocal about wanting to stay long term: "I love it here." A four-year deal in the $18–20M per year range is the reported framework. He has one year remaining on his current deal before becoming a UFA in 2027.

Jermaine Samuels — Houston Rockets (roster note: status unclear)

Samuels is no longer with the Rockets — Houston declined his qualifying offer in June 2024, making him an unrestricted free agent. He has since been playing professionally in Spain (San Pablo Burgos). No 2026 NBA activity has been reported. The roster entry appears to be outdated; Samuels does not currently have an NBA contract.

Jeremiah Robinson-Earl — Texas Legends (G-League)

Robinson-Earl had a strong G-League season with the Texas Legends, averaging 18.7 points, 12.2 rebounds, and 2.8 assists in 35.6 minutes while shooting 55% from the field. He earned NBA call-ups with both the Indiana Pacers and Dallas Mavericks during the season. He was also named to the USA Men's FIBA World Cup Qualifying Team in February 2026. No new NBA signing has been reported in the past 15 days, but his NBA-level production keeps him on team radars heading into the offseason.

Eric Dixon — Memphis Hustle (G-League)

The Villanova all-time leading scorer went undrafted in 2025 and signed a two-way deal with the Lakers, who waived him due to a foot injury. He then signed with the Charlotte Hornets (October 2025) but was waived and joined the Greensboro Swarm. In February 2026 he was traded to the Memphis Hustle. No recent news in the past 15 days; Dixon remains a prospect pursuing a path to the NBA from the G-League. His scoring ability gives him a chance, but health and roster opportunities remain the key variables.
