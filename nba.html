June 2026 Offseason Update

Offseason Snapshot

The biggest story on the roster this month: Jalen Brunson, Josh Hart, and Mikal Bridges are leading the New York Knicks through the 2026 NBA Finals against the San Antonio Spurs — the Villanova trio on the cusp of completing one of the most storied runs in franchise history, and potentially becoming the first trio of teammates to win both an NCAA and NBA championship together.

Players

Jalen Brunson — New York Knicks

Contract: 4-year, $156.5M extension (signed 2024 offseason, begins 2025-26), includes a 4th-year player option — notably $113M less than max to help Knicks avoid second apron. Trade Rumors: None; Brunson is the franchise cornerstone. Playoffs/Offseason: Brunson is quarterbacking the Knicks through the 2026 NBA Finals vs. San Antonio (NY leads 1-0 after Game 1). Acknowledged an "unacceptable" flaw heading into Game 3 but the team remains in control. News: Appeared on Good Morning America alongside father Rick Brunson discussing the historic Finals run.

Josh Hart — New York Knicks

Contract: Signed through 2027-28 on a 4-year, $81M extension ($94M total) — no contract uncertainty. Trade Rumors: None. Playoffs/Offseason: Critical contributor in the Knicks' postseason run; posted a playoff career-high 26 points in Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals vs. Cleveland. Currently active in the NBA Finals. News: The Trail Blazers are being called one of the biggest losers of the original Hart trade as the Knicks make their Finals run.

Mikal Bridges — New York Knicks

Contract: Signed a 4-year, $150M extension in the 2025 offseason (slightly below the $156.2M max available; includes 5.69% trade bonus). Trade Rumors: Speculative Giannis package talk has quieted with the Knicks in the Finals. Playoffs/Offseason: Playing in the 2026 NBA Finals — the Knicks' run has featured an 11-game win streak (third-longest in NBA history) and a 19.4-point average margin of victory. News: The "Nova Knicks" — Brunson, Hart, and Bridges as potential co-NCAA and NBA champions — has become a national story.

Donte DiVincenzo — Minnesota Timberwolves

Contract: Final year of a 4-year, $46.8M deal (~$12.5M cap hit in 2026-27); unrestricted free agent summer 2027. Injury: Torn Achilles in Game 4 of the first-round playoffs — out for the remainder of 2025-26 and likely much of 2026-27. Organization believes he could return at some point next season. Trade Rumors: Minnesota may need to move DiVincenzo to sign Ayo Dosunmu and avoid the second apron, but the front office is reluctant and nobody wants to trade him. News: Wolves eliminated from playoffs; DiVincenzo's health is the central offseason variable.

Collin Gillespie — Phoenix Suns

Contract: Entering unrestricted free agency; eligible for 4-year, $67M deal via Early Bird rights with Phoenix. Offseason Activity: Breakout season — 12.7 ppg, 4.6 ast, 4.1 reb, 1.2 stl across 80 games (58 starts), 40.1% from three. Rated by scouts as one of the top UFA point guards in the 2026 class. Trade Rumors: None; he is Phoenix's top re-signing priority. News: Gillespie has publicly stated he wants to stay in Phoenix; Suns analysts widely agree the team must prioritize locking him up this summer.

Cam Whitmore — Washington Wizards

Contract: On a 4-year rookie deal (~$15.6M total, ~$3.9M/year); RFA 2027. Not expected to receive a rookie scale extension after missing most of 2025-26 with a venous (vascular) condition. Trade Rumors: Hypothetical trade scenarios have circulated; analysts suggest the Wizards may explore moving him given forward depth and his health history. Offseason Activity: Continuing rehab/recovery. News: Originally traded from Houston to Washington for two second-round picks; Wizards regret reportedly growing as his situation stalls.

Saddiq Bey — New Orleans Pelicans

Contract: One year remaining at ~$6.6M; eligible for a 4-year, $93M extension this offseason. Offseason Activity: Career year — 17.7 ppg, 5.6 reb, 2.5 ast on 45.1/36.7/84.1 splits across 72 games (64 starts). Emerged as the crown jewel of the CJ McCollum trade. Trade Rumors: None; extension is the priority conversation. News: Bey has stated clearly he wants to stay in New Orleans long-term. GM Joe Dumars has sent positive signals. Most analysts view getting an extension done as the Pelicans' most important offseason task.

Jermaine Samuels — Houston Rockets

Contract: Re-signed by Houston on an Exhibit 10 deal; candidate for two-way conversion. Offseason Activity: Appeared in 14 NBA games (1.4 ppg) last season while doing heavy lifting in the G League for Rio Grande Valley Vipers (19.6 ppg, 7.8 rpg in 38 games). Trade Rumors: None. News: Remains a developmental piece in the Rockets' pipeline — G League production keeps him in contention for roster consideration heading into 2026-27.

Jeremiah Robinson-Earl — Texas Legends (G League)

Contract/Status: G League free agent after a busy 2025-26 campaign: signed with Dallas Mavericks (10-day, January 2026), earlier had stints with Indiana Pacers before returning to the Texas Legends. Offseason Activity: Strong G League season — 18.7 ppg, 12.2 rpg, 2.8 apg on 55% shooting. Named to the USA Basketball February 2026 Men's World Cup Qualifying Team (first senior national team call-up). NBA Interest: Mavericks 10-day and national team selection indicate ongoing interest. Looking for a two-way or roster spot for 2026-27. News: International options also on the table.

Eric Dixon — Memphis Hustle (G League)

Contract/Status: Undrafted in the 2025 NBA Draft; in the G League. Traded from Greensboro Swarm to Memphis Hustle (February 10, 2026). No current NBA team affiliation. Offseason Activity: Completing his first professional season in the G League after averaging 23.3 ppg, 5.1 rpg in his final Villanova season. NBA Interest: No specific call-up news yet; summer league would be the logical next step. News: Building his professional resume after going undrafted; Villanova track record gives him a long runway.
