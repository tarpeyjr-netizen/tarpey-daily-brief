  • New album Lordy drops July 10, 2026 via Dualtone Records — their first in five years. Produced by Brad Cook (Bon Iver, Mavis Staples) with guest spots from MJ Lenderman, Iron & Wine's Sam Beam, and Phil Cook. Lead singles "Tiger Blood" and the title track are out now. (Jambase, June 2026)

Noah Kahan

Zach Bryan

  • Sixth studio album With Heaven on Top (January 9, 2026) spans 25 tracks and debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 with 134,000 album-equivalent units. Singles include "Plastic Cigarette" and "Say Why." (Rolling Stone, Jan 2026)

Sturgill Simpson

  • Released Mutiny After Midnight as Johnny Blue Skies & the Dark Clouds — a groove-heavy dance record available only on vinyl, CD, and cassette (no streaming). Debuted at #3 on the Billboard 200 without digital availability. (Variety, 2026)

Kneecap

  • Second album Fenian released May 1, 2026 via Heavenly Recordings, produced by Dan Carey. 14 tracks featuring Kae Tempest, Lankum's Radie Peat, and Palestinian rapper Fawzi. Songs cover Irish history, solidarity, fame, and grief. (NPR, May 2026)

Ryan Bingham

  • New album They Call Us The Lucky Ones (with The Texas Gentlemen) released May 15, 2026 via Thirty Tigers — his first full-length in seven years. 10 tracks recorded mostly live, featuring fiddle, organ, and pedal steel. (MusicRow, Feb 2026)

Drayton Farley

  • Third album A Heavy Duty Heart released March 27, 2026 via One Riot Records, produced by Sadler Vaden (Jason Isbell's 400 Unit). 10 tracks recorded live to tape in Nashville — a hopeful turn after years of darker material. (Holler, Jan 2026)
