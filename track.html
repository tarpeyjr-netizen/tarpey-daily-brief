Week ending June 28, 2026

This Week

June 22–28, 2026. The week's signature event was the Paris Diamond League (Stade Charléty, June 28), which delivered a raft of Diamond League records. Standout performance: Trayvon Bromell surging from lane eight to clock 9.91s (SB) and hand Noah Lyles his first defeat of the 2026 season. Also notable: Australian Cameron Myers ran 3:28.00 in the 1500m — an area record and world lead — in the absence of the usual Ingebrigtsen/Kerr/Hocker/Nuguse quartet. Grant Fisher delivered a late kick to win the 5000m (12:54.80) for his first Diamond League victory, and Femke Bol set a personal best of 1:55.60 in the 800m behind Audrey Werro's DL-record 1:53.80.

Athletes

Trayvon Bromell — USA — 100m

This week: 1st, Men's 100m, Paris Diamond League — 9.91s SB (+0.1m/s). Bromell ran from lane eight and outkicked Olympic champion Noah Lyles (9.92, 2nd) and Lamont Marcell Jacobs (9.96 SB, 3rd). First Diamond League win of the 2026 season; Lyles's first defeat of the year. News: Continuing his comeback as a legitimate title threat ahead of the USATF Outdoor Championships (July 23–26).
