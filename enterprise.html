CEO Christian Klein unveiled the Autonomous Enterprise vision backed by shipping product: 50+ Joule Assistants, 200+ specialized agents, a €100M partner fund, and agent-led migration tooling claiming 35%+ effort reduction. SAP also completed the acquisition of Reltio (master data management) and agreed to acquire Dremio (open data lakehouse). The May S/4HANA compatibility pack cutoff closes the final on-ramp for migration holdouts. (SAP News)

Joule

The biggest Sapphire announcement for practitioners: Joule Studio — a fully managed, low-code environment for building and managing AI agents, applications, and workflows across the full lifecycle. Free design-time access is available to all SAP customers under fair-use limits through end of 2026. Joule Work launches as the new central hub (desktop, web, and mobile GA now), replacing scattered entry points. SAP invested ~$60M in n8n at a $5.2B valuation and will embed n8n's visual workflow canvas inside Joule Studio by Q3 2026. Q1 2026 release improvements include faster startup times, cross-thread search across all conversation history, and document ingestion scaling to 8,000 docs per pipeline. Joule is also being extended to on-premises customers for the first time. (SAP News, ERP Today)

WalkMe

The Q2 '26 WalkMe release (available May 18) is the most substantive update since the SAP acquisition. Smart Highlights surface live data from Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, and SAP SuccessFactors directly inside whichever app the user is already in — no context switching. AI Chat delivers context-aware responses based on what's visible on screen. The release also adds Learning Journeys for multi-course sequencing, audio narration, SCORM export, and bulk system configuration for global rollouts across 38 languages. Integration with SAP Ariba and SAP Fieldglass now includes step-by-step guided workflows for SOW creation and timesheet approval. (WalkMe Blog)

Signavio

A landmark release landed May 26: SAP Signavio unified Process Insights and Process Intelligence into a single product under the name SAP Signavio Process Intelligence, with a redesigned navigation, new personalized homepage (My Page), and SAP WalkMe embedded directly in the header. The Process Modeler was rebuilt with an AI-first architecture and a new Process Transformation Assistant lets business users run sophisticated process analysis via natural language — identifying high-impact opportunities for agent deployment without needing a process engineer. SAP Signavio was also named a Leader in the newly-created Gartner Magic Quadrant for Process Intelligence Platforms. Embedded guided tours are now free for all users across all license types. (SAP News, SAP Community)

BTP (Business Technology Platform)

Sapphire reframed BTP inside the new SAP Business AI Platform — a three-layer architecture consolidating BTP, SAP Business Data Cloud, and SAP Business AI. The layers: a context layer unifying SAP and non-SAP data with SAP's process knowledge; a build layer centered on Joule Studio 2.0 (rolling out June 2026); and a governance layer via SAP AI Agent Hub (GA Q3 2026) for tracking, managing, and monitoring all agents across the enterprise. SAP positioned this explicitly as an architecture story for buyers who have moved past "what can AI do?" to "how do we control it?" BTP ABAP Environment maintenance and major upgrade windows for 2026 are published on the SAP Community for planning purposes. (ERP Today, SAPinsider)

IBP (Integrated Business Planning)

The May 2026 (2605) IBP release continues the platform's shift away from Excel-centric planning. Planner Workspaces has matured into a fully capable planning cockpit — no longer a secondary option. Joule is now embedded in IBP for natural-language guidance and explanation within planning sessions. The harmonized data model introduced earlier in 2026 supports both time-series and order-based planning in a unified interface. Releases 2508, 2511, and 2602 laid the UX groundwork that 2605 builds on. (SAP Community, Flexso)

BRIM (Billing & Revenue Innovation Management)

No major product announcements from SAP this week specific to BRIM, but the surrounding context matters: the shift to usage-based and subscription pricing models is accelerating across telco, utilities, and digital services — exactly BRIM's target market. With SAP's Autonomous Enterprise push embedding agents into finance processes, watch for Joule-assisted billing exception handling and revenue recognition automation to appear in BRIM roadmap items later in 2026. Partners and practitioners should monitor the SAP Community BRIM topic page for Q3 roadmap disclosures. The SAPinsider BRIM track remains the best practitioner-level resource for implementation patterns. (SAPinsider)

Cloud ALM

The SAP Community Call for ALM (May 20) and the partner-focused session (May 27) covered the latest Cloud ALM and SAP Focused Run updates. Key developments: SAP for Me with Joule is rolling out in phases starting May 2026 at no extra cost to customers — Joule now assists inside the support portal. New AI observability features are coming to Cloud ALM in Q3 2026 GA, letting teams trace tool calls and system interactions at each point in agent execution — critical for customers running Joule agents in production. The monthly Community Call series continues as the fastest way to stay current; sessions are recorded. (SAP Community, Q2/2026 What's New)

Salesforce

Salesforce posted record Q1 FY27 results: $11.1B revenue, up 13% YoY, with GAAP EPS of $2.42 (+52%). The company launched Agentforce Operations to automate back-office workflows, and released the Data 360 MCP Server in Developer Preview — giving external agents structured access to Salesforce data via the Model Context Protocol. (Salesforce Newsroom)

ServiceNow

Knowledge 2026 (Las Vegas, May 5–7, 25,000 attendees) delivered two headline announcements: Autonomous Security and Risk, built on the Armis and Veza integrations, and Action Fabric + MCP Server now generally available — opening ServiceNow's system-of-action to external agents built on Claude, Copilot, or custom stacks. ServiceNow also completed a $4B multi-tranche debt offering to fund its expansion. (ERP Today)

Workday

Workday introduced Adaptive Decision Intelligence in Workday Adaptive Planning — letting finance teams ask natural-language questions, model scenarios in minutes, and commit decisions directly into the governed plan. Named a Leader in the 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for both Talent Acquisition Suites and Higher Education SIS. A partnership with Anthropic and LISC launched an AI Solopreneurship Accelerator with $150K in seed funding and free Claude credits. (StockTitan)

Microsoft 365

Microsoft 365 E7 (Frontier Suite) is now generally available, bundling M365 E5, Copilot, and Agent 365 into a single SKU. SharePoint got a Copilot-powered page creation workflow and AI Citations Analytics. Teams now auto-translates meeting captions live. Windows 365 Business prices dropped 20% effective May 1 — but list price increases on M365 commercial suites arrive July 1 for new and renewing customers. (Microsoft 365 Blog)

This is OpenAI's direct move into enterprise implementation services, competing head-on with Accenture and Deloitte. (OpenAI)

  • Hype vs. reality: Every major vendor invoked "agentic AI" this week. The honest read: MCP interoperability is the actual architectural shift — the ability for external agents to call into ServiceNow, Salesforce, and SAP via standardized protocols is real and meaningful. The "autonomous enterprise" framing, however, remains aspirational for the median customer still running ECC with custom Z-transactions.

✅ Implementation Wins & Losses

  • Win — Madrid City Council + SAP: Madrid's internal and tax management modernization on SAP went live, announced May 21. A large public-sector reference worth watching for other government ERP buyers. (SAP News)
  • Win — Martur Fompak International + SAP Joule: The automotive supplier went live with intelligent robotics enabled by SAP Joule and Embodied AI, announcing measurable throughput gains (May 20). One of the first real-world Joule production go-lives on record. (SAP News)
  • Loss — Tennant Company (ongoing): The industrial cleaning equipment maker's November 2025 ERP cutover is still costing them in 2026. Unplanned remediation costs this year alone are projected to exceed $20M (vs. a $5M budget), with cumulative spend since 2023 now ~$98M. Gross margin collapsed from 41.3% to 34.6% in a single quarter; stock fell 23.4% in one session. The system crippled order entry, shipping, and customer service for months. A canonical cutover risk cautionary tale. (Elevatiq)
  • Loss — Birmingham City Council + Oracle: A 2022 decision to replace legacy SAP ERP with Oracle has ballooned from an estimated £39M to ~£90M ($123M), with critical functionality still not ready. The pattern: inadequate project oversight plus shifting design requests. (CIO)

⚠️ Threats & Risks

  • Instructure Canvas ransomware breach: ShinyHunters compromised 3.65 TB of data belonging to ~275 million people across nearly 9,000 schools. Canvas Data 2 and Canvas Beta entered maintenance mode. EdTech platforms tied to enterprise SSO are firmly in scope for enterprise risk teams. (Rescana)
  • GitHub internal repositories breached: ~3,800 internal GitHub repos were accessed after an employee installed a malicious VS Code extension. Poisoned developer tooling is now a primary enterprise attack vector — a direct concern for organizations using GitHub Actions or Copilot-adjacent pipelines. (eSecurity Planet)
  • Trellix source code exposed: Attackers accessed source code powering Trellix's security tools, potentially enabling targeted exploitation of weaknesses in widely-deployed enterprise security software. (CISO Platform)
  • AI vendor lock-in compounding: 68% of tech leaders plan to cut vendor count by ~20% in 2026 — but agentic AI is making lock-in structurally worse. Enterprises running agents on proprietary orchestration layers are locked in at every layer of the stack simultaneously. The EU's Right to Repair Directive (July 2026) and the CMA's cloud market investigation are the first regulatory counterpressure. (Kai Waehner)
