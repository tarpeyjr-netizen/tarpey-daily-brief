  • ServiceNow instance vulnerability exploited: A flaw that allowed unauthenticated users to query customer instance tables was actively exploited before ServiceNow patched hosted instances on June 5, 2026. If you run on-premise or private-hosted ServiceNow, verify your patch status immediately.
  • Palo Alto Networks CVE-2026-0257 (CVSS 7.8): Active exploitation of a PAN-OS authentication bypass targeting GlobalProtect portals was observed beginning May 17, 2026. Enterprise perimeter devices continue to be primary initial-access vectors for threat actors — patch or segment now.
  • AI vendor lock-in accelerating: Analysis from Kai Waehner and others flags that agentic AI compounds lock-in at every layer: model, orchestration, data, and runtime. 68% of tech leaders plan vendor consolidation in 2026, yet the proprietary agent frameworks from SAP, Salesforce, ServiceNow, and Microsoft make true portability difficult. Open models (DeepSeek V3.1, Qwen3) offer an emerging exit ramp with inferencing costs up to 90% lower than frontier models.
  • Enterprise M&A wave intensifying: SaaS consolidation M&A deal volume is up 30–40% YoY as AI disruption forces mid-market vendors to scale or be acquired. Private equity has ~$1 trillion in US dry powder earmarked. Customers of smaller enterprise software vendors should begin evaluating contract exit provisions and data portability rights now.
  • Supply chain software attacks rising: Malware packages outnumber published CVEs 4:1 in 2026 (192,742 malicious packages vs. 48,175 CVEs in 2025), meaning traditional patch management alone is insufficient. Enterprise teams running Node, Python, or open-source dependencies inside integration pipelines (BTP, MuleSoft, Workato) are particularly exposed.
