Raleigh's Grand Slam Sends Mariners Back Atop the AL West

Seattle Mariners 8, Cincinnati Reds 0 — Final

Cal Raleigh turned a taut pitchers' duel into a laugher with one swing Monday night, blasting a first-pitch grand slam in the seventh inning as the Seattle Mariners blanked the Cincinnati Reds 8-0 at T-Mobile Park. Paired with Texas's loss to the White Sox earlier in the evening, the win vaulted Seattle into sole possession of first place in the American League West.

The Game

For six innings, this had the look of a tight, low-scoring grind. George Kirby, making his first start in 12 days, needed just 80 pitches to breeze through six shutout frames, scattering three hits and striking out six against a single walk. Reds starter Andrew Abbott matched him blow for blow, working around trouble of his own and holding Seattle off the board until the fifth — even as his defense quietly undid him behind the scenes.

Seattle's only run through six summed up Cincinnati's night. Victor Robles led off the fifth with a single, stole second on a throw that skipped past Edwin Arroyo, and raced home on an infield chopper by Julio Rodríguez that shortstop Elly De La Cruz double-clutched. The Mariners led just 1-0 — and lost Rodríguez in the process, as the center fielder pulled up holding his hamstring after beating out the infield hit and was promptly removed from the game.

The floodgates opened in the bottom of the seventh. Reliever Pierce Johnson allowed two singles before Colt Emerson, inserted into the lineup barely 90 minutes before first pitch on his 21st birthday, lifted a sacrifice fly to make it 2-0. Another single set up Weston Wilson, Rodríguez's injury replacement, who doubled home a run to push the lead to 3-0 and chase Johnson from the game. Reds manager Terry Francona then summoned lefty Caleb Ferguson, who intentionally walked Dominic Canzone to load the bases and pitch to Raleigh instead.

It backfired immediately. Raleigh turned on Ferguson's first offering, a hanging breaking ball, and sent it 408 feet into the left-field bleachers — his sixth career grand slam and 10th home run of the season — blowing the game open at 7-0 in a single swing. An unearned run off Chase Petty in the eighth, on a botched potential double-play ball that squirted loose, rounded out the scoring.

The win was Seattle's third straight and improved the Mariners to 51-50, a half-game ahead of Texas (50-50), which fell to the White Sox earlier Monday. It's a modest margin, but a meaningful one for a club that spent much of the summer chasing the Rangers — and one earned even as Seattle's leadoff man and center fielder walked off nursing a hamstring injury.

Diamondbacks Erase Seven-Run Deficit, Walk Off Cardinals in the 10th

Arizona Diamondbacks 8, St. Louis Cardinals 7 — Final/10

Down 7-0 in the sixth inning, the Arizona Diamondbacks clawed all the way back and won it when Max Kepler's double in the 10th scored automatic runner Jorge Barrosa, an 8-7 victory over the St. Louis Cardinals on Sunday at Chase Field. The rally matched the largest comeback in franchise history.

The Game

St. Louis built its cushion early and often. Blaze Jordan's RBI triple ignited a five-run third inning against Arizona starter Eduardo Rodriguez, who was rocked for seven hits and five earned runs and lasted just 2 2/3 innings. Jordan added a two-run single off reliever Philip Abner in the fifth to push the margin to 7-0, and Cardinals starter Andre Pallante carried a shutout into the sixth.

Arizona began chipping away in the sixth. Ketel Marte's single, a bases-loaded walk to Gabriel Moreno and another to Tyler Locklear trimmed the deficit to 7-3. Geraldo Perdomo cut it to two in the seventh with his first career inside-the-park home run, the ball skipping past Cardinals left fielder Jose Fermin.

The Diamondbacks completed the tie in the eighth. After Locklear singled with two outs off Gordon Graceffo, Nolan Arenado — notching career hit No. 1,999 in the process — drove a two-run homer to knot the score at 7-7 and send the game to extra innings.

Neither bullpen budged through the ninth. With Barrosa placed at second to open the 10th as the automatic runner, Kepler lined a double into the right-field corner to send him home and set off a celebration at Chase Field. Juan Morillo worked the final four outs for the win; Luis Gastelum took the loss after allowing the unearned, decisive run.

The victory matched the largest comeback in Diamondbacks history, tying the mark set in a 13-7 win over Philadelphia on August 29, 2022, and moved Arizona to 51-48 on the season.
